## Overview
Warm-paper editorial minimalism driven by an extremely heavy grotesque. Everything sits on a cream/oat canvas (#F5F3EE) with near-black ink, punctuated by one saturated sky-blue CTA color and occasional pastel bands (peach, mint, lavender). Full-bleed near-black (#121212) sections alternate with the cream ones to create strong page rhythm. Note: despite the "EL" metadata tag, the screenshots show no serif anywhere — this is a bold-grotesque system.

## Layout
Sticky top nav: wordmark left, center nav links grouped inside a rounded light pill, Log in + blue "Start free trial" button right. Heroes are quiet: left-aligned or centered black headline on cream, short subhead, single blue CTA, paired with either a cut-out photo over an organic peach blob (artists) or a product-dashboard card (features). Sections alternate cream / white / black full-bleed bands; long pages are stacked two-column blocks (text one side, tilted product-UI screenshot collages the other), swapping sides each section. Case-study blocks on home use a repeating template: photo + stat cards ("What's in X's Kit", subscriber count, revenue-breakdown donut) followed by a full-width pastel quote band. Blog is a clean 3-column card grid on cream with a featured post card up top. Pricing is a 3-card row with a subscriber-count slider above and an accordion FAQ below. Whitespace is generous; grids are strict and card-based.

## Components
Buttons: small-radius (~4-6px) rectangles; primary = flat sky blue #3BA5F8 with dark/black text, secondary = white or light-gray fill with black text, dark sections use white-on-black ghost or blue fill; no shadows, no gradients. Cards: white fill, 8-14px radius, hairline border or very soft shadow on cream background; pricing cards get a tiny green "Recommended" tag. Blog cards: image top, black title, small italic category label. Inputs/controls: pill toggle (Pay monthly / Pay yearly), blue slider thumb with value tooltip, rounded dropdown ("Categories"), chevron accordion rows as individual white rounded bars. Quote blocks: oversized colored quotation-mark glyph + large bold sans pull-quote, often on a full-width pastel band. Checklists use small blue circular check icons.

## Signature
- Ultra-heavy, tightly-spaced grotesque headlines ("Ready. Set. Send.", "Easy. Efficient. Effective.") — short punchy fragments, often period-separated.
- Cream/oat canvas alternating with full-bleed near-black bands; a single electric sky blue reserved for CTAs and brand moments.
- Full-width pastel testimonial bands (peach, mint, lavender) with giant quote glyphs between product sections.
- Tilted, overlapping product-UI screenshot collages and cut-out creator photos over organic blob shapes.

## Do / Don't
**Do**
- Keep one blue (#3BA5F8) exclusively for primary CTAs and brand accents; everything else stays cream/black/white.
- Alternate cream and near-black full-bleed sections to pace long pages; drop a pastel quote band roughly every two product sections.
- Set headlines in the heaviest grotesque weight with tight tracking; keep body copy small, gray-black, and airy.
- Use white cards with subtle borders/shadows and 8-14px radius on the cream background for all grid content.
- Show real creator photography (cut-outs, case-study portraits) next to concrete stats (subscriber counts, revenue donuts).

**Don't**
- Don't introduce serifs, script faces, or thin display weights — the system lives on one heavy grotesque.
- Don't use gradients, glassmorphism, or heavy drop shadows; every surface is flat.
- Don't make buttons pill-shaped or large-radius — only the nav container and toggles are pills; buttons stay near-rectangular.
- Don't put colored text in body copy; color arrives only as flat bands, blob shapes, or the single highlighted headline word (e.g. orange "purpose.").
- Don't crowd sections — each block is one idea with generous margins and a strict left-aligned or centered grid.
