## Overview
Editorial minimalism on a pure white canvas: a bookish serif carries every headline (often with an italic word for emphasis) while a quiet gray sans handles body copy. Color arrives only in soft-tinted rounded panels — warm gray, parchment cream, pale sage, blush — and in hand-drawn botanical line illustrations with small orange/purple gradient blobs. Buttons and emphasis moments are near-black, giving a calm two-tone print feel more than typical SaaS gloss.

## Layout
Split nav: left links (Download, Pricing, Help center, Company), centered logo, right utilities plus a solid black "Get started" pill-ish button. Hero is centered serif headline + short gray subhead + product screenshot; sections alternate between full-bleed white and large rounded tinted panels (16-24px radius) that act as section containers. Two-column card grids dominate (feature pairs, quote/text pairs, blog index); pricing uses a light card vs. dark inverted card side by side. Generous vertical whitespace between sections; centered closing CTA block ("It's free to get started…") repeats on every page above a 4-column footer.

## Components
- Buttons: solid near-black (#1a1a1a) fill, white text, ~8-12px radius, no border or shadow; secondary is same shape on white/dark contexts. App Store / Google Play badges used as literal buttons on the download page.
- Cards: large-radius (16-24px) flat panels in soft tints (#f6f5f2 gray, cream, sage, blush, lavender); no borders, no shadows. One inverted charcoal card with cream serif quote text used as a rhythm break.
- Pricing table: hairline row dividers, checkmarks, paid column highlighted with a pale mint fill.
- FAQ: full-width light-gray rounded accordion rows with a thin "+" toggle.
- Product screenshots shown flat inside tinted panels or in device frames (MacBook, iPhone), never floating with heavy shadows.

## Signature
- Book-serif display type with selective italics ("Make sense *common*", "Analysis made for *everyone*") against a gray grotesque body.
- Large rounded pastel panels as section backgrounds instead of full-width color bands.
- Occasional full-dark inverted card/section with cream serif text as contrast beats.
- Hand-drawn botanical/doodle line illustrations with small warm gradient accents (orange, purple, blue dots).

## Do / Don't
**Do**
- Set all headlines in a light book serif and italicize one key word for emphasis.
- Keep body copy mid-gray (#6f6f6f-ish) on white; reserve near-black for headings and buttons.
- Wrap sections and feature pairs in large-radius flat tinted panels (warm gray, cream, sage) with no borders or shadows.
- Use one dark charcoal card or band per page as a contrast moment, with cream/off-white serif text inside.
- Keep CTAs as small solid black buttons; end every page with the same centered "get started" serif CTA block.

**Don't**
- Don't use saturated brand blues/purples for buttons or links — color here lives only in pastel panel fills and illustration accents.
- Don't add drop shadows, gradients on UI chrome, or bordered cards; everything is flat fills on white.
- Don't set headlines in a bold geometric sans — the serif/sans split is the identity.
- Don't crowd sections; each idea gets a full-width panel or a two-column pair with generous air.
- Don't render screenshots with glossy mockup effects; keep them flat inside tinted containers or simple device frames.
