## Overview
An ultra-clean white canvas that acts as a gallery for the product itself: floating pastel widget cards drift around bold black headlines. Nearly all color comes from product screenshots and tinted card fills, not from chrome. The single brand hue is a soft periwinkle/indigo used for the CTA and the hero browser frame. Everything else is white, near-black text, and quiet gray captions.

## Layout
No traditional navbar — just a centered logo mark and wordmark above the hero headline; footer carries the text links (Log in, About us, Explore). Strictly center-aligned single column: logo → two-line headline → one-line subhead → solitary CTA button → laurel "Product of the day" badge → oversized product mockup in a periwinkle browser frame. Section rhythm is generous: huge vertical whitespace between blocks, each section again centered (avatar row of makers, a fading stacked headline "Your Videos. Podcasts. Newsletters..." that dissolves into the background, a masonry collage of widget cards, then the "bento.me/username" ticker section). Decorative widget cards are scattered asymmetrically at the page edges with slight rotation, breaking the otherwise rigid symmetry.

## Components
Primary button: periwinkle (#7E8BF5) fill, white small-size label, ~10px corner radius, no border, flat (no visible shadow); repeated identically at top and bottom ("Skip the Waitlist for 48 Hours"). Widget cards: white or pastel-tinted fills (lemon, blush, sky, mint), 8-14px radius, hairline or no border, very soft diffuse drop shadows, slight random rotation on the floating ones; each contains a mini CTA chip in its service color (Twitter-blue Follow, hot-pink Buy for $56, red YouTube Subscribe) — pill or small-radius chips with white text. Text link with arrow ("Explore the most creative Bentos →") in plain black, no underline. Circular avatars in a tight overlapping row. Small monochrome/brand-color icon squares for integrations.

## Signature
- Floating, slightly rotated bento-widget cards scattered around the page edges, doubling as both decoration and product demo
- One periwinkle accent doing all brand work (CTA + giant browser frame) against pure white
- Bold black centered headlines with a gradient fade-out effect on the stacked capability list
- Personality carried by real content thumbnails and pastel card tints rather than illustration or gradients

## Do
- Keep the page background pure white and let tinted cards supply all the color
- Center every section: headline, one-sentence gray subhead, single CTA
- Reuse the exact same primary button (color, label, size) top and bottom instead of inventing variants
- Scatter small rounded cards with soft shadows and 1-3° rotation as edge decoration
- Use small type sizes overall — even the CTA label is modest — and let whitespace create hierarchy

## Don't
- Don't add a full navigation bar or multi-column hero; the design depends on a bare centered stack
- Don't introduce a second saturated brand color in the chrome — service colors live only inside widget chips
- Don't use heavy borders or hard shadows on cards; shadows here are barely-there and diffuse
- Don't set headlines in anything other than tight, heavy grotesque black-on-white
- Don't fill the whitespace between sections; the huge empty gaps are part of the rhythm
