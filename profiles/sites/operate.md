## Overview
A monochrome-green "working document" aesthetic: nearly all text — headings, body, links — is set in one brand green on a pale gray, dot-grid paper canvas, so the whole page reads like an annotated engineering spec. Illustration carries the story: a chaos-to-clarity dot-path data viz in the hero, marker-drawn flowchart arrows, wireframe paper sculptures, and torn dark-green paper polygons for the CTA. Product screenshots are the only full-color, neutral-UI elements, making them pop against the tinted document world.

## Layout
Slim top bar styled as a document header ("OPERATE ALPHA" left, "Want in? [ Request early access ]" center) with vertical mono captions running down both page edges (document name, date). Hero is a full-width green-paper panel containing an abstract scatter-plot illustration bracketed by "Chaos ↔ Clarity" ruler labels, with the value prop in a small outlined annotation card at top right. Below, content shifts to a single narrow prose column (blog-like measure) with a sticky "On this page" table of contents in the right rail, complete with progress dots and a 0/100% counter. Sections are numbered (I., II., 2.1, 4.1) with roman-numeral chips; rhythm alternates centered display headings → green prose → large floating product screenshot or diagram. Footer is a light-green band laid out as an indexed colophon (4.1 ABOUT, 4.2 LINKS) crediting fonts and stack.

## Components
Primary CTA is a dark-green (#127024) torn-paper polygon panel holding a pill button with thin light border and white label ("Yes, it's a form"); small CTAs are tiny outlined mono chips like "EARLY ACCESS →". Section labels are pill-outline chips in mono caps ("WAITLIST", "THE CRM YOU DIDN'T KNOW YOU NEEDED"). Product UI mock is a white card with medium radius, subtle border, soft shadow, floating over the dot grid, annotated with black rounded callout tags (1.1 DIAL IT IN) carrying green status chips. Links are green with dotted/solid underlines; body lists use simple green bullets. Inline doodads (scissors glyph, shuffle tag, starburst) punctuate copy.

## Signature
- One-ink discipline: virtually every heading, paragraph, and link in the same forest green on gray dot-grid paper — no black text outside UI screenshots.
- Document conceit throughout: dated header, numbered sections (I/II/III, 2.1), sticky "On this page" TOC with completion percentage, colophon footer, vertical margin captions.
- Hand-annotated diagram language: thick marker-stroke green arrows, flowchart chips (TALK TO USER → GET FEEDBACK → SHIP FEEDBACK → RINSE & REPEAT), numbered black callout tags on screenshots.
- Torn dark-green paper polygons as CTA/hero surfaces, plus green-tinted paper texture panels bookending the page.

## Do / Don't
Do:
- Set headings, body, and links all in the single brand green; reserve neutral/black text for embedded product-UI screenshots and callout tags.
- Keep the dot-grid/columned paper texture visible across the canvas and tint full-bleed panels with the light paper green (#8cc197 range).
- Use mono (Cinetype-like) uppercase for every meta layer: section numbers, chips, dates, edge captions, footer index.
- Frame the CTA as an irregular torn-paper dark-green shape with a pill ghost button, not a flat rectangle.
- Annotate screenshots and diagrams with numbered tags and hand-drawn marker arrows to keep the "working document" feel.

Don't:
- Don't introduce a second brand hue for text or buttons — blue exists only inside the hero data-viz illustration.
- Don't use solid-filled colored buttons; CTAs here are outlined pills and chips sitting on green surfaces.
- Don't use pure white page backgrounds or hard geometric section dividers; surfaces are textured paper grays and greens with thin rules.
- Don't drop the numbering/TOC apparatus — without the document metaphor the one-color palette reads flat.
- Don't apply large radii to content panels; roundness belongs to pills, chips, and the product-frame corners only.
