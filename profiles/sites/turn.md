## Overview
Turn.io is maximal color-blocking: entire viewport-height sections are flooded with saturated fills — chartreuse yellow, tomato orange-red, deep forest green, violet, lavender, mint — with white used only as an occasional resting section, not the default. Warm documentary photography of real people (mothers, health workers, kids with phones) sits beside flat playful illustration and product-UI screenshots. The mood is optimistic civic-tech: loud color, humble type, human subject matter.

## Layout
Slim one-line nav on the section color (logo left, five links center-left, Log in + pill Sign up right); nav background inherits whatever color the hero uses. Heroes are short and typographic — a big centered or left headline on a solid color field with one pill CTA — followed by a logo strip of partners (Meta, OpenAI, CARE, Greenpeace). Rhythm is a vertical stack of full-bleed color slabs alternating dark (forest green, dark olive, deep purple) and light (chartreuse, lavender, white), often separated by curved/arched shapes and huge concentric-circle motifs behind CTAs. Case-study interludes pair a full-width photo with a small stat callout (e.g. "4.9m mothers / 40k questions"). Every page closes with the same yellow "Start improving lives today" band over faint concentric rings, then a black footer with an oversized turn.io wordmark.

## Components
Buttons: pill-shaped, solid fills, no border, no shadow; dark purple on light sections, white or light lavender on dark sections; small compact padding. Cards: pricing plans are large rounded (~16px) solid-fill panels (mint green Pro, lavender Enterprise) with an internal spec table of thin divider rows; community event cards are borderless — just a rounded image with date, title, and a purple "Learn more" text link below. Product screenshots sit in rounded-corner frames placed directly on the section color, occasionally inside a contrasting rounded "browser" slab. Feature chips on the solutions page are small pill tags in many alternating bright colors (yellow, green, pink, blue) packed into rows. Pricing toggle is a small pill segmented control with a "save 20%" tag. FAQ rows are plus-icon accordion lines. Checkmarks in comparison tables are green circular badges.

## Signature
- Full-bleed section color-blocking in an unusual chartreuse/forest-green/orange-red/violet set — almost no gray anywhere
- Giant faint concentric circles radiating behind CTAs and phone mockups
- Documentary photos of beneficiaries interleaved with flat character illustration and real product UI
- Repeated closing ritual: yellow "Start improving lives today" band + black footer with an enormous wordmark and "We are hiring" tag

## Do / Don't
Do:
- Fill whole sections edge-to-edge with saturated brand colors; let the nav and footer adopt the adjacent section's color
- Use pill buttons exclusively, dark purple on light grounds and white/lavender on dark grounds
- Alternate dark and light slabs down the page and bridge them with curved shapes or arcs
- Ground bold color with real-world photography and concrete impact stats (users, messages, mothers reached)
- Keep headlines sentence-case, warm, and human ("Scale conversations. Not your team.")

Don't:
- Introduce gray or muted corporate palettes — even table sections sit on white or a color, never gray
- Add borders or drop shadows to buttons and cards; hierarchy comes from color contrast alone
- Use sharp rectangular corners on any interactive element or media frame
- Write ALL-CAPS headings or use thin/light display weights
- Let sections share a background color with their neighbors — each slab should hard-cut to a new hue
