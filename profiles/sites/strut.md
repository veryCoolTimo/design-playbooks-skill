## Overview
Warm, paper-like minimalism: an unbroken oat-beige canvas, charcoal type set in one slightly techy grotesque at generous sizes, and a single marigold-yellow pill as the only saturated color. Product screenshots do the visual heavy lifting — crisp off-white app windows floating on the beige field. Mid-page the palette inverts to a near-black charcoal block for drama, then returns to beige.

## Layout
Slim top nav: wordmark left, three text links plus a yellow "Sign Up" pill right, all sitting directly on the beige canvas with no bar or border. Hero is left-aligned display text ("The complete writing workspace") overlapping a large full-app screenshot that bleeds off the top-right of the viewport. Sections alternate heading+copy on one side with oversized screenshot clusters on the other, separated by full-width hairline rules. Rhythm is long and airy — one idea per viewport. A full-bleed dark section ("Edit. Focus. Finish.", centered, stacked one word per line) breaks the page, with perforated ticket-stub edges top and bottom. Near the footer, a bento-style feature grid mixes a large dark screenshot tile with small labeled chips (Notes, Ideas, Characters, Drafts). Interstitial oversized statement paragraphs (large body text as section copy) act as breathing room between demo sections.

## Components
Buttons: full pills, no border, no shadow. Primary is flat marigold #FEB546 with dark charcoal label; secondary is muted greige #C6C3B8 pill with dark label — both quiet, small, sentence-case. Cards: off-white (#FCFAF6) app-window screenshots with medium (~10-12px) rounded corners, hairline neutral borders, and at most a whisper of shadow. Feature list items are small text rows with tiny line icons and hairline separators, not boxed cards. In the dark section, tiles go #2C2E2F with lighter inner screenshot panels; a yellow "Rachel" collaborator cursor tag and a toolbar popover echo the accent. Small UI chips (Notes, Drafts, Done) carry tiny colored dot/line icons (green, purple, orange) as the only other color.

## Signature
- One yellow pill (#FEB546) as the entire color story on an oat-beige page — no gradients, no secondary hues in the marketing chrome.
- Single grotesque typeface everywhere, with huge headings set in regular/medium weight rather than bold.
- Beige-to-charcoal full-bleed inversion mid-page with perforated ticket-stub edges.
- Screenshots as hero imagery: real app windows in off-white, overlapping the hero headline and stacked in loose collages.

## Do / Don't
Do:
- Keep the whole page on one warm beige (#E3DED3); reserve pure-ish white (#FCFAF6) for app screenshots and UI cards only.
- Use the yellow pill for exactly the primary action (Sign Up / Get Started); pair the secondary action as a greige pill beside it.
- Set display type large but light — regular/medium weight, left-aligned, sentence case, tight leading.
- Separate sections with full-width 1px hairlines and generous vertical whitespace instead of background changes.
- Use one inverted charcoal section for the emotional beat, with centered stacked words and decorative perforated edges.

Don't:
- Don't add blues, gradients, or glassmorphism — the palette is strictly beige/charcoal/yellow plus tiny status-dot colors inside UI chips.
- Don't put shadows, borders, or outlines on buttons; pills are flat fills only.
- Don't use bold or condensed display weights, all-caps headings, or a second typeface.
- Don't box feature lists into heavy cards; keep them as icon + text rows with hairline dividers.
- Don't use illustration or stock photography — product screenshots are the only imagery.
