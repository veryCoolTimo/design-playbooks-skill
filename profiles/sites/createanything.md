## Overview
A dreamlike photographic hero — a retro CRT monitor sitting in a pink flower field under a soft blue sky — sells the "turn imagination into software" promise, while everything below it snaps to a stark, utilitarian black-and-white product UI. The tension between the cinematic image plus serif wordmark and the clinical grotesque-sans sections is the whole identity. Color is rationed: photography and tiny green/blue text highlights carry all the warmth; interface chrome stays monochrome.

## Layout
Floating pill nav bar centered over the hero photo, with a dark pill "Get started" button at the right; on the white pricing page the nav flattens to a plain top bar. Home hero is full-bleed photography with centered white serif headline, a one-line sub, and a prompt-input bar as the primary interaction (chat box, not a form). Sections alternate hard between white and near-black (#191919) full-width bands, each with a small centered heading + one-line subhead, then card grids or large product screenshots. Generous vertical whitespace between bands; content column stays narrow and centered. The page closes with a second sky-gradient band, another prompt input, and an oversized cropped "Anything" wordmark (green italic serif + blue outline sans) bleeding off the fold above a charcoal footer. Pricing: centered title, monthly/yearly segmented toggle, 4-column plan cards, long single-column comparison table with uppercase micro-labels per group, stacked accordion FAQ.

## Components
Buttons: solid black fill, white text, pill or near-pill radius, no shadow; secondary buttons are white with a thin light-gray border (Free plan's "Get Started"). Cards: white with 1px #e5e5e5-ish borders, 8-14px radius, flat (no visible shadows); the "Popular" pricing card gets a faint gray fill and a small black "Popular" tab on top. Testimonial cards are plain bordered rectangles with avatar + name + role. Inputs: the prompt bar is a rounded white field with placeholder text and a circular black submit button inside its right edge; credit-selector dropdowns are small bordered pills. FAQ rows: full-width bordered rounded rows with right-aligned chevrons. Checkmarks are thin gray/black glyphs; table uses hairline row dividers only.

## Signature
- Surreal photo hero: analog hardware (CRT) placed in an idyllic flower-field landscape, with the chat prompt input embedded directly in the image.
- Typographic collision as branding: italic serif "Any" fused to grotesque sans "thing", echoed by serif hero headline over sans-everything-else, and a giant cropped two-tone wordmark near the footer.
- Strict white/near-black alternating section bands with almost no chrome color — green or blue appears only as single highlighted words ("Amazing", "default").
- Product proof via embedded UI screenshots (phone mockups, dashboards, chat threads) rather than illustration or iconography.

## Do / Don't
Do:
- Reserve photography for hero and closing bands; keep all mid-page sections flat white or #191919 with monochrome UI.
- Use solid-black pill buttons for every primary CTA, including inside cards; outline-only white buttons for the free/secondary tier.
- Highlight at most one word per headline in green or blue; leave the rest in ink.
- Pair a serif display moment (hero line, wordmark) with grotesque sans for all headings, labels, and body.
- Lead with a working prompt/chat input as the hero CTA instead of a signup form.

Don't:
- Add drop shadows, gradients, or tinted fills to cards — everything sits on hairline borders and flat surfaces.
- Introduce a saturated brand color for buttons or links; the CTA is black, full stop.
- Use icon sets or illustrations in feature sections — real product screenshots do that work.
- Round cards to pill extremes or square off buttons; keep the pill-button / medium-radius-card split.
- Fill the layout edge to edge — content stays in a narrow centered column with big vertical breathing room between bands.
