## Overview
A warm, near-monochrome minimal-light page: off-white gray canvas, near-black ink, and a single near-black CTA fill, punctuated by real photography of people and small Figma-style multiplayer cursor accents in green, purple, and terracotta. The whole page reads like a well-organized design file — hairline dotted column guides run vertically through sections. Color is earned, not decorative: a soft tan (#e0dbcf) block breaks the rhythm mid-page, and everything else stays grayscale plus photo.

## Layout
Sticky-feel top nav: logo left, five text links center-right, then a paired CTA group (outlined "Join Free Slack" with Slack icon + solid black "View Bootcamps"). Hero is a two-column split: huge 3-line bold headline left with short paragraph and one black button; right side is a 2x3 video-call grid of smiling students with floating name-tag cursors around it. Sections alternate: centered oversized headline intros with tiny uppercase gray kickers ("ONLINE TECH CAMPUS", "LIVE BOOTCAMPS"), a monochrome logo strip, a 4-column course-card grid separated by dotted vertical hairlines, an avatar masonry wall, a full-width tan feature block, a stats row, a video-thumbnail strip, and a testimonial masonry of quote cards. Generous whitespace throughout; content sits in a wide centered container.

## Components
Primary button: solid near-black (#1b1a1d) fill, white medium-weight label, ~8px radius, no shadow. Secondary button: white/transparent fill with a 1px near-black border, same radius ("View Bootcamp", "Join Waitlist"); disabled/waitlist states render in muted gray. Nav secondary CTA carries a leading Slack icon inside the outlined pill-ish rectangle. Course cards are borderless: rounded photo thumbnail with a small "UX/UI" chip and a row of tiny tool-logo badges overlaid, red star + "5/5 · 100% completion rate" metadata line, bold title, gray "Part-Time · N weeks" subline over a short hairline rule, then a button. Testimonials are plain text cards with small circular avatars, name, role, and flag emoji — no borders or shadows. Cursor name-tags are small colored rounded rectangles with pointer arrows.

## Signature
- Figma-multiplayer cursor name-tags (green, purple, lavender, terracotta) floating around hero and section imagery
- Dotted vertical column guides visible between grid columns, making the page look like a design canvas
- Near-total grayscale UI where photography of real students supplies all the warmth and color
- Tiny uppercase letter-spaced gray kickers above every oversized bold section headline
- One soft tan (#e0dbcf) full-width block as the sole non-gray surface

## Do
- Keep the UI chrome grayscale (#f8f8f8 canvas, #1b1b1b ink) and let candid people-photography carry color
- Use solid near-black fills for the single primary CTA and 1px-outlined ghosts for everything secondary
- Precede every section headline with a small uppercase gray kicker label
- Scatter a few small colored cursor/name-tag accents near imagery to signal live collaboration
- Separate grid columns with faint dotted hairlines instead of card borders or shadows

## Don't
- Don't add drop shadows, gradients, or colored button fills — no saturated primary exists anywhere
- Don't box testimonials or course cards in visible borders; whitespace and hairlines do the separation
- Don't use more than one tinted section background (the tan block is a deliberate single break)
- Don't introduce a second accent color into buttons or links; accents live only in cursor tags and the red rating star
- Don't tighten the vertical rhythm — sections breathe with very large gaps between oversized headline intros
