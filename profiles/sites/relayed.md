## Overview
Minimal-light SaaS language with one theatrical exception: the home hero and every footer flip to near-black navy (#14162B), while interior pages stay stark white. Color is rationed to a pink-to-violet gradient family that appears as CTA fill, inline highlighted words, and hand-drawn squiggle illustrations. Everything else is monochrome, giving the few gradient moments outsized presence.

## Layout
Slim top nav: logo left ("Relayed by Typeform"), centered text links (Product, Use cases, Pricing, About), Log in + gradient pill CTA right. Home opens with a huge centered two-line headline over dark canvas, decorated with small squiggle doodles overlapping the letterforms. Sections alternate: centered statement blocks, 3-column icon+text feature rows, and on Product/Use cases a strict zig-zag two-column rhythm (text left / visual right, then swapped) repeating 5-6 times down the page. Whitespace is extreme — sections are separated by 150-300px of empty canvas, sometimes bridged only by a thin gradient squiggle divider. Footer: dark navy band with oversized "Interaction for a remote age." statement plus two link columns.

## Components
- Primary button: pill, pink-to-violet horizontal gradient fill (#E75BDF → #9D5CF0 feel), white label, no border or shadow; small size even in nav.
- Secondary "Start recording" button inside a product card: solid pink pill with icon.
- Cards: testimonial cards on dark hero are slightly-lighter dark panels (#1E2033-ish), ~10px radius, avatar + name + role header, no border. Light-page visual cards are white with very soft diffuse shadow and 12px radius (e.g., "Choose the service you want to connect" integration list).
- Product screenshot framed in a rounded (~14px) white window with player controls; big circular gradient play button overlay.
- Inputs/dropdowns (share-permissions widget): white, thin gray border, small radius, tiny labels.

## Signature
- Hand-drawn multicolor squiggle illustrations — profiles/faces made of single pink/violet/black strokes — used instead of photos or 3D renders.
- Pink→violet gradient reserved for exactly three jobs: CTA pill, highlighted words inside headlines ("captures", "summarizes", "stay up to date"), and wavy divider lines.
- Dark-navy bookends (hero + footer) sandwiching pure-white body sections.
- Headlines and section titles end with a period ("Conversation hub.", "Zoom ready.", "Research.").

## Do
- Keep body pages white and reserve #14162B for the hero and footer bands only.
- Apply the gradient to key words inside otherwise white/black headlines to create emphasis without buttons.
- Use loose one-stroke doodle illustrations in pink/violet/black as section art; let them overlap text edges in the hero.
- Maintain the strict alternating text/visual two-column rhythm with very tall gaps between sections.
- End every heading with a period and keep headings short (2-5 words).

## Don't
- Don't add borders or heavy shadows to buttons — the gradient pill stands alone.
- Don't introduce photography or detailed screenshots as illustration; the only realistic imagery is the framed product UI.
- Don't use the gradient for body text, backgrounds, or large fills — it stays small and rationed.
- Don't tighten section spacing; density would kill the airy, statement-per-screen pacing.
- Don't add more nav items or a mega-menu — the header stays to four links plus two actions.
