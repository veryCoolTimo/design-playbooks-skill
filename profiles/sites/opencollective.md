## Overview
A friendly civic-fintech language: heavy navy headlines on generous white, hand-drawn ink-line characters sitting on loose pastel watercolor blobs, and real community photographs scattered in casual collages. One saturated navy (#0D2D66) does all the serious work — headings, CTAs, a full-bleed mid-page section — while the pastels (baby blue, pink, mint, lime) keep the money talk warm and grassroots. Bold inline phrases inside body copy carry emphasis instead of extra headings.

## Layout
Slim white top nav: wordmark left, centered dropdown links (Solutions, Product, Company), search field and Sign In right. Hero is fully centered: huge navy headline, short paragraph with bolded key phrases, pill CTA pair, then a floating product-UI screenshot flanked by an illustrated character. Sections alternate: centered heading blocks, photo collages (overlapping rotated snapshots), and zig-zag two-column rows pairing a watercolor illustration with a short text block + text link. A curved-top full-bleed navy section holds a network diagram and a 3x2 grid of fiscal-host cards. Footer stack: torn-paper pink watercolor band with navy "Join the movement" panel and white CTA cards, then a plain white link footer. Whitespace is abundant; content column stays narrow and centered.

## Components
Buttons: solid navy pill ("Start Now", "Get in touch"), and white pill with 1px light gray border for secondary ("Watch Video" with play glyph). No shadows on buttons. Cards: white, large radius (16-24px), thin navy or light-gray border, flat — the stat band card is a full-width outlined rounded rectangle with a navy arrow icon as its only CTA. Fiscal-host cards on the navy section: white rounded cards with illustrated/photo headers, flag + country label, bold navy title, small gray body. Feature list items: small pastel line-icon above bold label and tiny gray text, arranged in loose icon grids. Search input: pill with inset icon.

## Signature
- Hand-drawn black ink-line mascots (birds, characters with umbrellas, rockets) sitting on loose pastel watercolor washes.
- One deep navy (#0D2D66) used everywhere serious: display type, CTA fill, arrows, and an entire curved-top page-width section.
- Torn-paper / rough watercolor edges as section dividers (pink footer band).
- Overlapping, slightly rotated real-community photo collages instead of stock hero imagery.

## Do / Don't
Do:
- Set headlines in heavy tight grotesque, navy #0D2D66, centered, and bold key phrases inline within body paragraphs.
- Use pill buttons: navy fill for primary, white with hairline gray border for secondary; keep them shadow-free.
- Pair each feature block with a watercolor-blob illustration in one pastel (blue/pink/green) plus black line art.
- Break the white page with one full-bleed navy section with a curved or torn top edge; put white rounded cards on it.
- Use thin navy outlines on large-radius white cards; let a lone arrow icon serve as the card CTA.

Don't:
- Don't introduce gradients, drop shadows, or glassmorphism — everything here is flat fills and hairline borders.
- Don't use corporate stock photography; photos must look like candid community snapshots, collaged and rotated.
- Don't add a second saturated brand color; pastels stay washed-out and navy stays the only strong hue.
- Don't square off buttons — buttons are always pills, and card corners never drop below ~16px.
- Don't crowd sections; each section is one idea with generous vertical padding and a narrow centered column.
