## Overview
Warm light-gray canvas overlaid with thin hairline vertical grid columns, near-black ink, and pill-shaped monochrome CTAs. The look is architectural Swiss-minimal: huge centered grotesque headlines, tiny uppercase mono eyebrows, and white or near-black content cards sitting flat on the grid. Color appears only in small doses — a mint-cyan trial button on the home page, blue line-art icons, and pastel tag chips on the blog.

## Layout
Floating capsule nav bar centered at the top: white pill with hairline border containing logo, text links, an outlined "Book a demo" pill, and a filled dark "Get started for free" pill. Heroes are centered display type (2-3 lines) with a small mono subline and one or two pill CTAs beneath. Full-page background carries visible vertical hairline rules (6-column feel) that sections snap to; alternating white and canvas-gray bands create rhythm. Home floats product-UI screenshot cards around concentric hairline circles. Resources uses a 3-column masonry blog grid mixing dark and light card covers. Footer is a full-bleed near-black block with 4-column link lists and a giant ghosted wordmark at the bottom edge.

## Components
Buttons: full pills, three tiers — filled near-black with white label (primary), white with hairline border (secondary), and a mint-cyan #35F0D2 filled pill used for trial CTAs on the home page; arrow glyph often trails the primary label. Cards: flat white rectangles with 2-6px corners, hairline or no border, essentially shadowless; pricing pairs an inverted near-black card (highlighted Pro plan) with a white one, each ending in a full-width pill button. FAQ rows are white bars with a right-aligned plus icon. Tag chips on blog cards are small colored pills (pink, blue, green, purple). Inputs: white rounded field with inline pill submit button ("Build →"). Compliance badges are tiny icon-plus-mono-label lockups in a single row.

## Signature
- Hairline vertical grid columns visible on the page background, with sections and cards snapping to them
- Floating white capsule nav containing both an outlined and a filled pill CTA
- Tracked-out uppercase mono micro-labels ("ASK QATALOG", "NO TECHNICAL EXPERTISE REQUIRED") under huge grotesque headlines
- Dark/light card inversion for emphasis (black Pro pricing card, black blog covers) instead of adding color
- Giant ghosted wordmark bleeding off the bottom of the dark footer

## Do
- Keep the palette monochrome-first: black pills on light gray, reserving cyan/blue/pastels for one accent per view
- Show the vertical hairline grid on the canvas and align every section and card edge to it
- Set heroes as large centered grotesque type with a small uppercase mono eyebrow or subline
- Use inversion (near-black card on light canvas) to highlight a plan or featured item
- End dark-card layouts with a full-width white pill button inside the card

## Don't
- Don't add drop shadows or heavy borders — cards read flat with hairlines at most
- Don't mix corner languages: buttons are always pills, cards stay near-square; no medium-rounded hybrids
- Don't use gradient or multicolor CTAs — the only colored button is the flat mint trial pill
- Don't left-align hero copy; headlines, sublines, and CTA rows are centered
- Don't fill the canvas with saturated section backgrounds — color lives only in chips, icons, and blog cover art
