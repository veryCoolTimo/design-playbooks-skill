## Overview
A scrapbook-meets-moodboard landing page: grainy salmon-pink hero, then a pale cream section overlaid with light-blue graph-paper grid, all typeset in one loud red-orange rounded sans. There is almost no neutral text — headlines ARE the copy, and imagery is a physical-feeling collage (gilded picture frame, paint-chip cards, taped sketches) rather than product-shot cards. The only cool color is a saturated blue reserved for the CTA label and small doodads.

## Layout
No conventional navbar: a lone white pill CTA sits top-right with a stacked red serif company mark and tiny icon row beneath it. The hero is just a giant centered two-line headline ("Arc is a browser...") with sticker-like doodles attached to the letters. Sections are full-bleed color bands (pink → graph-paper cream → plain cream) with generous vertical whitespace; content is a single centered column. Mid-page, a loose asymmetric collage of framed screenshots and swatches replaces any grid of features. A dotted red vertical rule leads the eye down to the closing question and repeated CTA. Footer is minimal: logo left, red serif attribution right.

## Components
Buttons: white pill-ish rectangles (roughly 8-12px radius) with subtle soft shadow, blue (#3B49E0) label plus a small arrow — identical top-right and bottom-center. Cards: none in the UI sense; images sit directly on the canvas as collage pieces with drop shadows, one inside an ornate gold frame. No inputs, no bordered containers; texture (film grain on the pink band, graph-grid on the cream band) does the sectioning work.

## Signature
- One-color typography: nearly every word on the page is the same red-orange, set huge in a chubby rounded sans.
- Background texture as structure: grainy pink and blue graph-paper backgrounds delineate sections instead of dividers or cards.
- Analog collage art direction — gilded frame, paint swatches, taped da Vinci-style sketches — standing in for product screenshots.
- Typographic play: an italicized word and a letterspaced "s p a c e" mid-headline; sticker doodles perched on hero letters.

## Do / Don't
**Do**
- Set headlines enormous, centered, and in the single red-orange ink; let them carry the narrative with minimal supporting body text.
- Use textured full-bleed background bands (grain, graph paper) to separate sections.
- Keep CTAs as small white shadowed rectangles with blue label + arrow, repeated top-right and at page end.
- Present the product inside playful analog props (frames, swatch cards, pinned scraps) with soft drop shadows.
- Inject typographic mischief sparingly: one italic word, one letterspaced word, a sticker on a letterform.

**Don't**
- Add a standard horizontal nav bar, link list, or mega-menu — the page has none.
- Introduce card grids, bordered panels, or feature-tile layouts; content floats directly on the canvas.
- Use gray or black body text blocks; neutral ink appears nowhere except tiny UI-screenshot content.
- Fill buttons with the brand red or blue — color lives in text and backgrounds, buttons stay white.
- Flatten the backgrounds to clean solid white; the grain and grid textures are core to the look.
