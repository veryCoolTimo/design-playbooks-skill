## Overview
Neon-maximalist retro-pop language built around the mixtape metaphor: skeuomorphic cassette-tape illustrations sit on saturated flat color fields with diagonal streak and starburst textures. Every section is a full-bleed color block — electric blue, acid yellow, mint green, bubblegum pink — with near-black or white type slammed on top in heavy all-caps. The hero background rotates between colorways (cyan/lime and yellow/green seen), matching the color-swappable cassette product itself.

## Layout
Minimal nav: just a small centered logo lockup at the top, no menu links. Hero is a centered stack — cassette illustration, giant two-line all-caps headline ("MAKE YOUR PLAYLIST POP"), single small black pill CTA — on a textured full-bleed color field. Below, a 3-step feature sequence: each step is a huge rounded rectangle panel in its own color (yellow, green, pink) with left-aligned step label + headline + short copy and a right-side product/chat screenshot. Then a dark charcoal band with a horizontally scrolling card row of cassette use-cases, and a closing electric-blue CTA section with a mock chat exchange. Rhythm is loud block / loud block / dark palate-cleanser / loud block; whitespace lives mostly in generous panel padding rather than empty canvas.

## Components
- Buttons: small pills with solid fills — black pill with white label in the hero ("Get it started"), yellow pill with black label on the blue footer section ("Create a Cassettine"). No borders, no visible shadow, modest size relative to the shouting headlines.
- Cards: large-radius (~16-24px) flat color rectangles, no border, no drop shadow; color itself does the separation. Use-case cards stack cassette art above an all-caps title and small caption.
- Screenshots/embeds: rounded-corner dark UI captures (Spotify, WhatsApp) inset inside colored panels.
- Small chips/tags: tiny pill "Model A/B/C" selectors inside the flow mockup; chat-bubble style rounded labels ("Click on it!", "Wow!") used as decorative UI.
- Cassette illustrations: detailed pseudo-3D renders with handwritten-script labels on the tape stickers — the core visual asset.

## Signature
- Skeuomorphic cassette tapes with handwritten sticker labels as the recurring hero object and card art.
- Full-bleed section color blocking in clashing brights (blue, acid yellow, mint, pink) with diagonal streak / starburst background textures.
- Heavy wide all-caps grotesque headlines set directly on flat color, black on brights, white on blue.
- Playful marginalia: doodle cassette mascot, circular "tap here" rotating badge, hand-drawn arrows, fake chat bubbles.

## Do / Don't
**Do**
- Give every section its own saturated flat background color and treat panels as large rounded color slabs with generous internal padding.
- Set headlines in a heavy wide grotesque, all caps, black on bright fields and white on the blue canvas.
- Add energy with diagonal streaks, starbursts, and hand-drawn doodles/arrows rather than gradients or photography.
- Keep CTAs as small solid pills (black or yellow) that contrast the field they sit on — let headlines carry the size, not buttons.
- Lean into the cassette/mixtape metaphor: illustrated hardware, handwritten script labels, chat-message mockups for the sharing story.

**Don't**
- Don't use white or neutral gray page backgrounds — the palette never drops below full saturation except the one charcoal band.
- Don't add borders, strokes, or drop shadows to cards; separation comes from color contrast alone.
- Don't use sentence-case or lightweight headings — display type is always heavy and all-caps.
- Don't introduce gradients, glassmorphism, or photographic imagery; everything is flat color plus illustration.
- Don't oversize buttons or multiply CTAs — one small pill per section is the pattern.
