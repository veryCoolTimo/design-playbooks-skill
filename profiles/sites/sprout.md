## Overview
A retro-warm, scrapbook-flavored landing page in a sunbaked palette: mustard yellow, terracotta coral, cream, and deep burgundy-maroon used as both ink and near-black surface. Big friendly serif headlines sit over collage-style product mockups full of webcam cutouts, polaroids, stickers, and flowers. The whole page reads like a craft-paper mood board rather than a tech SaaS.

## Layout
Slim top nav on the yellow hero: wordmark left, "Log in" plus one dark CTA button right. Hero is centered — oversized two-line serif headline, short sans subcopy, then a large browser-chrome mockup as the visual anchor; small floating avatar bubbles decorate the hero edges. Below, the page alternates full-bleed color bands (cream → coral → cream → burgundy → white → blush pink → cream → burgundy footer), each band one idea with a centered serif heading and a mockup or 2-column feature pair. One section flips to a left-aligned heading with side-by-side text/webcam card. A horizontal gallery strip of user-made spaces runs edge to edge near the end. Generous vertical padding; content column stays narrow and centered.

## Components
- Buttons: solid fills, small corner radius, no visible border or shadow. Dark burgundy fill with white label for the nav CTA; coral fill for the closing "Create a new space" CTA; white/outlined variant ("Try it with your face") on cream.
- Product mockups framed as macOS-style browser windows: dark burgundy chrome bar with traffic-light dots, medium-rounded corners, containing grid-paper canvases.
- Content cards inside mockups (webcam frames, sticky notes) are flat, thin-bordered or paper-like with slight rounding; polaroid-style photo frames with white borders.
- Small pill/underline tab labels beneath mockups act as scenario switchers (Play time, 1:1 Meeting, ...).
- Sparkle/star glyphs used as decorative accents near the final CTA.

## Signature
- Deep burgundy (#3B0A14–#4A0E1E) doing triple duty as headline ink, button fill, and full section background — no pure black anywhere.
- Alternating full-bleed warm color bands (mustard, coral, blush, cream) that give the page a striped, poster-like rhythm.
- Collage aesthetic: grid-paper canvases, webcam cutouts, stickers, flowers, and polaroids inside browser-chrome mockups.
- Playful copy in a soft serif with mixed styling tricks (strikethrough "chair" → "Facedock", ampersands in "Meet, create & play").

## Do / Don't
Do:
- Use burgundy as the universal ink and primary button color on warm light backgrounds; reserve coral for the final conversion CTA.
- Alternate saturated warm section backgrounds (mustard, terracotta, blush) with cream to pace the scroll.
- Frame product shots in browser-window chrome with rounded corners and grid-paper interiors full of collage elements.
- Pair large soft-serif headings with small, plain grotesque body copy for contrast.
- Keep buttons compact with small radii and flat fills — no gradients or shadows.

Don't:
- Introduce cool colors (blue, teal, violet) — the palette is strictly warm earth tones.
- Use pure black or pure white text; ink is always the deep burgundy, and light text sits on burgundy bands.
- Add heavy drop shadows, glassmorphism, or glossy 3D — surfaces stay flat and papery.
- Make buttons pill-shaped or oversized; CTAs here are modest rectangles.
- Sterilize the mockups — empty, clean UI screenshots would lose the scrapbook personality that defines the brand.
