## Overview
A retro pixel-art world wrapped around an otherwise clean, light SaaS page. The entire canvas is a pale sky blue dotted with pixelated clouds; headings are set in a chunky bitmap font, a pixel robot mascot wanders the page, and the footer is literal 8-bit grass and soil. Orange is the single loud color, used for CTAs, heading highlights, and inline link underlines against near-black pixel headlines and slate body text.

## Layout
Sticky-feeling top nav: pixel wordmark left, plain text links (Features, Pricing, FAQ, Blog, Discord) center-left, orange text link plus one orange pill CTA right. Hero is centered: two-line pixel headline (black line, orange line), short paragraph, primary CTA paired with a quiet text alternative, then a large light-mode app screenshot framed as a macOS window. Sections alternate centered pixel headlines with two-column comparison cards, a three-up card row, a full-width white "protocol" panel with a pixel-dripping orange top border, a stacked single-column FAQ accordion, and a personal letter card with a team photo. Generous vertical whitespace throughout; decorative pixel clouds fill the gaps. The page ends with grass/dirt pixel terrain that becomes the dark brown footer.

## Components
Buttons: orange (#ff652f) pills/rounded rectangles with a subtle top-light gradient and soft drop shadow, white label, often with an Apple glyph; secondary actions are plain dark text with a small icon, no border. Cards: white, large radius (~16-24px), faint shadow; the "Obscura" comparison card gets a 1px orange border while the "traditional VPN" card stays neutral. FAQ rows: white pill-radius bars with a gray "?" icon left and a thin "+" right. Inline links are orange with dotted/dashed underlines. The app-screenshot card mimics macOS chrome (traffic-light dots, sidebar). Footer sits on solid brown (#6c3200) with white text and thin divider rules.

## Signature
- Bitmap/pixel display font for every headline, with one phrase per headline flipped to orange.
- Pixel-art scenery: clouds on the sky-blue canvas, an orange robot mascot recurring down the page, and a grass-and-dirt terrain strip leading into the brown footer.
- Single accent discipline: orange carries CTAs, highlights, links, and diagram nodes; everything else is white, slate, and sky blue.
- Playful game-world framing (dripping pixel border, mascot "head-janitor" letter) applied to serious privacy content.

## Do / Don't
**Do**
- Set headlines in a pixel/bitmap face and highlight exactly one phrase per headline in the orange accent.
- Keep the whole page on the pale sky-blue canvas (#dcedfd) with white cards floating on it; reserve dark brown for the footer only.
- Pair every primary orange CTA with a low-key text-plus-icon secondary ("Other platforms").
- Scatter small pixel clouds and the mascot as section connective tissue, at low density.
- Use dotted/dashed orange underlines for inline links inside slate body text.

**Don't**
- Don't use the pixel font for body copy, nav links, or FAQ questions — those stay in the clean grotesque.
- Don't introduce a second accent hue in the UI; green and brown exist only in the terrain/footer illustration.
- Don't put gradients or photography in section backgrounds; the only photo on the page is the small team snapshot inside the letter card.
- Don't sharpen corners — nothing on the page is square-cornered except pixel art itself.
- Don't make comparison cards symmetric; the favored option carries the orange border and mascot while the rival stays gray.
