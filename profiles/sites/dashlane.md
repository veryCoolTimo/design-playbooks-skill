## Overview
Warm, pastel-blocked consumer-security design: a soft blush-pink hero with a 3D toy-like product illustration, followed by alternating white, mint, and peach section bands, all anchored by one very dark teal that serves as ink, button fill, and full-bleed band color. Friendly and approachable rather than techy — lifestyle photography and playful 3D renders instead of screenshots or lock icons.

## Layout
Slim white top nav: logo left, centered dropdown links (Business / Personal / Plans / Try), Log in + solid dark "Get Dashlane" button right. Hero is a 50/50 split on a full-bleed pink band — illustration left, left-aligned headline + copy + dual CTAs right. Below: a pale-gray press-logo strip, then a steady rhythm of full-width color bands (white, mint #E4F4F3, peach #FFEDD6, dark teal) with generous vertical padding. Recurring patterns: eyebrow label in small caps above headings, three-column icon-feature row with a segmented Business/Personal toggle, inset promo cards (webinar, customer-logos) sitting as colored rectangles on white, a 3-card blog grid, an FAQ accordion split heading-left/questions-right, and a dark full-bleed testimonial quote band and final CTA band before a dense dark-teal footer.

## Components
Buttons: rectangular with tiny radius, compact padding, small label text; primary is solid dark teal (#0E3A44) with white text, secondary is white/transparent with a thin dark border — the two are frequently paired ("For you" / "For your business"). On dark bands the pairing inverts (white solid + white outline). Cards: flat, no shadows, no borders — differentiation comes entirely from background-color blocks; blog cards are an image block over left-aligned bold title and small body on white. FAQ rows are thin-rule-divided accordions with chevrons. Inputs not shown. Photography is squared-corner editorial lifestyle shots, sometimes overlapping band edges.

## Signature
- One dark teal (#0E353D-ish) doing triple duty: all text, all primary buttons, and full-bleed quote/CTA/footer bands.
- Pastel section striping — blush pink, mint, peach — instead of white-plus-gray alternation.
- Soft 3D "toy" illustration of the product in the hero on a monochrome pink stage.
- Flat, shadowless composition; hierarchy built from color blocks and type weight, never elevation.

## Do / Don't
Do:
- Pair every primary CTA with an outline secondary twin (personal vs. business paths).
- Alternate full-width pastel bands (pink, mint, peach) with white sections to create rhythm.
- Set headings and body in the same grotesque; use bold weight and size, not color, for hierarchy.
- Use the dark teal for full-bleed statement bands (press quote, final CTA) with white serif/logo accents.
- Keep corners nearly square across buttons, cards, and photos.

Don't:
- Add drop shadows, gradients, or glassmorphism — everything here is flat fills.
- Introduce a bright blue or green "tech" accent; the palette stays warm pastels + one dark teal.
- Use pill buttons or heavily rounded cards.
- Show UI screenshots in the hero — this brand leads with soft 3D illustration and lifestyle photos.
- Put borders around cards; separate content with background-color changes instead.
