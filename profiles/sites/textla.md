## Overview
A cartoon desert-Western world rendered in a strict three-color system: cream-yellow canvas, deep forest-green ink, and pops of bright yellow and kelly green. Every card, button, and product mockup wears a solid dark-green outline, so the whole site reads like a hand-inked comic strip. Playful roadrunner-mascot illustrations (cacti, mesas, paragliders, a flag-waving tank) fill section transitions instead of photography.

## Layout
Slim top nav on the cream canvas: left logo, center links (Product, Resources, Pricing), right Log in + yellow pill CTA + dark-green pill phone number. Hero is split: left-aligned headline with an italic accent word plus dual CTAs; right side a floating outlined SMS-dashboard mockup over an illustrated desert scene. Sections alternate full-bleed background bands — cream, yellow-green tint, saturated yellow, and dark forest green — separated by illustrated mesa/mountain silhouettes acting as wavy dividers. Two-column feature rows pair floating chat-bubble/table mockups with short copy; 3-column card grids for blog/plans. A scrolling "Get a demo" marquee ribbon (yellow band, alternating roman/italic) breaks up long pages. Generous vertical rhythm; centered headlines mid-page.

## Components
Buttons: pill-shaped, solid yellow fill with dark-green text for primary, dark-green fill with light text for secondary/phone, plus outlined text links with arrow glyphs. Cards: white or tinted fills, 16px+ corners, uniform 1.5-2px dark-green borders, no drop shadows — hierarchy comes from fill color (cream, yellow, green, dark green) not elevation. Pricing table columns are outlined white cards with green check / crossed circle icons. FAQ rows are full-width outlined pills with chevrons. Inputs: white pill fields with green outline (newsletter). Product mockups are drawn as outlined floating panels with pill tags and chat bubbles, matching card styling exactly.

## Signature
- Everything outlined: 2px forest-green strokes on every card, button, mockup, and stat pill — zero drop shadows.
- Desert cartoon universe: cacti, mesas, roadrunner mascot, paraglider, tank with a Textla flag as recurring section-divider art.
- Headlines mix an upright bold sans with one oversized green serif-italic word ("Unbeatable", "outreach", "affordable").
- Four-band color rhythm down the page: cream → yellow-green → bright yellow → dark green, with silhouette landscape transitions.

## Do / Don't
**Do**
- Give every surface (cards, buttons, mockups, inputs) the same dark-green outline weight; use fill color, not shadow, for hierarchy.
- Use yellow pill buttons with dark-green labels as the primary CTA everywhere, paired with a dark-green secondary pill.
- Italicize exactly one accent word per headline and color it kelly green.
- Break long pages with illustrated landscape silhouettes and a repeating "Get a demo" marquee band.
- Draw product UI as flat outlined mockup panels with pill-shaped tags and chat bubbles, floating over illustrations.

**Don't**
- Don't add drop shadows, gradients, or glassmorphism — the language is flat ink-on-paper.
- Don't introduce colors beyond the yellow/green family; even alerts use green checks and outlined circles.
- Don't use photography outside testimonial video thumbnails; illustration carries the brand.
- Don't use sharp-cornered buttons or small radii — buttons are always full pills, cards always generously rounded.
- Don't set headlines in a single uniform style; the roman/italic contrast within one line is core to the voice.
