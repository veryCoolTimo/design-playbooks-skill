## Overview
Near-black developer-tool aesthetic punctuated by candy-pastel color: mint-cyan CTAs, and feature cards filled solid with peach-orange, lavender, and sky-blue holding dark text. Headlines are large, light-weight grotesque with one keyword tinted in a blue/gradient wash. Faint grid and diamond textures sit behind heroes; code blocks, terminal tables, and isometric wireframe cube diagrams reinforce the database-infrastructure subject.

## Layout
Slim sticky top nav: logo left, centered text links (Docs, Pricing, Templates, Blog, Community, About), right side pairs a dark "Star us on GitHub" pill with a mint Sign up button. Heroes are centered, short (headline + one-line subhead + single CTA) over subtle grid texture. Sections alternate: centered H2 intro, then 2-4 column card grids or a full-width dark diagram/table; three-up pastel accent-card rows recur after diagrams. Every marketing page closes with a full-width rounded pastel-gradient CTA panel (peach to lavender to sky) with dark text, then a 5-column dark footer with an "All Systems Operational" status dot. Docs adds a left sidebar tree; generous vertical whitespace throughout.

## Components
Buttons: small-radius (4-6px) compact rectangles; primary is solid mint-cyan #7DEDE8 with black text, secondary is dark fill with 1px gray border; pricing's recommended column swaps to amber #E8B04B fills. Cards: dark #141414-ish panels with 1px low-contrast borders and 10-16px radius (template/blog/tutorial cards carry screenshot or illustration thumbnails on top); accent feature cards use flat pastel fills (orange, lavender, cyan) with dark headings and small "Learn more →" links. Inputs: dark rounded search fields with leading magnifier icon and kbd hint. Also: pill-shaped tag filters on blog, striped dark data tables, accordion FAQ rows with chevrons, amber-outlined highlighted pricing column with "Recommended" badge.

## Signature
- Flat candy-pastel cards (peach-orange / lavender / cyan with dark text) sitting on a pure-black canvas
- Full-width multi-stop pastel gradient CTA band ("Ready to launch?") before the footer on every page
- Mint-cyan primary button paired with amber as the pricing/emphasis secondary
- Big light-weight headlines with a single blue-gradient-tinted keyword, plus a typewriter-cycling word in the home hero
- Dark technical artifacts as decoration: isometric wireframe cubes, SQL code blocks, tenant data tables

## Do / Don't
- Do keep the canvas near-black and reserve color for small solid-fill accent cards and the closing gradient band
- Do give buttons tight small radii and black text on mint or amber fills; keep them compact, not oversized
- Do tint exactly one word of a headline (blue/gradient) and leave the rest white
- Do use real technical artifacts — code snippets, data tables, wireframe cube diagrams — as section illustrations
- Do end pages with the rounded pastel-gradient CTA panel with dark text
- Don't use glassmorphism, neon glows, or heavy drop shadows — surfaces are flat with 1px hairline borders
- Don't put light text on the pastel accent cards; they always carry dark ink
- Don't introduce saturated brand red/green; the palette stays mint, amber, lavender, sky pastels
- Don't round buttons into pills or enlarge radii on small controls; only large CTA panels get 20px+ corners
- Don't brighten body text to pure white; body copy stays muted gray against black
