## Overview
A near-black, space-themed dark-tech language: charcoal canvas with faint nebula glows (violet, teal, amber) bleeding behind heroes and section corners. Elegant editorial serif headlines sit against a cold fintech UI, warmed only by a candle-amber CTA treatment. Product depth comes from embedded dashboard screenshots and floating metal card renders lit like objects in the dark.

## Layout
Single-row top nav: logo left, text links right, amber "Request Access" as the final item (text emphasis, not a filled pill). Heroes are centered: small uppercase or chip eyebrow, large serif headline (2 lines), short muted paragraph, one small CTA button — then a full-width product screenshot or render. Sections alternate centered headers with left-text/right-visual split blocks; the pricing page uses a repeated two-column pattern (left label + description, right zebra-striped fee rows). Feature grids run 3-4 columns of dark tiles (home "bento" area). Generous vertical whitespace between sections; footer is a 4-column sitemap with FDIC/legal fine print and a green "All systems operational" status dot.

## Components
- Buttons: small, compact, slightly rounded rectangles; dark warm-brown translucent fill (#2b2115-ish) with a thin amber border and amber label (#e0a458). No large filled primary buttons anywhere — the CTA whispers.
- Cards: #17171a-ish panels a step lighter than canvas, hairline low-contrast borders, 8-14px radius, no visible drop shadows; many carry an internal radial glow or image.
- Category chips (blog): outlined pill-ish tags color-coded per category — amber (Product Releases), red (Life at Starlight), blue (Company News).
- FAQ accordions: full-width dark rows with a plus icon right, medium radius.
- Pricing rows: alternating darker row stripes with right-aligned values or amber checkmarks.
- Value cards (about): 2x2 grid, each with a small colored rounded-square icon (orange/green/blue/red) and a shared center glow behind the grid.

## Signature
- Serif/grotesque headline duet — flowing editorial serif for heroes, bold uppercase grotesque for interstitial heads, sometimes mixed within one heading ("BACKED BY WORLD-CLASS / INVESTORS & BUILDERS").
- Amber-outline ghost CTA on near-black: the only warm interactive color, reused for nav "Request Access", checkmarks, and links.
- Nebula/starfield ambience: soft multicolor radial glows and faint grid texture behind heroes, dark metal card renders floating in space.
- Color-coded small icons and chips (blue/green/orange/red squares) as the only saturated moments on an otherwise monochrome page.

## Do
- Keep the canvas near-black (#101014 range) and lift cards only one step (#17171a) with hairline borders instead of shadows.
- Use the amber (#e0a458) exclusively for CTAs, key links, and checkmarks; let everything else stay grayscale.
- Set hero headlines in a light editorial serif at large size, with body copy in muted gray grotesque underneath.
- Place a soft radial glow (violet/teal/amber) behind heroes and behind card clusters to break the flat black.
- Show the product via full dashboard screenshots and dramatic 3D card renders rather than abstract illustration.

## Don't
- Don't introduce a large filled solid-color primary button — CTAs here are small, dark, amber-outlined.
- Don't use pure white text blocks; body text is dimmed (#a0a0a0-#d8d8d6) and headings stop short of #fff glare.
- Don't mix in bright saturated backgrounds or light-mode sections; every page stays dark end to end.
- Don't round buttons into pills or oversize the radius — corners are tight on controls, moderate on cards.
- Don't add heavy drop shadows or glassmorphism blur; depth comes from glows and border hairlines.
