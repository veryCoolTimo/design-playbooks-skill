## Overview
A literary, editorial take on the illustration style: warm off-white paper canvas, near-black ink, hand-drawn flat illustrations of stacked books in muted pastels, and a distinctive stencil-cut display face that reads like letterpress printing. Color is scarce and deliberate — one bright spring green (#32e982) for interactive states, soft gradient blobs for artwork — everything else stays black-on-paper. The whole page feels like a small-press book brought to the web.

## Layout
Slim top nav: wordmark left, center text links (Start a Story, Prompts, Library, About, FAQ), Sign Up / Log In pills right, separated by a hairline rule. Hero is centered and text-first: big stencil wordmark, two-line serif-weight subtitle, a large flat illustration (book pile), a short paragraph, then a pill CTA pair (outline + black fill). Sections follow a strong editorial rhythm: left-aligned stencil headings sitting on full-width hairline rules, generous vertical whitespace. The "process" section is a 5-step diagonal cascade — numbered oversized digits with small UI-mockup cards stepping down and to the right. Mid-page a full-bleed two-column split pairs a soft gradient-blob poster with scattered typography against text content. Values are stacked hairline-divided rows (label left, paragraph right). Footer is solid black with an oversized "S-L" monogram.

## Components
Buttons: pills, two variants only — solid black with white text (primary, e.g. "Sign up for free") and thin black outline on canvas with arrow glyphs (secondary, e.g. "Start your own story ↗"). Cards: pale greenish-gray (#f0f1ec) panels with medium rounded corners and thin borders, used as miniature product mockups (a dot-scatter partner picker, sliders with bright green knobs, a text editor with blinking cursor, a generated cover-art preview, a typeset serif story page with highlighted passages). Carousel controls are small outlined pill arrows. No drop shadows anywhere — separation comes from tint shifts and hairlines.

## Signature
- Stencil-cut heavy display type on hairline-ruled section headers — the single strongest identity element
- Flat hand-drawn book-stack illustrations in muted pastel palette with black line details
- Numbered diagonal step cascade using miniature functional-looking UI cards
- One loud color only: electric spring green (#32e982) reserved for slider knobs, cursors, and interaction cues
- Soft airbrushed gradient blobs paired with scattered poetic typography as section art

## Do / Don't
Do:
- Keep the canvas a warm paper off-white and let black do almost all the work; introduce color only through illustration and the single green interaction accent
- Underline sections with full-width hairline rules and set stencil headings flush left on top of them
- Use pill buttons exclusively — black fill for the primary action, thin outline with ↗ arrow for secondary
- Render product features as small self-contained UI vignettes inside pale rounded cards rather than screenshots
- Switch to a classic serif with drop caps whenever showing actual story content

Don't:
- Add drop shadows, gradients on UI chrome, or elevated card effects — the page is entirely flat
- Use the green accent for buttons or headings; it belongs only to small interactive details (knobs, cursor, highlights)
- Center-align section headings below the hero — the editorial left-rule pattern carries the page
- Replace the stencil display face with a plain geometric sans; the letterpress character is the brand
- Introduce photography — all imagery is flat illustration or soft gradient poster art
