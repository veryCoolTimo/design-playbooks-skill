## Overview
Playful Memphis-meets-Bauhaus SaaS design: flat geometric shapes (circles, arches, diamonds, squiggly dashed paths) and cartoonish 3D character eyes/hands scattered over cream and white canvases. A single saturated cobalt-royal blue does all the interactive heavy lifting, with yellow, green, and pink as decorative confetti. Product screenshots ground the whimsy in real UI.

## Layout
Sticky-feel top nav on white: logo left, center links (Blog, Release notes, Integrations, Pricing), Log in + pill CTA right. Heroes are centered stacks — big two-line headline, short subhead, one or two pill buttons — set on either a cream illustrated collage (home) or a soft lavender-gradient band with faint dashed loop motifs (blog, integrations, release notes). Sections alternate white and cream backgrounds; content sits in a narrow centered column with generous vertical whitespace. Cards go 3-across. Every page ends with the same big blue rounded CTA panel decorated with dashed paths, a yellow dot, a green diamond, and a pink toggle, followed by a light-gray rounded footer block.

## Components
Buttons: fully pill-shaped; primary is solid cobalt blue (#3D3DE2) with white text, secondary is white/transparent with a thin blue or white outline. On blue panels the primary inverts to white with blue text. Cards: light-gray (#F5F5F7) rounded rectangles with centered brand logos, no shadow, tiny pill category badges (pink "Source", lavender "Collaboration"). Pricing tiers: white cards with a hairline border and a pastel header band per tier (mint, pink, periwinkle); comparison table uses hairline row dividers and colored checkmarks. Accordion rows (feedback lifecycle) are bordered rounded bars with small multicolor ring icons. A pill segmented toggle ("Without Cycle / With Cycle") and a blue billing switch appear as controls. Shadows are essentially absent — flat color and borders carry all depth.

## Signature
- Cobalt blue (#3D3DE2) as the sole functional color against cream (#FAF3EC) and white, with yellow/green/pink strictly decorative
- Googly cartoon eyes, 3D hands, and flat Memphis shapes (arches, diamonds, pencils, dashed squiggle paths) as recurring mascot decoration
- The repeated end-of-page blue rounded billboard CTA with dashed-line doodles and a pink toggle overlapping its corner
- Pastel color-coding per tier/category (mint, pink, periwinkle headers and badges) on otherwise flat gray-bordered cards

## Do
- Keep one saturated blue for all CTAs and links; let every other color be non-interactive decoration
- Use pill buttons everywhere, pairing one solid and one outlined per hero
- Alternate cream and white section backgrounds; end pages with the blue rounded CTA billboard
- Decorate empty space with flat geometric shapes and dashed connector paths, never with gradients or photos
- Show real product screenshots inside rounded frames to offset the playfulness

## Don't
- Don't add drop shadows or glassmorphism — surfaces are flat with hairline borders
- Don't use dark sections; the darkest large surface is the cobalt CTA panel
- Don't introduce a second interactive color — yellow/green/pink never appear on buttons
- Don't use serif or condensed display type; headlines stay bold geometric sans, tightly tracked
- Don't square off buttons or over-round cards — pills for controls, ~12px for cards
