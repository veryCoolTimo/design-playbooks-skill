## Overview
Warm minimal-light marketing site for a macOS design tool. Near-white gray canvas, near-black grotesque type, and one loud vermilion-orange action color, garnished with hand-drawn script words, scribble underlines, and sticker-like flourishes that signal "made by designers." Large floating app screenshots and bento grids of real in-app UI crops do most of the selling; the chrome around them stays quiet.

## Layout
Slim sticky nav: logo left, dropdown menus, "Sign in" text link plus a black pill CTA right. Home hero is asymmetric — huge left-aligned display heading ("Designers, welcome home.") with a small right-aligned intro paragraph and CTA — sitting on a soft pink-lilac gradient with a massive tilted app screenshot bleeding down into the page. Sections open with small outlined uppercase eyebrow chips (DESIGN, PROTOTYPE, COLLABORATE), a two-line bold heading, then a bento mosaic of feature tiles mixing UI crops, gradients, and short captions. Features page alternates centered sections with isometric 3D icons and drops one full-bleed dark "Made for macOS" band with two-column checkmark lists. Pricing: centered title, two bordered plan cards side by side, a dashed-border business card below, then a very long comparison table and FAQ accordions. Blog runs a distinct sub-brand ("beyond the canvas"): light-blue hero band with an image carousel, monospaced headlines with a highlighted first word, and a two-column feed of small-thumbnail article rows. Generous whitespace throughout; content column stays narrow and centered mid-page.

## Components
Buttons: two co-existing primaries — a black pill ("Get started", nav and hero; home hero version has a pink glow outline ring) and a vermilion-orange filled rounded rectangle (#F1692B, "Get started for free" on pricing); secondary is a thin-bordered white pill ("Get in touch"). Blog buttons are near-square black blocks with mono text. Cards: white fill, 1px light-gray border, 8-14px radius, essentially shadowless; pricing plan cards use a thin orange outline, the business tier a dashed orange border. Checkmarks are filled orange circles (standard plan) vs black circles (Mac license). Tag chips are small outlined rectangles with mono text. Inputs: plain bordered fields with an adjacent small filled "Sign up" button. FAQ rows are flat with plus toggles and hairline dividers.

## Signature
- One saturated vermilion-orange accent doing all the pointing — CTA fills, plan borders, checkmarks, a single highlighted word ("great", "today!") — on an otherwise gray/black/white page
- Hand-drawn energy layered onto minimalism: marker-script words, scribble boxes and underlines, sticker-like isometric icons, the diamond logo used as a section ornament
- Oversized, high-fidelity macOS app screenshots floating on soft pastel gradients as hero imagery
- Blog as a typographic costume change: monospaced headlines, highlight-swatch on the opening word, pale blue band, boxy black buttons

## Do / Don't
**Do**
- Keep the canvas near-white (#FAFAFA) and reserve #F1692B for actions and single emphasized words; everything else stays near-black or gray
- Sell with real product UI: big tilted screenshots in heroes, cropped in-app details inside bento tiles
- Mix a black pill CTA (nav/hero) with orange filled CTAs on conversion moments like pricing
- Add one hand-drawn touch per major section — a script word, scribble underline, or dashed border — not more
- Open sections with small outlined uppercase eyebrow chips before the bold heading

**Don't**
- Don't add drop shadows or heavy elevation to cards — this system is borders and whitespace, not depth
- Don't use blue or multi-hue accents in the marketing chrome; color variety lives only inside product screenshots and illustrations
- Don't let the mono typography leak out of the blog sub-brand into product/pricing pages
- Don't center the home hero — its identity is the huge left-set headline against a small right-set paragraph
- Don't dilute the orange with tints or gradients on buttons; it appears flat and full-strength or not at all
