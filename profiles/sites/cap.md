## Overview
Minimal-light SaaS design anchored by one motif: a photographic blue sky with white clouds. The home hero is fully immersed in sky imagery with white text; interior pages flip to a white canvas with near-black headings and slate body text, keeping the sky only in illustration cards, blog thumbnails, and CTA banners. Everything else is restrained — hairline borders, faint gray panels, generous air — so the periwinkle-blue brand color and cloud photography carry the entire personality.

## Layout
Sticky centered top nav: logo left, plain text links center, GitHub pill + dark "Login" pill (or blue "Download App" pill) right; nav sits on white with a hairline bottom border on interior pages, transparent over the sky hero on home. Heroes are center-aligned: large heading, 1-2 line muted subhead, a horizontal pair of pill buttons (white secondary + blue primary). A handwritten-script eyebrow ("simple plans", "powerful features", "you're in great company") floats above some section headings. Sections run on a wide centered container (~1200px) with big vertical gaps; social proof is a single row of grayscale logos (Tesla, Microsoft, Coinbase, IBM, Dropbox). Feature grids mix card widths (1 + 2-wide bento rows); testimonials use a 3-column masonry of quote cards. Every page ends with a rounded sky-photo CTA banner followed by a large light-gray rounded footer block with 3-4 link columns plus an "Additional Links" SEO row.

## Components
Buttons: fully pill-shaped, small-to-medium size; primary is periwinkle blue (#4785ff) with a subtle lighter-top gradient and white label; secondary is white with hairline gray border and dark text; a solid near-black pill is used for Login. Cards: white fill, 1px light-gray border (#e5e9ef-ish), 12-18px radius, essentially shadowless; testimonial and blog cards follow the same recipe with image tops on blog. Pricing tiers differentiate by fill: pale gray (free), solid brand blue (Pro), near-navy black (Custom), each keeping white pill CTAs inside. Tags/chips are tiny gray-bordered pills. Comparison table uses filled blue check circles for the brand column, gray checks/crosses for competitors. Product mockups sit in rounded frames with soft diffuse shadows.

## Signature
- Photographic blue-sky-and-clouds imagery as the recurring brand texture: full hero on home, rounded CTA banners, feature tiles, blog covers.
- Periwinkle blue (#4785ff) as the single accent against otherwise gray/white UI; the ring-shaped logo mark echoed as a page watermark (giant faint arcs framing About/Product heroes).
- Handwritten script micro-labels above section headings, contrasting the clean grotesque type.
- Pill buttons everywhere paired with hairline-bordered, shadowless white cards.

## Do / Don't
Do:
- Use the sky/cloud photograph treatment for heroes and end-of-page CTA banners, always inside large-radius rounded containers on interior pages.
- Keep cards white with 1px light-gray borders and 12-18px radius; let whitespace, not shadows, do the separation.
- Make every button a pill; pair one blue gradient primary with one white bordered secondary.
- Add the handwritten script eyebrow above major section headings for a personal, indie touch.
- Close pages with the light-gray rounded footer panel containing link columns and an SEO "Additional Links" block.

Don't:
- Don't introduce warm accent colors or multi-color gradients; the palette is strictly blue + grayscale (navy black allowed for a premium tier/Login).
- Don't use heavy drop shadows or dark borders on cards — elevation stays near zero.
- Don't set body copy in pure black; it is always muted slate/gray against white.
- Don't square off buttons or use small-radius rectangles for CTAs; pill shape is non-negotiable.
- Don't crowd sections; each block gets generous vertical padding and a single clear focal element.
