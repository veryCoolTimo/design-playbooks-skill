## Overview
Monochrome Swedish-modernist fintech: a pure #000000 top half with white regular-weight grotesque type set very large, flipping to pure white mid-page sections. Color is rationed to one lime-yellow (#ECE87E) used only inside isometric product illustrations and a warm beige (#EEEBE5) panel behind the Visa logo. Photography (reeded-glass office shots, product-in-hand, laptop close-ups) carries all remaining warmth; the UI itself is strictly black/white.

## Layout
Slim one-row nav on black: logo left, two product links (Lender/Merchant) beside it, secondary links right, single white pill CTA at far right. Hero is a split composition — headline and copy on solid black left, full-bleed textured photo bleeding off the right edge and top-cropped below the nav. Long scroll alternates full-width black and white bands; two-column rhythm throughout (illustration/photo on one side, editorial text block on the other, sides swapping section to section). "Why choose" section uses a 2x2 grid of thin-bordered cards with black wireframe isometric icons. Footer is a 3-up row of slate-gray (#494D50) link cards (Press / IR / Career) plus a fourth contact card, over a white sitemap footer. Whitespace is generous; paragraphs are long and unapologetically editorial.

## Components
Buttons: pill-shaped, low-key and small relative to the huge type; white fill with black label on dark ("Get in touch", "Contact us"), black-outlined white pill on light sections ("For lenders", "For merchants"). Cards: white, hairline dark border, sharp or barely-rounded corners, no shadows; each holds a line-art isometric illustration, a small black pill tag as title, and a one-line caption. Footer cards are flat solid gray blocks with white text and an arrow link. Logo row ("Trusted by") shows partner marks at full contrast on white. No inputs visible.

## Signature
- Regular-weight (not bold) grotesque headlines at enormous scale on pure black — hierarchy through size and space, not weight
- One accent only: lime-yellow #ECE87E, confined to isometric layered-stack product diagrams, never on UI controls
- Black wireframe isometric illustrations (stacks, cubes, timers) inside hairline-bordered white cards
- Full-bleed reeded-glass/office photography abutting solid black panels with a hard vertical seam

## Do / Don't
Do:
- Set headlines in a regular-weight neo-grotesque at 60-90px; keep buttons small pills so type dominates
- Alternate full-width pure-black and pure-white sections; keep each section strictly two-column
- Reserve #ECE87E for illustration fills and diagram highlights only; use #EEEBE5 beige as an occasional quiet panel
- Draw feature cards as white boxes with 1px dark borders, sharp corners, line-art isometric icons, and pill-shaped black tag labels
- Let photos run full-bleed to the viewport edge against a solid black column

Don't:
- Don't put the yellow on buttons, links, or text — every CTA on this site is white-or-black pill only
- Don't bold the headlines or add gradients/glows; the dark-tech feel comes from flat #000000, not effects
- Don't round card corners or add drop shadows — only buttons and tag chips are pill/rounded
- Don't introduce a third hue; the palette is black, white, one yellow, one beige, one slate gray
- Don't shorten copy into punchy fragments — the voice is long-form editorial paragraphs beside visuals
