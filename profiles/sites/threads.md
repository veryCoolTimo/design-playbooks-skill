## Overview
A dark-neon SaaS language: near-black violet canvas washed with large purple-to-magenta gradient glows, white heavy geometric headlines, and product screenshots framed inside multicolor gradient halos. Neon accents (lime, cyan, orange) are used sparingly for plan tiers and playful moments, while the core brand stays firmly in violet. One page (the "threads84" mini-game) flips to a light retro-hardware scene, showing the brand tolerates a playful light-mode detour.

## Layout
Floating pill-shaped dark nav bar with centered wordmark logo, links left, Log In + filled purple CTA right. Heroes are centered: huge bold headline, one-line subhead, single glowing CTA; the home hero sits under a bright purple trapezoid gradient beaming down from the top edge. Sections alternate centered intro text with large product-screenshot panels; the gallery page runs a strict 3-column card grid. Generous vertical whitespace between sections; content column stays narrow and centered. Pricing uses a 3-up plan card row, wide banner cards (Enterprise/Community), then a long dark comparison table with repeated CTA header rows.

## Components
Buttons: filled violet (#6D4AEF) rounded-rect CTAs ~10-12px radius; hero CTAs get an outer glow ring/border in lighter purple. Secondary buttons are white or ghost on dark. Pricing tier buttons are color-coded per plan (white, neon green, cyan, orange). Cards: dark charcoal (#1C1826-ish) rounded 16px+ panels; gallery cards top a screenshot on a vivid multicolor gradient backdrop and end with a thin caption strip separated by a hairline border. Feature blocks use full-bleed gradient panels (red-orange, deep purple). Inputs: dark rounded email field paired inline with a small purple submit button. Testimonial card is a glowing periwinkle rounded slab with dark text.

## Signature
- Purple gradient "spotlight" trapezoid pouring from the top of the page onto a near-black canvas.
- Product screenshots framed on rainbow-gradient halo mats inside dark cards.
- Heavy rounded geometric headline face, always white, tightly set, centered.
- Neon per-plan color coding (green/cyan/orange) against otherwise monochrome violet UI.
- Checkerboard-dot logo mark and lowercase "threads" wordmark.

## Do
- Keep the canvas near-black violet and let one large purple gradient glow anchor each hero.
- Frame every product screenshot on a saturated multicolor gradient mat inside a dark rounded card.
- Reserve neon lime/cyan/orange for differentiation (pricing tiers, playful moments), not for body UI.
- Center headlines and CTAs; keep one glowing violet button per hero.
- Use thin hairline dividers and caption strips to close cards.

## Don't
- Don't use pure black or pure gray backgrounds — the dark always leans violet.
- Don't spread neon accents across body text or links; ink stays muted lavender-gray.
- Don't give buttons and cards the same radius — buttons stay ~10-12px, cards go 16px+.
- Don't use thin or light-weight display type; headlines are always heavy and tightly tracked.
- Don't left-align hero content; the whole marketing rhythm is center-axis.
