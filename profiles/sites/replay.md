## Overview
A light, white-canvas developer-tool site energized by hot-pink/magenta gradients and a single dark synthwave set piece: the hero video panel with retro-chrome "Bugslayer" arcade art on a purple starfield. Everything outside that panel is restrained — gray body text, generous whitespace, small pink CTAs — so the neon-retro moment reads as the brand's personality rather than the whole theme. Note: despite the "dark-neon" tag, the page itself is light; the dark neon lives inside embedded media and imagery.

## Layout
Slim top nav: logo left, five small text links center-right, dark navy pill "Login" at far right; faint diagonal constellation lines decorate the hero's upper-right corner. Hero is left-aligned text (two-line headline, second line in pink) with a short paragraph and one small pink pill CTA, followed by a full-width rounded dark video panel. Sections alternate: white feature-pair rows with a vertical timeline connector, a centered "Developer Tools" section with tabbed screenshots on pink-peach gradient washes, a light-gray band holding a 2x2 grid of white feature cards, then a full-bleed pink-gradient testimonial band, logo grid, security icon-card row, and a pink-gradient footer CTA banner above a dark navy footer. Rhythm is airy — large vertical padding between every section.

## Components
Buttons: small pill-shaped, solid pink (#f02d5e) with white text for primary actions; dark navy pill for Login; white/outline pill variants on the pink bands. Cards: white, medium-rounded corners, hairline light borders, essentially shadowless; feature cards contain miniature UI vignettes (comment rows, bar charts, chat bubbles) with tiny pink status pills and play buttons. Tabs: small uppercase labels with a pink underline/active state. Screenshots sit on soft pink-to-peach gradient mats with rounded corners. Footer CTA is a full-width rounded pink gradient banner with a dark pill button.

## Signature
- One dark retro-synthwave hero panel (chrome 3D lettering, purple starfield, hoverboard) embedded in an otherwise clean white page.
- Hot pink #f02d5e as the single functional color: CTAs, headline highlights, tab underlines, status pills, section bands.
- Pink-to-peach gradient mats and full-bleed gradient bands used as the only large color surfaces.
- Small pill buttons everywhere — CTAs stay compact and lowercase-quiet rather than oversized.

## Do / Don't
**Do**
- Keep the canvas white/light-gray and concentrate saturated color into a few rounded gradient panels and bands.
- Use pink for every interactive or emphasized element (CTA fills, headline second lines, active tab underlines, badges) so it reads as one brand color.
- Render feature cards as white, hairline-bordered containers with miniature product UI inside, not stock illustration.
- Keep buttons small pill shapes; pair one pink primary with a dark navy secondary.
- Let the playful retro artwork live inside a contained, rounded dark media panel.

**Don't**
- Don't make the whole page dark — the neon aesthetic here is a contained accent, not the theme.
- Don't introduce blues/greens as UI colors; purple appears only inside the hero artwork.
- Don't use heavy drop shadows — depth comes from gradient mats and hairline borders.
- Don't oversize CTAs or use sharp-cornered buttons; everything interactive is a compact pill.
- Don't crowd sections; the design depends on wide vertical whitespace between bands.
