## Overview
Notion's marketing site is a near-monochrome, paper-like canvas: pure white background, near-black ink, and black CTA buttons, warmed by light-gray (#F6F5F4) section cards. Color arrives sparingly and semantically — purple for AI, coral-red for wikis, pastel chips on Calendar — while hand-drawn black line-art figures give the otherwise austere layout personality. Real product UI, framed in bordered rounded cards, does most of the persuading.

## Layout
Slim single-row top nav: logo left, dropdown links, right-aligned "Request a demo · Log in" plus a black pill-ish CTA; product pages add a second sticky sub-nav bar with a per-product CTA. Heroes are either centered (headline, one-line subhead, black CTA, giant product screenshot below) or split two-column with a device mockup. Sections alternate white background with full-width or bento-grid gray rounded cards; 2-4 column feature-card grids are the dominant rhythm. Ink-drawn characters sit beside or above section headings as visual punctuation. Pages end with a logo strip or testimonials, an FAQ accordion, a final centered CTA with illustration, and a dense five-column footer.

## Components
Primary buttons: solid black (#191918) fill, white text, small 4-6px radius, sometimes with a leading icon (Apple App Store) or trailing arrow. Secondary buttons: light-gray fill or thin-bordered white, same shape. Cards: #F6F5F4 fill, 8-12px radius, no shadow, no border — flat panels holding an icon, bold micro-heading, and 2-3 lines of small gray text. Product screenshots sit in white cards with a hairline border and very soft shadow. FAQ accordions are hairline-divided rows with plus icons. Pricing uses four flat columns, a "Most popular" tag, and check-mark feature lists in a hairline table. Small colored tag/chip elements (purple "New", pastel labels) appear inline.

## Signature
- Hand-drawn black ink doodle illustrations (people reading, writing, collaborating) scattered through every page, occasionally with one flat accent color.
- Black-on-white restraint: primary CTA is pure black, links are the only persistent blue (#2383E2).
- Per-product accent theming on a shared monochrome shell — purple for AI, coral-red for Wikis/Docs, pastel multicolor for Calendar.
- Serif pull quotes with customer logos breaking the sans-serif rhythm; emoji used as functional icons inside embedded product UI.

## Do
- Keep the base strictly black/white/warm-gray; introduce at most one accent color per page, tied to meaning.
- Use flat #F6F5F4 rounded cards for feature grids — icon, bold short title, small muted body, no shadows or borders.
- Show real product UI large and often, framed by a hairline border and gentle shadow.
- Punctuate sections with playful line-art illustrations rather than stock photos or gradients.
- Set headlines bold and tight in a grotesque sans; drop to a classic serif only for testimonial quotes.

## Don't
- Don't use a colored primary button — CTAs are solid black everywhere, even next to purple AI branding.
- Don't add drop shadows, gradients, or glassmorphism to cards; surfaces stay flat and matte.
- Don't use large corner radii or pill buttons; everything sits at small-to-medium radius.
- Don't crowd the canvas — sections breathe with generous white padding between gray panels.
- Don't decorate with abstract 3D or photographic hero art; the only imagery is product UI, ink doodles, and occasional documentary-style community photos.
