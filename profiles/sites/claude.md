## Overview
An editorial, literary take on an AI product site: a warm cream page floor instead of the category's usual cool white, regular-weight slab-serif headlines over humanist sans body, and a muted coral as the sole brand voltage. Pages alternate between cream sections, slightly deeper cream cards, and near-black warm-dark panels that carry real product chrome (code editors, terminals, model cards). Depth comes from these surface swaps, almost never from shadows.

## Layout
Cream top nav (64px) with the black radial-spike mark plus wordmark left, horizontal menu, and a coral "Try Claude" button right. Hero is a 6/6 split — serif headline and buttons left, an illustration or dark code-editor mockup card right. Sections repeat on a 96px vertical rhythm inside a ~1200px container; feature grids run 3-up, connector tiles 4-6-up, pricing 3-4-up. The pacing rule: no two adjacent bands share a surface tone (cream, cream card, dark mockup, coral callout, dark footer).

## Components
Primary buttons: solid coral #cc785c, white 14px/500 sans label, 8px radius, 40px tall; press darkens to #a9583e. Secondary buttons are cream with a 1px hairline (#e6dfd8); on dark panels they stay dark (#252320) rather than inverting to light. Cards are flat color blocks — cream #efe9de feature cards or dark #181715 mockup cards at 12px radius with 32px padding, no shadows. Inputs: cream fill, hairline border, 8px radius, coral border plus soft coral ring on focus. Badges are pills; inline text links render in coral. Featured pricing tier signals emphasis by flipping to the dark surface.

## Signature
- Cream #faf9f5 + coral #cc785c pairing — deliberately warm where competitor AI brands go blue/slate
- Serif display at weight 400 with negative tracking; emphasis comes from size, never boldness
- Dark warm-black panels showing genuine product UI (syntax-highlighted code, line numbers, terminals) instead of abstract marketing art
- The black four-spoke asterisk mark prefixing the wordmark and reappearing as an inline content marker

## Do / Don't
Do:
- Keep the page floor tinted cream; the warmth is the identity
- Set every headline in the serif at 400 with tight negative tracking, body in humanist sans
- Alternate surface modes band by band — cream, card-cream, dark, coral — as the pacing device
- Show real code/product chrome inside dark 12px-radius cards rather than illustrating it
- Confine coral to primary CTAs, inline links, and occasional full-bleed callout bands

Don't:
- Swap in pure white or cool gray backgrounds
- Bold the display serif or replace it with a sans headline
- Introduce blue/cyan accents or a fourth surface color (no purple/green sections)
- Scatter coral across minor UI elements — it stays scarce outside CTAs and callouts
- Lean on drop shadows for depth; contrast between cream and dark surfaces does that work
