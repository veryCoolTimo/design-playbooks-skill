## Overview
Warm minimal-light system on a pale cream-yellow canvas with near-black grotesque headlines, muted gray body text, and a single amber-orange CTA color. Grainy, film-textured mountain photography and hairline orange line-drawings (loops and orbits) give an analog, editorial feel to an otherwise strict tech layout. Parts of display headlines are rendered with an eroded/glitch texture, as if half-printed.

## Layout
Sticky-free top nav: logo left, uppercase text links (DOCS, BLOG, PRICING, ABOUT US) center-left, "APP" plus solid orange "GET A DEMO" pill-less button right. Heroes are left-aligned type blocks with generous top whitespace; home pairs a large H1 with a full-width textured landscape photo containing an embedded dark code window. Sections alternate: left-column section label (small orange square + monospace uppercase tag like "FAQS") with heading, content in the right column — the FAQ uses this split-grid with hairline-divided accordion rows. Repeating closer on every page: "Get started in minutes" heading with the word "minutes" in orange, dual CTAs, then a full-width panoramic mountain photo above the footer. Blog uses a large featured card (image left, light gray text panel right) plus hairline-divided list rows with small textured thumbnails.

## Components
Buttons: solid amber-orange fill, black uppercase label, small 2-4px radius, often with a diagonal arrow glyph; secondary buttons are light gray fill, same shape; pricing cards use full-width black buttons with white text (inverted to off-white on the black card). No shadows anywhere. Cards: flat warm-gray (#E2E1D5) rectangles with square corners; one pricing card inverted to near-black for emphasis; feature tiles are gray blocks with thin-line icons and monospace uppercase captions plus index numbers. Accordions: hairline top borders, plus-icon on the right. Tags: tiny black rectangles with white monospace uppercase text (MODELS, INFRA, NEWS). Toggle (MODELS/REPOS): paired rectangular buttons, active one filled orange.

## Signature
- Cream-yellow canvas + one amber accent + near-black type: essentially a three-color system
- Grainy vintage mountain-range photography as recurring hero, section, and pre-footer imagery
- Eroded/half-printed glitch texture applied to select words in display headlines (and one orange word per closing headline)
- Thin freehand orange line loops drifting across page backgrounds
- Monospace uppercase micro-labels with orange square bullets and index numbers marking sections

## Do
- Keep the palette to cream canvas, black/gray type, and orange reserved for CTAs, section-tag bullets, and single highlighted headline words
- Use flat, square-cornered gray or black panels for cards and pricing; invert one card to black for emphasis
- Apply film grain/texture to all photography and keep imagery in the warm mountain-landscape family
- Separate list content (FAQs, blog posts) with hairlines, not boxes or shadows
- Pair every section heading with a monospace uppercase label and small orange marker

## Don't
- Don't add drop shadows, gradients, or glassmorphism — everything here is flat ink on paper
- Don't round cards or use pill buttons; radii stay at a few pixels on buttons only
- Don't introduce blues, greens, or a second accent color; the orange carries all emphasis
- Don't use clean, saturated stock photos — untextured imagery breaks the analog print feel
- Don't center-align hero copy; headings and text blocks sit left on a wide whitespace field
