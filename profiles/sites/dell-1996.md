## Overview
A 1996 mail-order-catalog aesthetic translated to HTML tables: the whole page lives inside a thick literal black border, and each product family gets its own flat pastel color block. Depth comes exclusively from 1px hairlines and hand-drawn GIF bevels — no gradients, no soft shadows, no rounded corners. Type inverts the modern rule: heavy all-caps sans for headings and UI, default serif for every paragraph.

## Layout
Fixed ~760px table layout (800x600-era) wrapped in an ~8px solid black page frame. A black top banner carries the headline, a yellow "BUY a DELL" sticker, and a red 1-800 phone number pinned top-right on every page. Homepage splits into a ~28% left rail (icon-link grid plus one red CTA panel) and a ~72% right column stacking product "ribbon cards" with roughly 40px between stacks. Density is high; breathing room exists only inside each card (white title bar over tinted body). Footer is a 4-icon nav row joined by a thin green rule, with blue underlined links and serif small print.

## Components
Ribbon card: white all-caps Helvetica Bold title bar with a 1px black bottom rule, above a flat tinted body (one of eight catalog tints — sage, salmon, peach, lime, sky, steel, periwinkle, olive) holding Times Roman copy, with a beveled product photo notched into the right ~25% of the row. Buttons: square, 1px black border, bold 12px Helvetica caps — black fill/white text for primary, white fill/black text for secondary. Inputs: white, 1px black border, serif text, square. Stickers: yellow "NEW!" bursts rotated ~15 degrees and a yellow buy tab, both hard-beveled; a circular red PC Magazine seal is the lone round element. Links are classic Netscape blue (#0000ee) with underlines. No hover states documented.

## Signature
- The literal black frame around the entire viewport — the page reads as a bordered printed brochure
- Eight-tint flat color coding, one pastel per product line, always paired with 1px black hairlines
- Arial Black 900 all-caps eyebrows on tinted blocks versus Times Roman serif body — sans-for-UI, serif-for-copy inversion
- Red used only twice per page: one CTA panel and the phone number; yellow stickers and beveled GIF photos supply the rest of the noise

## Do / Don't
Do:
- Keep the black page frame at every breakpoint (compress to 2-4px on mobile, never remove)
- Reserve #e91d2a for a single red CTA panel plus the phone callout — nothing decorative
- Assign one catalog tint per product family and reuse it consistently across that line
- Set all display headings in Arial Black 900 caps and all body copy in Times Roman 14
- Fake depth with hard 1px borders and crisp hard-offset bevels (e.g., drop-shadow(2px 2px 0 #000))

Don't:
- Add any radius to buttons, cards, or inputs — only the award seal is round
- Use soft shadows, gradients, or translucency; every fill is flat and opaque
- Swap the serif body for a sans or webfont
- Invent colors beyond the eight tints plus red, sticker yellow, purple stripe, and link blue
- Place two red CTA panels on one page, or round/clip the product photos — they stay hard rectangles notched into the card edge
