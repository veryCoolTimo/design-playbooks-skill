## Overview
A near-monochrome, editorial landing page: warm light-gray canvas, black ink, serif headlines, and hand-drawn black-and-white sticker-collage illustrations punctuated by small full-color photo cutouts of people. Color is rationed to the tiny gradient logo mark, company logos, and the photos — everything else is grayscale. The whole page reads like a printed zine translated to SaaS.

## Layout
Minimal top bar: small logo left, a single pill "Log in" button right — no nav links at all. Hero is fully centered: serif H1, two short sans-serif subline tiers ("By invitation only." / "$20/month"), then a large collage illustration instead of a product shot; notably there is no signup CTA button. Below: a centered serif section heading over a 3-column icon+text feature row, then a centered logo wall in two rows. Mid-page alternates full-width bands (light gray #EFEFEE vs white) each holding a 2-column split — serif heading + short paragraphs on one side, collage illustration on the other, sides swapping each band. Footer is sparse: logo, one link column, legal line. Generous vertical whitespace throughout; narrow text measure (~420-520px paragraphs).

## Components
- Button: one pill, off-white fill (#FBFBFB) with a hairline light-gray border and dark text; no colored or filled CTA anywhere on the page.
- Feature items: bare icon-over-text stacks — chunky black doodle icons (~64px), bold sans title, regular gray-leaning body; no card container, border, or shadow.
- Illustrations: layered black-and-white browser-window/sticker doodles with hexagon- and polygon-masked color photos of people tucked between layers.
- Logo wall: full-color brand marks at small size on white, no grayscale filter.
- No inputs, no boxed cards, no visible shadows anywhere.

## Signature
- Black-and-white doodle collages (windows, cursors, badges, keyboard keys) with color photo cutouts as the only imagery system.
- Serif display headlines against a grayscale, near-empty page — editorial rather than techy.
- Deliberate scarcity: single Log in pill, "By invitation only." replacing the expected signup CTA.
- Alternating gray/white full-width bands with swapped text/illustration columns as the entire section rhythm.

## Do / Don't
**Do:**
- Keep the page grayscale; let color enter only through photos, third-party logos, and the small logo mark.
- Use a serif for every heading (hero and section) and a small neutral sans for all body and UI text.
- Build imagery as layered black-and-white doodle collages with polygon-masked photo cutouts, not screenshots or 3D renders.
- Alternate light-gray and white full-width bands, swapping the text/illustration sides each section.
- Underplay the CTA: one small pill button in the header; let scarcity copy ("By invitation only") do the selling.

**Don't:**
- Add a filled, colored primary button — nothing on the page uses a saturated fill.
- Wrap features in cards, borders, or shadows; content sits directly on the band.
- Add a multi-link navigation bar or sticky mega-header; the header carries only logo + one button.
- Use gradients, glassmorphism, or decorative background shapes beyond the tiny scattered dot accents.
- Set headlines in a bold grotesque — the serif/sans split is the core typographic identity.
