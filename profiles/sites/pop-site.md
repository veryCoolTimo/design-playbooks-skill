## Overview
Ultra-clean minimal-light SaaS landing built almost entirely from white space, near-black grotesque type, and floating iPhone mockups with soft drop shadows. One saturated blue is reserved for the single repeating CTA ("Claim your pop.site"), while pale blue-lavender gradient washes warm up alternating sections. A full-bleed near-black testimonial band provides the only hard contrast break.

## Layout
Slim top nav: italic wordmark left, plain text links right ("Blog" gets a small blue "New" pill), no nav border beyond a hairline. Hero is centered: one-line H1, claim-your-URL input + pill CTA directly beneath, then a fanned row of five phone mockups. Sections alternate: centered heading (small logo chip above it) → repeated URL-claim bar → visual proof (phone collage, scattered polaroid-style cards, 3-column feature card grid). Rhythm is generous — huge vertical padding, content column ~640-760px for text, wider for mockup collages. Blog is a single-column list: thumbnail left, title/date center, gray "Pro Tips" tag right. Footer is sparse 4-column text links on the light canvas.

## Components
- Buttons: solid #3B7BF7 pill, white 14px text, no border or shadow; inside mockups CTAs are black or white pills.
- URL input: white pill split field — gray placeholder "yourname" + fixed ".pop.site" suffix cell, 1px light-gray border, paired inline with the blue pill CTA.
- Cards: white, 16-24px radius, hairline border or none, very soft diffuse shadow; feature cards hold an angled phone photo, bold title, 2-line gray description.
- Phone mockups: realistic device frames, slightly rotated, deep soft shadows, layered/overlapping.
- Tags: small rounded-rect chips — blue "New", light-gray "Pro Tips", "No Credit Card Required" line with icon.

## Signature
- The claim-your-URL bar (yourname · .pop.site · blue pill button) repeated in nearly every section as the sole conversion device.
- Grotesque headlines with one word set in serif italic ("Start making *your* personal site now.", "The *easiest* way…").
- Fanned/scattered iPhone mockup collages and loose polaroid-like section cards on soft blue-lavender gradient washes.
- Strict one-accent discipline: everything grayscale except the single blue.

## Do / Don't
**Do**
- Keep the canvas white-to-pale-blue and reserve #3B7BF7 exclusively for the primary CTA and "New" badges.
- Repeat the same inline input+CTA pattern at every section break instead of varying CTA designs.
- Use pill shapes for all interactive elements and 16px+ radii on cards.
- Italicize one emphasized word per headline in a serif face; keep the rest in tight grotesque sans.
- Sell with realistic, slightly rotated device mockups and soft, large-blur shadows.

**Don't**
- Don't introduce a second accent hue — the palette is blue + grayscale only.
- Don't add heavy borders, hard shadows, or filled section backgrounds beyond the pale gradient and the single black band.
- Don't left-align hero or section headings; the marketing layout is consistently centered.
- Don't use icon-heavy feature grids — features are shown as photographed phone screens, not icon tiles.
- Don't crowd sections; vertical padding is extreme and each section carries one idea.
