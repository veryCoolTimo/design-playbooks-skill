## Overview
A storybook-illustrated landing page: the entire scroll is one continuous hand-painted world that moves from a Ghibli-style daytime sky with cumulus clouds, through watercolor meadows, into a deep-blue starry night, and back to a warm paper-cream ground with sunflowers at the footer. Product UI screenshots and functional elements (email capture, testimonial cards) float on top of the painted scenery. Serif headlines set against the illustration give it an editorial, human, "handcrafted" tone rather than a tech-SaaS one.

## Layout
Minimal nav: just a small logo dot + wordmark top-left, no menu bar. Hero is a centered stack — serif headline ("For doers & dreamers"), two lines of sans subcopy, and an email + Apply capture pill — with a paper-airplane illustration off to the right. Sections alternate between full-bleed illustrated backdrops (sky, meadow, night sky) and quiet cream paper stretches; each section is a single centered idea: one short serif heading plus one large product screenshot. Testimonials break the single-column rhythm with a 3-column masonry grid of pastel quote cards. Very generous vertical whitespace between sections; content column stays narrow and centered throughout. Page closes symmetrically with the same email/Apply capture and a grass-and-sunflowers footer strip.

## Components
- Primary CTA: bright blue (#4585ed) pill button with white sans label ("Apply"), fused to the right end of a pill email input with cream fill (#f9f5f2), thin light border, and soft drop shadow.
- Testimonial cards: pastel fills (mint, peach, lavender) with slightly darker 1px borders in the same hue, ~12px radius, no shadow; quote text in dark sans plus a small role glyph.
- "A Message from the Team" card: dashed light-blue border on transparent/cream fill, square-ish corners, serif heading, gray sans body, periwinkle "Read more" text link.
- Product screenshots: real browser-window captures with macOS chrome, rounded corners and soft shadows, sometimes wrapped in a painterly gradient frame.
- Portrait tiles in "How people Surf": small illustrated avatar cards in a 2x3 grid.

## Signature
- Continuous painted scenery as the scroll backdrop — day sky → meadow → starry night → cream paper → sunflower grass footer — with tiny illustrated characters (girl, elephant) anchoring sections.
- Old-style serif display type over illustrations, paired with plain neo-grotesque body copy.
- Email + Apply pill capture repeated at hero and footer as the only CTA on the page.
- Pastel mint/peach/lavender quote cards color-coded by audience (students, engineers, designers), with inline glyphs and a blue-highlighted word in the serif section heading.

## Do / Don't
Do:
- Let full-bleed illustration carry section transitions; blend scenes into each other with soft painted gradients instead of hard section borders.
- Keep the CTA pattern to a single email-input + blue pill Apply button; repeat it verbatim at top and bottom.
- Set headlines in the high-contrast serif at modest sizes with sentence-case, conversational copy ("This is the future of bookmarks."), quotes included.
- Use real product screenshots with macOS window chrome and soft shadows as the proof points under each heading.
- Color-code repeated card groups with the pastel trio (mint/peach/lavender) and matching 1px borders.

Don't:
- Don't add a conventional nav bar, mega-menu, or footer link columns — the page has none.
- Don't use dark UI panels or saturated brand gradients; outside the night-sky scene everything sits on cream paper or painted sky.
- Don't make the serif bold or tightly tracked — display weight stays book/regular with natural spacing.
- Don't put shadows or heavy elevation on the pastel quote cards; they stay flat with hairline borders.
- Don't use the blue primary for anything except the Apply button and occasional single-word text highlights.
