## Overview
A calm, editorial take on AI-utility SaaS: warm off-white canvas, quiet light-gray cards, and classic serif display headings set against tiny grotesque body text. Color is used sparingly — a deep forest-green CTA, one soft watercolor gradient in the home hero, and a single bright WhatsApp green on the integration page. The overall effect is paper-like and restrained, closer to a well-set magazine than a typical gradient-heavy AI landing page.

## Layout
Slim top nav: small logomark + wordmark left, text links right, one dark pill "Sign up" button. Home hero is centered and symmetric — announcement pill, app icon, serif wordmark, two-line description — floating over a soft pastel yellow-pink-mint gradient that fades into cream. Content lives in a narrow centered column (~1000px) with big vertical breathing room between sections. Sections alternate between 2-column card grids (Features, Pricing), a 4-up platform/card row, and dense two-column checklist-plus-testimonial lists (Use cases). Feature page uses a full-bleed photographic hero with white serif headline bottom-left and a 3-up icon "How it works" row, then 2x2 feature cards. Section headers are single serif words ("Features", "Pricing", "How it works") centered above the content.

## Components
Buttons: pill-shaped throughout — primary CTAs filled deep forest green with white text ("Try it on WhatsApp" with arrow), secondary pills white/ghost with thin border; platform download buttons are small black pills with icons. Cards: light warm gray (#F4F3EF-ish) fills, very subtle or no border, no drop shadows, 10-14px corners; each contains a bold sans title, small gray body text, and an embedded product UI vignette (white mini-window with its own small radius and hairline border). Pricing cards are white with a thin border and a large colored price block inside (green for Individual, muted maroon for Teams). Testimonials sit in gray rounded blocks with quote marks and yellow-highlighted key phrases. FAQ items are plain text rows with a small circled "?" icon — no accordion chrome visible.

## Signature
- Serif editorial headings ("Features", "Use cases", "Take notes on WhatsApp") paired with very small grotesque body copy — strong scale and style contrast.
- Deep forest-green pill as the only recurring CTA color on an otherwise near-monochrome cream/gray page.
- Soft airbrushed pastel gradient confined to the home hero; the rest of the page stays flat paper tones.
- Marker-style yellow highlight on key phrases inside testimonial quotes.
- Flat gray cards with embedded miniature product-UI screenshots instead of illustrations.

## Do / Don't
**Do**
- Set section titles as single short serif words/phrases, centered, with lots of whitespace above and below.
- Keep the palette 90% cream/gray/near-black; reserve the forest-green fill strictly for CTAs and price blocks.
- Use flat light-gray rounded cards with no shadows; put realistic mini product UI inside them as the visual.
- Use pill radii on every button, including tiny platform-download chips.
- Let one hero carry the color moment (pastel gradient or full-bleed photo); return to flat cream immediately after.

**Don't**
- Don't use serif type for body copy or UI labels — serif is display-only here.
- Don't add drop shadows, heavy borders, or dark-mode sections; everything stays light and flat.
- Don't introduce blue/purple "AI" gradients — the only saturated accent besides green is the contextual WhatsApp green.
- Don't make body text large; the system relies on small, quiet sans text against oversized serif headers.
- Don't fill cards with abstract illustrations or 3D art — evidence shows only real product screenshots and simple line icons.
