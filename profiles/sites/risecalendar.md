## Overview
Dark-neon productivity aesthetic built around one violet: a white hero melts through a lavender-to-violet aurora gradient into a near-black (#0c0c0c) body where dense, colorful calendar-app screenshots do most of the visual talking. Type is a quiet neo-grotesque in black-on-white or gray-on-black; the only saturated brand element outside the product shots is the #6d68e9 CTA. The page ends on a purple "sunrise over a dark planet horizon" glow that literalizes the Rise name.

## Layout
Slim one-row nav on white: small logo left, five text links center-right, "Sign in" link plus a violet pill-ish "Get started" button. Hero is centered and text-first — beta announcement pill, two-line display headline, two-line gray subhead, single CTA — then a full-width app screenshot floating over the white-to-violet gradient. A dark logo bar ("Used and loved by...") with 7 grayscale logos bridges into the black body. Feature storytelling alternates: full-width screenshot strips with text overlaid to one side, then a 2-column and a 3-column grid of #171717 feature cards. Mid-page palate cleanser: a long white manifesto section in large gray text. Then a light section pairing a left headline with a stacked column of three gradient-washed cards. Closes with a centered dark CTA section (two buttons) above the horizon glow and a 4-column footer.

## Components
- Buttons: solid violet #6d68e9 fill, white label, small chevron suffix ("Get started ›"), ~8px radius, no visible border or shadow; secondary button on dark is a neutral dark-gray fill with white text ("Download Desktop app").
- Cards: #171717 panels on the black canvas, 12-16px radius, no borders, screenshot crops bleeding to card edges; light-section cards carry soft white-to-violet gradient washes.
- Announcement pill: dark rounded-full chip with white text and chevron, sitting on the white hero.
- Testimonials: plain centered quote in gray with a small circular avatar, name, and role — no card chrome.
- Checklist bullets: small checkmarks with short gray lines inside feature blocks.
- Product screenshots ARE the illustration system: dense dark calendar UI with orange/tan, purple, and blue event blocks.

## Signature
- The white→lavender→violet→black vertical gradient that fuses a light hero onto a dark page — one continuous scroll from day into night.
- A single violet (#6d68e9) doing all interactive work; everything else is grayscale.
- Real, information-dense calendar screenshots used at huge scale as section art, with copy floated over or beside them.
- The closing planet-horizon purple glow under the final CTA (the "rise" motif).
- A full white manifesto section of oversized light-gray paragraphs breaking the dark rhythm mid-page.

## Do / Don't
**Do**
- Keep exactly one accent hue (violet #6d68e9) and let product screenshots supply all other color.
- Bridge light and dark sections with a tall soft gradient rather than a hard seam.
- Set headlines tight and medium-weight; rely on size contrast, not weight or color, for hierarchy.
- Use #171717 borderless cards on #0c0c0c so panels read as tone shifts, not outlined boxes.
- End CTAs with a chevron (›) and keep button copy to two or three words.

**Don't**
- Don't add neon glows, gradients, or strokes to buttons — CTAs here are flat solid fills.
- Don't outline or drop-shadow cards; separation comes from a one-step luminance lift.
- Don't use illustration or stock imagery — the product UI is the only artwork.
- Don't introduce a second brand color (no teal/pink accents); grayscale + one violet only.
- Don't run body copy at full white on dark; it sits at muted gray (#a9a9a9) with white reserved for headings.
