## Overview
Pure white minimal-light SaaS design where nearly everything is grayscale except colorful "emoji badge" pills embedded directly inside headlines (e.g. "Manage capacity planning [thumbs-up right away]"). Product screenshots and app-UI fragments carry the color; the page chrome stays black-on-white. Tone is playful but restrained — hand-drawn black-and-white mascot illustrations (a caped superhero) punctuate long white stretches.

## Layout
Slim single-row top nav: logo left, plain text links center-left, "Log in" text plus one bordered "Get started for free" button right, hairline bottom border. Heroes are centered stacks: huge two-line bold headline with an inline colored badge, short centered paragraph, single dark CTA button — no hero imagery until the large product screenshot below. Sections alternate: centered badge-headline ("Schedule [people]", "Track [progress]"), then light-gray (#F5F5F6) rounded panels holding app screenshots and 2-3 column feature cards. Very generous vertical whitespace between sections; content column is narrow (~600-900px) for text, wider for screenshot panels. Every page ends with the same closing block: mascot illustration, the badge-heavy "Schedule People and project in a simple and easy way" headline, dark CTA, then a 4-column footer.

## Components
Primary button: near-black (#2C2C2E) fill, white 600-weight label, small radius (~6px), compact padding. Secondary button: white fill, 1px light-gray border, black text, same shape. Cards: white or very light gray (#F5F5F6) fill, 1px #E5E5E7 border or borderless, 10-14px radius, no visible drop shadows. Blog cards: light-gray image area on top, white text area below, hairline border. Pricing Pro card: warm cream fill (#FDF8F0) with a 2px orange (#E8930C) border; Free card plain white with gray border. FAQ rows: bold question text with right-aligned chevron, hairline separators. Signature emoji badges: saturated pill/rounded-rect chips (blue, orange, green, red) with an emoji plus one bold white word, slightly rotated shadow-lifted look, used inline within display headlines.

## Signature
- Inline colored emoji-word badges dropped into otherwise black-and-white display headlines — the single most recognizable device.
- Almost total grayscale page chrome; saturated color appears only in badges, product-UI screenshots, and allocation bars.
- Hand-drawn black-ink superhero mascot and doodle illustrations against pure white.
- Recurring closing ritual on every page: mascot + badge headline + dark "Get started for free" button.

## Do
- Keep the canvas pure white and reserve saturated color for inline headline badges and product screenshots.
- Set headlines very bold with tight tracking, centered, and embed at most 2-4 emoji badges per headline.
- Use near-black solid buttons for the primary CTA everywhere; keep secondary buttons white with a hairline border.
- Frame product screenshots inside light-gray rounded panels with realistic app UI (Gantt bars, avatars, status chips).
- End long pages with the mascot illustration and the repeated badge headline + dark CTA sign-off.

## Don't
- Don't add gradients, drop shadows on cards, or colored section backgrounds — panels stay white/light-gray, borders stay hairline.
- Don't color links or nav items; navigation is plain black text with the active item grayed out.
- Don't use large-radius or pill-shaped primary buttons — buttons are compact rectangles with small (~6px) rounding.
- Don't introduce photography or full-color illustration; the only drawings are black-and-white ink doodles.
- Don't scatter accent colors into body copy or icons — emphasis in paragraphs is done with plain underlined text.
