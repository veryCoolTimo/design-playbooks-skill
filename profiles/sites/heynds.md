## Overview
A warm, near-monochrome minimal-light page: everything is built from one blush-white canvas, near-black type, and a single vivid red-orange (#F63901) used at full saturation for CTAs, keywords, icons, and checkmarks. Faint pink hairline curves radiate across the background like ray lines, giving texture without adding color. The result reads friendly and energetic rather than corporate, with playful emoji dropped directly into headlines.

## Layout
Centered top nav (logo left, links center, small filled orange CTA right) over a text-only centered hero: two-line display headline with an inline orange pill badge interrupting the words, gray subhead, then a filled + outlined button pair and an avatar-row social proof strip. Below sits a large rounded product-demo video frame. Sections alternate: centered two-line headlines (one line black, one keyword orange), 3x2 feature-card grid, two-up screenshot comparisons, an app-icon marquee, a chat-style before/after mock, a stats/comparison block, three-column pricing with the middle card outlined in orange, a single-column accordion FAQ, three-column testimonials with big stat numerals, and a full-bleed solid-orange pre-footer band. Whitespace is abundant; every section is symmetric and center-aligned.

## Components
Primary buttons: solid #F63901 fill, white label, arrow glyph, ~8-10px corners, no shadow. Secondary buttons: same shape, transparent fill, 1px orange border, orange label. Disabled/"coming soon" state: pale salmon #FC9A81 fill. Cards: blush fills (#FCF1EF-#FFECE8) with hairline pink borders, 12-16px radius, flat (no shadows); feature cards lead with an orange icon + orange title, dark body text. Pricing: featured card gets a 1px solid orange border and rounded orange check chips. FAQ rows are full-width bordered accordion bars with chevrons.

## Signature
- One-hue discipline: red-orange #F63901 carries every accent — CTAs, highlighted headline words, icons, checkmarks, stats, and the closing full-orange band.
- Two-line centered headlines where the second line (or a key phrase) flips to orange, often ending with an emoji or sparkle glyph.
- Faint pink radiating hairline curves across the blush-white canvas as ambient background texture.
- Flat, shadowless surfaces: tint changes and 1px borders do all the layering work.

## Do / Don't
Do:
- Reserve #F63901 for interactive/emphasis elements and let the blush canvas stay dominant.
- Color one keyword or line of each section headline orange; keep the rest near-black.
- Layer surfaces with blush tints (#FCF1EF/#FFECE8) and hairline borders instead of shadows.
- Center-align every section headline, subhead, and CTA cluster.
- Use inline emoji/sparkle glyphs inside headlines for personality.

Don't:
- Introduce a second accent hue — blues, greens, or purples break the one-color system.
- Add drop shadows or gradients to buttons and cards; everything here is flat.
- Use pure white (#FFFFFF) or cool gray backgrounds; the canvas is warm blush #FEFAF9.
- Left-align hero or section headers; the layout is strictly symmetric.
- Render CTAs as text links — primary actions are always solid orange rounded buttons.
