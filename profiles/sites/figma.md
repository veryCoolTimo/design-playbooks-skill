## Overview
Swiss-grotesque neutrality as a stage for loud, saturated color. The chrome (nav, type, buttons) is strictly black-on-white; personality comes from full-bleed pastel section bands (lavender, pale lime, salmon, cornflower blue) and dense collages of colorful product screenshots and abstract geometric shapes. The blog inverts to a near-black canvas with the same accent system. Feels like a design tool demonstrating itself: restrained system, exuberant content.

## Layout
Slim white sticky nav: small logo left, dropdown links, Log in + two pill CTAs (outlined "Contact sales", solid black "Get started for free") right. Heroes vary by page: home opens with an edge-to-edge masonry collage of user work with a floating prompt pill; product/solutions pages use a left-aligned H1 with a small abstract shape cluster top-right; events centers the H1 on a full-width lavender band. Section rhythm alternates white → tinted full-bleed band (lavender #CBBBF6/#E5DEFA, pale lime #E4F1CE, salmon #F8A78F, blue #6E8DF7) → white. Content sits on a generous 12-col grid with 2-up and 4-up card arrangements; big pull-quote sections set a customer quote as display type with a logo lockup. Black full-width footer with 5-column link matrix on every page.

## Components
Buttons: fully pill-shaped; solid black fill with white label for primary, 1px black outline on transparent for secondary, white pills on dark/tinted bands. Small, quiet, never gradient. Cards: mostly flat and shadowless — product screenshots in thin-bordered or borderless frames with small corner radius, sitting directly on white or on tinted bands; blog cards are image-top with mono-spaced uppercase tag chips below. Pricing: four flat columns separated by hairline rules, black pill "Select plan" per column, green dot matrix in the comparison table. Inputs: pill email field with inline arrow submit; FAQ rows as hairline-divided accordions with chevrons. Tag/label chips come in bright pill colors (lime, lavender, yellow) with black text.

## Signature
- Full-bleed pastel color bands (lavender, pale lime, salmon, cornflower) slicing an otherwise black-and-white page.
- Black pill buttons everywhere — the only CTA color is no color.
- Collages of real product UI screenshots and playful abstract geometry (arches, checkerboards, blobs, cursors) as the imagery, not stock photos.
- Neo-grotesque headings in sentence case, medium weight, tight leading; mono-spaced uppercase for eyebrows and tags.

## Do / Don't
Do:
- Keep all buttons pill-shaped and monochrome (solid black or thin black outline); let sections carry the color.
- Alternate white sections with full-width flat pastel bands; put cards and screenshots directly on the band color.
- Use hairline rules and flat borders instead of drop shadows for card and table separation.
- Set headlines in sentence case with tight leading; use small mono uppercase strings for labels, dates, and tags.
- Show cursors, canvas UI, and multiplayer artifacts inside imagery to signal the product.
Don't:
- Don't color the primary CTA with a brand hue — Figma never uses a colored button fill.
- Don't use gradients, glassmorphism, or soft shadows; every surface is flat.
- Don't round cards heavily — big radii belong only to buttons and chips.
- Don't desaturate the accents; the pastel bands are bold and fully opaque, not washed-out tints.
- Don't use photography of people as hero imagery; illustration, product UI, and geometric shapes only.
