## Overview
Cinematic near-black space aesthetic: pages read like a dark film set lit by violet-magenta glows, wireframe globes, and 3D renders of paper planes, cubes, and portals. Huge all-caps display type dominates, with select words swapped into an elegant serif italic for contrast. Ghosted oversized background words (RELIABILITY, COMPETITORS, FAQ) act as section wayfinding. The blog breaks pattern with a white canvas and a giant purple-gradient INSIGHTS masthead.

## Layout
Fixed slim dark nav bar: wordmark "DOPE.SECURITY" left, sparse uppercase links right, small white pill "BOOK A DEMO" as the lone filled CTA. Heroes are full-bleed dark scenes with a left-aligned mega headline and a small CTA cluster; home pairs the headline with a boxed deploy panel on the right. Sections alternate: text block left, glowing 3D render right (product page repeats this rhythm eight-plus times down a very long scroll). Enormous ghost words bleed off-canvas behind each section header. Generous vertical whitespace; content column is narrow against the wide dark field. Footer is a consistent 4-column dark block with the stacked serif-accented logo. Decorative HUD details everywhere: crosshair plus marks, tick rulers, dashed lines, coordinate-style annotations.

## Components
Buttons: pill-shaped, mostly 1px light outline on dark with uppercase tracked labels ("CONTACT SALES", "TOTALLY FREE 30-DAY TRIAL"); the primary action is a solid off-white pill with dark text (nav "BOOK A DEMO", "BECOME A PARTNER"). SSO buttons are small white rounded rectangles with Google/Microsoft logos. Cards: thin 1px violet or light outlines on translucent dark fills with soft purple glow (pricing card, partner "pillars" cards with ghost numerals 01/02); no drop shadows, glow instead. Pricing comparison table uses violet grid lines, green check circles, red cross circles, and a green-tinted highlight column for dope.security. FAQ rows are underlined accordions with plus icons. Blog cards (light page): white cards, thumbnail top, date in purple caps, pastel-outlined pill tags. Team labels styled as snake_case monospace annotations with connector lines.

## Signature
- Mixed-typeface headlines: all-caps grotesque with individual words set in a high-contrast serif italic.
- Ghosted 100vw+ background words naming each section, clipped by the viewport.
- Violet-magenta glow lighting on black; 3D metaphor renders (paper planes, wireframe globes, glass cubes).
- Monospace uppercase micro-annotations with underscores and connector lines, like console/HUD readouts.

## Do
- Keep the canvas near-black and light every section with a violet/magenta glow gradient rather than flat fills.
- Set headlines huge, all-caps, lightweight, and swap one or two words into a serif italic.
- Use outlined pill buttons by default; reserve the solid off-white pill for the single primary CTA per view.
- Add HUD dressing: plus-mark crosshairs, tick rulers, dashed rules, and snake_case mono captions.
- Use giant clipped ghost words behind section headings for wayfinding.

## Don't
- Don't use saturated purple as a button fill — purple is glow, borders, and data accents only; CTAs are white/off-white.
- Don't add drop shadows or heavy card fills; cards stay translucent with 1px outlines and glow.
- Don't use rounded-corner-heavy or colorful UI chrome; keep chrome thin, dark, and monochrome.
- Don't set headlines in bold weights; the display type stays light with wide tracking.
- Don't carry the dark theme into content-index pages blindly — the blog deliberately flips to a white canvas with pastel tag pills.
