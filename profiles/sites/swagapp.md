## Overview
A purple-saturated, sticker-collage aesthetic: pale lilac canvas, deep aubergine ink, and mid-purple accents everywhere, punctuated by periwinkle blue and yellow section cards. Product UI chips (leave requests, balances, timesheets) are scattered like stickers over photos of workers, with hand-drawn squiggle doodles and pattern swatches layered behind. It reads as a consumer app, not enterprise HR software.

## Layout
Minimal header: logo pill left, "Menu" pill beside it, single dark "Download" pill right. Hero is a split: left-aligned H1 + short paragraph + one CTA on the left, a rotated photo-collage with floating UI stickers on the right. Sections alternate text-left/collage-right and collage-left/text-right. Mid-page has a tabbed feature switcher (Work / Career / Money / Benefits) whose panel is a huge full-width rounded card in a solid accent color (blue for Money) with stacked yellow/orange card edges peeking out behind it like a card deck. One section is pure typographic spectacle: viewport-spanning "How it Works" headline interrupted by an overlapping rotated purple card. Generous vertical whitespace between sections; content column is wide but airy.

## Components
Buttons: pill-shaped, solid dark purple (#391853, near-navy #140E59 on the blue section) with pale pink-white label; no borders, no visible shadows. Cards: large radius (~20-24px), solid fills (white, periwinkle #99A9FC, purple #BA80E6), often rotated a few degrees with a slightly offset drop-shadow card behind them for a stacked-paper effect. Small UI sticker chips: white, rounded, tiny soft shadow, containing fake product data (balances, approvals, timesheets). Tab pills: white with thin outline; active tab filled #BA80E6 with white text. Social links: white circles with dark glyphs sitting on a solid purple band. List rows in feature cards: pale lavender rounded strips.

## Signature
- Monochrome-purple world: lilac canvas, aubergine ink, violet accents — purple at every tonal level, with blue/yellow used only as section-card fills.
- Rotated photo collages overlaid with white product-UI "stickers" and hand-drawn squiggles.
- Giant display typography that bleeds past the container edge, with a rotated card overlapping the headline.
- Stacked/offset card decks: a colored panel with yellow and orange card edges peeking from behind.

## Do / Don't
Do:
- Keep every neutral tinted purple — backgrounds, text, and shadows all sit on the violet axis; never use plain gray.
- Rotate collage images and floating chips 2-6 degrees and let them overlap section boundaries.
- Fill buttons with the darkest purple and keep them pill-shaped with short two-word labels ("Download Now", "Learn More").
- Use one saturated solid (periwinkle, yellow, violet) per feature card, full-bleed inside a big rounded container.
- Decorate with small hand-drawn doodles (squiggles, bursts) in the aubergine ink color.

Don't:
- Don't add gradients — every surface here is a flat solid.
- Don't use borders or heavy shadows on buttons; elevation comes only from offset stacked cards and tiny sticker shadows.
- Don't set headings in a serif or condensed face; the voice is a friendly rounded grotesque throughout.
- Don't present screenshots in device frames — product UI appears as loose, rotated white chips over lifestyle photos.
- Don't crowd the header: logo, one menu pill, one CTA is the entire nav.
