## Overview
A two-color poster system: near-white gray (#F0F0F0) and a single searing orange-red (#FF3700) trade places as background and ink down the whole page. Enormous all-caps display type with mischievous swapped-out glyphs does almost all the visual work; UI chrome is minimal. Real product screenshots and a full-bleed image-grid strip supply the only photographic color.

## Layout
Slim text-only nav: black wordmark left, orange text links (Pricing, Blog, Log in) plus one small orange filled Sign up button right. Hero is a giant staircase-aligned headline ("THE SEARCH / ENGINE / FOR CREATIVE / PEOPLE") with lines deliberately offset left/right, a small pill search input and one line of copy beneath. Sections alternate as full-width color blocks: gray hero, then a long orange block with tilted, overlapping product screenshots and white text cards floating over them, a client-logo strip, back to gray for a features section, then a full-bleed edge-to-edge image mosaic (search-results demo), then pricing. Headlines are staggered across the grid rather than centered; whitespace is generous vertically, and type frequently spans nearly the full container width.

## Components
Buttons: small filled orange rectangles with medium (8-12px) rounding, white label ("Sign up", "SUBSCRIBE"); on gray the same orange fill, low-key sizing that contrasts with the huge type. Hero search input: white pill with tiny placeholder text and an adjacent "Search for free" button. Cards: white rectangles with big orange display type inside ("649K IMAGES FROM MOVIES..."), slightly rotated and layered over screenshots; pricing cards are flat, thin-bordered outlines on gray with orange headline prices and check-listed features. Product screenshots have small radius and drop shadows, stacked with rotation like loose photo prints. Feature icons are hand-drawn-style orange line pictograms with dashed motion strokes.

## Signature
- One ink, one paper: everything is #FF3700 on #F0F0F0 or inverted, with black reserved for the wordmark and photos
- Display glyph swaps: every "O" in headlines becomes an asterisk, notch-cut disc, or wedge, making the type itself the brand mark
- Staggered, stair-stepped headline layout — lines shift horizontally instead of aligning, spanning the full page width
- Tilted, overlapping screenshot stacks and rotated white caption cards over the orange field

## Do / Don't
Do:
- Set headlines in heavy condensed caps at extreme sizes (spanning 60-90% of viewport width) and offset each line horizontally
- Swap selected glyphs (especially O) for geometric symbols in display text only, keeping body text clean
- Flip the two-color scheme per section: orange-on-gray, then gray/white-on-orange
- Keep interactive elements small and quiet (compact filled buttons, pill search) so the type stays the loudest element
- Rotate screenshots and caption cards a few degrees and overlap them for a pasted-collage feel

Don't:
- Introduce a second brand hue or gradients — the system is strictly orange + gray + black wordmark
- Center-align the big headlines or shrink them to conventional h1 sizes
- Add shadows, borders, or heavy card chrome on the gray sections; surfaces stay flat
- Use the glyph-swap trick in body copy, form labels, or pricing numbers
- Round buttons to full pills (only the search input is a pill; buttons stay 8-12px)
