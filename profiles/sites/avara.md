## Overview
A single-screen, pitch-black stage where four sticker-style mascot illustrations carry the entire visual load: a lavender ghost in a cap, an orange scalloped frame, a smiling blue cloud, and a green smiley "G" badge. There is no conventional UI — no nav bar, no buttons, no copy blocks — just characters, a wordmark, and four hairline footer links. The vibe is toy-like and playful set against austere darkness, with characters clearly built for motion/interaction (poses shift subtly across captures).

## Layout
No header navigation; a lone speaker/audio-toggle icon sits top-left. The hero is the page: four character illustrations arranged in a loose horizontal row, vertically centered, each representing an ecosystem product. Bottom center holds the custom AVARA wordmark with a single row of tiny text links beneath it (X · Hey · Blog · Careers) separated by middle dots. Extreme whitespace (blackspace) — roughly 70% of the viewport is empty black, focusing all attention on the character row.

## Components
Effectively zero traditional components: no visible CTA buttons, cards, or inputs (primary hex above is sampled from the green badge character, the strongest brand color on the page). Links are bare, small, low-contrast gray text with no underline or container. The illustrations act as the interactive units: each is a flat-color sticker with a thick self-colored outline plus a white/light halo edge, minimal face details (dot eyes, thin smile), and slight pose changes between frames indicating hover/idle animation. Audio toggle is a minimal line icon.

## Signature
- Sticker-cutout mascots: flat fills, bold rounded outlines, pale halo edge, floating on pure black
- One character per product — brand system told entirely through cute anthropomorphic icons, not text
- Custom crossbar-less "AVARA" wordmark as the only typographic statement on the page
- Anti-layout minimalism: no nav, no headline, no CTA — a dark toybox stage with motion doing the storytelling

## Do / Don't
**Do**
- Keep the canvas true black and let saturated character colors (purple, orange, sky blue, green) provide all contrast
- Give every illustration a thick outline plus a light halo edge so it reads as a die-cut sticker on the dark field
- Center content in a single horizontal band and leave the rest of the viewport empty
- Keep text nearly invisible in hierarchy: tiny, gray, dot-separated links only at the bottom
- Imply interactivity through idle motion and subtle pose changes rather than buttons or labels

**Don't**
- Add a conventional nav bar, hero headline, or CTA button — the page deliberately has none
- Use gradients, shadows, or 3D rendering on the characters; fills stay flat with line-art details
- Introduce body copy or explanatory text blocks on the landing screen
- Box content in cards or bordered containers; shapes are organic blobs, clouds, and badges
- Brighten the footer/link text to high contrast — it is intentionally recessive against the mascots
