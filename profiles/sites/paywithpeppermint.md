## Overview
A confetti-bright, illustration-first fintech landing page that swaps corporate SaaS polish for a party-invite mood. Full-bleed color blocking alternates between deep navy, electric violet, cream, and blush, with oversized cartoon arms handing off phones and cards across section boundaries. Type is huge, dark-or-cream, and extrabold; everything else is playful collage — ribbons, sparkles, tilted pastel cards, a money-gun illustration.

## Layout
Single centered logo pill in the nav area (no visible link row), sitting on a violet hero blob whose bottom edge is a curved organic shape, not a straight line. Hero is fully centered: giant three-line headline, one short subline, one small pill CTA. Sections are full-bleed color bands (violet → navy → cream → navy → blush) with illustrations physically bridging bands — a pink arm runs from the hero down into the navy section. Mid-page: a fan of four tilted feature cards under a centered H2; a "Built for you" band with a real photo carousel and an underlined tab strip; a narrow violet column shape framing a single message. Bottom: FAQ accordion list on blush, one final dark CTA, minimal footer, and a giant cropped "peppermint" wordmark bleeding off the page bottom.

## Components
Buttons: pill-shaped; hero CTA is a small pale-lavender pill with dark text, final CTA is a large dark-navy pill with cream text — both flat, no borders or shadows. Feature cards: large-radius (~16-20px) pastel rectangles (mint, pink, lavender, warm gray), each rotated a few degrees, containing a small spot illustration, bold title, and short body copy; no borders, very soft or no shadow. FAQ accordion: full-width large-radius rows, white when closed with "+" icons, pale-yellow fill with "−" when expanded. Photo carousel uses a wide rounded image with small chevron arrows and a text-tab strip where the active tab is bold with an underline.

## Signature
- Illustrated arms and hands that cross section boundaries, stitching the color bands together (handshake over a card, hand firing a money gun).
- Hard alternation of saturated full-bleed bands: violet, deep navy, cream, blush — with curved/organic edges instead of straight section breaks.
- Confetti ribbons, sparkles, and squiggly pink underlines scattered around extrabold centered headlines.
- Giant lowercase brand wordmark cropped at the footer edge in muted yellow.

## Do / Don't
**Do**
- Center everything in the hero and section intros: headline, subline, one pill CTA — no split hero layouts.
- Alternate full-bleed background bands (violet #6B4EE8, navy #1B2150, cream #FBEFD6, blush #FAE9DE) and let illustrations overlap the seams.
- Rotate pastel feature cards 2-5 degrees and fan them like a dealt hand.
- Use extrabold, tightly-spaced display type at very large sizes, in cream on dark bands and navy on light bands.
- Keep buttons flat pills with high-contrast fills; reserve pale yellow as the "active/open" state (expanded FAQ row).

**Don't**
- Don't add gradients, glassmorphism, or drop-shadow depth — surfaces here are flat color.
- Don't use straight horizontal section dividers; edges are curved blobs or bridged by artwork.
- Don't introduce corporate dashboard screenshots or device mockups as hero art — illustration carries the product story (the one photo is of people, not UI).
- Don't use thin or light font weights for headings; nothing on the page is lighter than bold.
- Don't crowd the nav — the page ships with essentially a logo and a single CTA, no mega-menu.
