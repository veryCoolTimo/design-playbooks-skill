## Overview
A Y2K "console faceplate" design that treats the page as hardware: everything sits on brushed periwinkle metal, each module rendered as a beveled plate with a bright top edge and a chrome-indigo shadow line beneath. A near-black carbon layer with halftone-dot texture handles all system controls (nav, right-rail buttons, footer), while warm gold/amber/orange is strictly rationed to navigation and action cues. Heroes break the cool chrome with full-bleed photography tinted per page and topped by outlined, drop-shadowed box-art wordmarks.

## Layout
Fixed ~780-830px centered canvas, no reflow. Masthead with Mario cutout + speech bubble and a search module, then two stacked nav bars: carbon primary (gold section words, amber utility chips) over a pale-sky secondary strip of utility links. Body: full-width hero plate, then a two-thirds content column of stacked panels (news list, 2x2 featured-site grid, poll) beside a one-third right action rail; a thin strip of rotated carbon tabs clings to the left edge. Whitespace is nearly absent — plates butt together separated only by thin bevel seams, deliberately reading as a packed control panel.

## Components
Buttons come in three warm-coded tiers: amber 2px-radius chips with carbon text for utilities (pressed state darkens to nav gold), signal-orange fills with white text for submit/forward actions, and sharp-cornered carbon slabs with white text for right-rail actions; round 22px orange discs and 18px orange chevron chips serve as "go" affordances. Inputs are small white fields (~20px tall, 2px radius, 1px hairline border) with bold inline labels, housed in platinum-gray form panels capped by canvas-colored uppercase header bars. Cards range from platinum list rows with trailing arrow chips to periwinkle raised panels (polls, system tiles) and white info cards with amber header tabs. Depth is entirely hard bevel simulation — no blurred shadows anywhere.

## Signature
- Beveled-metal-plate construction: every region edge-lit on top, shadow-lined in chrome indigo below, with chamfered (45-degree cut) corners on the largest panels
- Warm color as pure wayfinding: nav gold for menu words, amber for tool chips/badges, signal orange for anything that moves you forward — never decorative
- Carbon-navy command slabs with halftone speaker-grille dot texture carrying nav, action rail, and footer
- Hero wordmarks in heavy Arial Black with thick outline + hard drop shadow over page-tinted photo fields (lavender home, circuit-teal systems, racetrack-red games)

## Do / Don't
Do:
- Give every panel the bevel treatment (bright top edge, #3d4f97 shadow line) so the page reads as an assembled machine
- Set all structural labels in uppercase Arial Bold 11px with 0.5px tracking — the silkscreened-legend voice
- Outline and hard-shadow every display wordmark over its photographic field
- Keep the carbon halftone layer exclusive to system controls, separating chrome from content
- Pack modules tightly with thin seams; density is the texture

Don't:
- Round every corner uniformly — sharp/chamfered chrome with only pill logo, radios, and arrow discs rounded is the identity
- Use soft blurred drop shadows; depth here is hard bevels and pictorial photography only
- Let orange or amber appear decoratively — warm always signals "act here"
- Add saturated accents beyond the per-page hero tints (#206479, #a7282b); steady-state chrome stays cool periwinkle + carbon
- Open up whitespace into an airy layout or merge the dual nav bars into one
