## Overview
A bright, illustration-driven SaaS language built on a clean white canvas punctuated by saturated gradient blocks. Purple-to-pink-to-blue gradients carry the brand across hero art, feature cards, and full-bleed dark-navy sections. Flat geometric and isometric illustrations (mountain vistas, isometric buildings, tetromino blocks, abstract shapes) do the emotional work; UI screenshots on devices ground it in product reality.

## Layout
Sparse top nav: wordmark left, four text links plus a solid purple pill CTA right, on white. Heroes vary per page: home splits headline-left / large isometric illustration-right; community uses a full-bleed illustrated landscape with centered white type; jobs and use-cases center a huge headline over illustration or device collage. Section rhythm alternates white canvas, rounded gradient panels, and full-width near-black (#0E1233 / #1A1A1A) bands. Content sits in a generous centered container; feature sections often pair one wide card with a two-up card row below. Blog is a 3-column grid of illustrated thumbnails, with two featured wide cards up top.

## Components
Buttons: pill shape throughout — solid purple fill for primary CTAs, white fill with dark text on gradient backgrounds, and ghost/outlined white pills ("Read more", "Read the guidelines") on image cards. No visible shadows on buttons. Cards: large radius (16-24px), filled with either flat illustration, vivid multi-stop gradients, or dimmed photographic/illustrated backgrounds with white text overlaid; borders absent, elevation minimal. Small eyebrow labels in caps (purple or letter-spaced) above headings; tag chips on blog posts are tiny gray rounded rectangles. The "FOUNDER" badge is an outlined pill with gradient border.

## Signature
- Purple→pink→blue gradient fills used as full card backgrounds, not just accents.
- Flat geometric/isometric illustration system: mountain-and-pines night scenes, isometric building cutaways, tetromino block art, abstract shape compositions on every blog thumbnail.
- Alternating white and deep-navy full-bleed sections with heavily rounded gradient cards floating inside.
- Pill buttons everywhere, with ghost-white outline variants sitting directly on imagery.

## Do / Don't
**Do**
- Fill entire cards with saturated multi-stop gradients (magenta→violet→blue) and set white text on them.
- Give every blog post a bespoke flat-geometric illustration thumbnail instead of photos.
- Alternate white sections with full-width dark-navy bands to reset visual rhythm.
- Use pill buttons: solid #8B5CF6 for the main CTA, white or ghost-outline variants on colored surfaces.
- Show the product as device screenshots (laptop, phone) embedded inside gradient cards.

**Don't**
- Use stock photography or realistic imagery — everything illustrated is flat, geometric, or isometric.
- Add borders or heavy drop shadows to cards; color and radius do the separation.
- Use small or timid headlines — display type is huge, bold, and tightly tracked ("A team that fits").
- Introduce sharp-cornered buttons or cards; nothing interactive is squarer than ~16px.
- Let gradients drift outside the purple/pink/blue/coral family into greens or teals for brand surfaces.
