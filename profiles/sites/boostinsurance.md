## Overview
Dark-tech insurtech landing on a near-black blue-green canvas (#012025), lit almost entirely by one signature gradient that runs cyan → green → lime-yellow (#3EDCD9 → #5BEA95 → #D6EB40). White geometric-sans headlines, muted teal surfaces one step above the canvas (#043239), and isometric circuit-board illustrations give it an "API infrastructure" feel while staying friendly and rounded. A single off-white section flip at the very end ("Get in Touch!") breaks the darkness before a dark footer.

## Layout
- Slim top nav: logo left, four dropdown links center-left, pill-outlined "Contact Us" right; no solid nav bar, it sits directly on the canvas.
- Hero: left-aligned two-column — large 3-line headline with a gradient underline on the key word ("growth."), short paragraph, gradient pill CTA; right side is a translucent product mockup with a floating widget card and yellow/cyan tag chips.
- Section rhythm: logo-wall panel (heading left, 2×4 grayscale logo grid right, dotted-line dividers) → stat/illustration 3-card row → "Why Boost?" with giant faded ghost text ("Why Boost? Wh…") bleeding off-canvas behind the heading → three alternating feature rows (dark text card left, browser mockup with a thick cyan→lime gradient border right) → centered CTA panel over faint circuit-trace linework → image+text product split → two-column link-chip list → 4×2 icon-card grid ("Who We Work With") → off-white contact split → dense multi-column dark footer.
- Generous vertical whitespace between sections; content in a contained max-width column; dotted lines and thin circuit traces fill negative space.

## Components
- Primary button: pill, filled with the cyan→lime horizontal gradient, dark teal label + thin arrow, no shadow. Secondary button: pill with 1px gradient stroke on transparent (dark canvas) or on off-white, dark/white label.
- Cards: #043239 surface, ~16-24px radius, borderless and shadowless; illustration zone on top separated from caption text by a dotted divider. Icon cards in "Who We Work With" use cyan (#49D6E6) two-tone icons, centered white title, small muted body.
- Browser mockups: dark UI frames wrapped in a 2-3px cyan→lime gradient border, large radius, with floating white overlay cards carrying small yellow (#F0D73E) and cyan tag pills.
- Link chips: full-width rounded rows with thin borders in a two-column list.
- Logos rendered flat gray/desaturated on the panel.

## Signature
- One gradient everywhere: cyan → green → lime-yellow, used for CTA fills, mockup borders, keyword highlights, and gradient underlines.
- Near-black teal canvas (#012025) with surfaces only one shade lighter — depth comes from color steps and gradient strokes, not shadows.
- Circuit-board motif: dotted lines, thin lime traces, and isometric chip/mailbox illustrations echo the "API infrastructure" story.
- Giant translucent ghost headline ("Why Boost?") bleeding off both edges behind a section heading.

## Do
- Reserve the cyan→lime gradient for CTAs, borders, and single highlighted words; keep everything else in the teal ramp.
- Use pill buttons with the gradient fill for the primary action and a 1px gradient outline for secondary ones.
- Layer surfaces by nudging the teal lighter (#012025 → #043239 → mockup interiors), separated by dotted dividers rather than shadows.
- Highlight one word per headline with a gradient color or underline (e.g., "growth.", "$65 Billion", "Revenue").
- End the page with an off-white contact section to reset contrast before the dark footer.

## Don't
- Don't introduce reds, purples, or warm neutrals — the palette is strictly teal/cyan/green/lime plus one small yellow tag accent.
- Don't add drop shadows or glassy blur to cards; the site keeps flat color-step surfaces.
- Don't use sharp corners; everything from buttons to mockup frames is pill or heavily rounded.
- Don't render partner logos in color — they stay flat desaturated gray on the panel.
- Don't fill the canvas edge-to-edge with content; keep wide dark breathing room with only faint circuit traces in it.
