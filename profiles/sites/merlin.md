## Overview
Airy, Apple-adjacent minimal-light landing page: pure white canvas, a faint pastel aurora gradient bleeding down from the top edge, oversized regular-weight grotesque headlines, and pill-shaped black CTAs. Warmth comes from handwritten green script annotations with doodle arrows and soft ambient-gradient product cards, keeping an otherwise sterile layout personal.

## Layout
No visible persistent nav bar in the capture; the hero is a centered stack (dotted eyebrow line, two-line headline with an inline app-icon glyph, gray subhead, script annotation, black pill CTA, then a grayscale "Used by professionals at" logo strip). Sections alternate between white and off-white (#f5f5f3) full-bleed bands, with one saturated full-bleed sky-blue photo section as a mid-page pattern break. Content sits in a wide centered column; product demos live inside huge rounded containers with soft sky-to-gray gradient fills. A two-up bento card row (warm beige gradient / cool blue gradient) follows the main demo. Generous vertical whitespace between sections; centered section titles.

## Signature
- Handwritten green script annotations ("Try merlin free for 7 days", "How it works") with hand-drawn curly arrows pointing at CTAs and headings
- Faint multicolor aurora gradient across the very top of an otherwise white page
- Inline app-icon glyphs embedded directly inside display headlines
- Ambient pastel-gradient demo cards (sky blue, warm beige) with huge corner radii against flat white/off-white bands
- Glossy 3D micro-details (sphere step badges, sheen on green pill) inside a flat minimal system

## Do / Don't
Do:
- Keep the canvas white/off-white and let one saturated full-bleed blue section act as the single mid-page break
- Use pill buttons everywhere; black fill for the main CTA, light gray for secondary actions
- Pair every major CTA or section heading with a small green handwritten annotation plus a doodle arrow
- Set headlines large but at regular/medium weight; reserve gray (#636363) for subheads and body
- Round cards and demo containers heavily (24px+) and fill them with soft ambient gradients rather than borders

Don't:
- Don't add heavy borders, hard shadows, or dark-mode sections beyond the sky-blue band
- Don't use bold, condensed, or serif display type — the headline voice is a quiet neutral grotesque
- Don't introduce more brand colors; green script + blue chat/section blues are the only accents against black-and-white
- Don't crowd sections — each section is a single centered idea with wide vertical padding
- Don't render the pricing as colored plan cards; it's a flat gray checklist comparison, not tiered pricing tiles
