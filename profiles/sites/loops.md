## Overview
Minimal-light SaaS landing built on a pure white canvas washed with soft blush-pink and peach radial glows. A single hot orange is reserved for CTAs, while near-black navy blocks (code demo, feature tiles) punch contrast into an otherwise airy page. The product itself — a white dashboard mock with an orange logo and teal status pills — doubles as the hero artwork.

## Layout
Slim top nav: logo left, two text links + small outlined "Log in" pill right, on white. Hero is left-aligned text (YC badge pill, two-line bold headline, short subtitle with bolded keywords, inline email input + orange button) with a subtle 3D concentric-arc sculpture on the right. Below: a monochrome logo strip, then a full-width dashboard screenshot in a rounded light card. Section rhythm alternates white feature sections with full-bleed rounded dark-navy blocks; one bento grid mixes orange-gradient, white, starry-navy, and black tiles. Content sits in a narrow centered column (~1200px) with generous vertical whitespace; a repeat email-capture bar and a three-column footer close the page.

## Components
Buttons: solid orange (#FB5A0C) with white uppercase-ish small label, small 4-6px radius; secondary "Log in" and "Create" are tiny dark pills. Inputs: white field with hairline gray border, small radius, paired inline with the CTA button. Cards: white with 1px light-gray border, minimal or no shadow, 10-16px radius; feature list items are stacked bordered rows where the active row shows a title + gray description. Status pills in the dashboard mock use teal/green text. Segmented control (API / Integrations / Manual) with a dark active pill. Dark blocks contain code samples with syntax-colored orange strings on near-black.

## Signature
- White page tinted by soft pink/peach radial glow washes bleeding in from the edges
- One saturated orange used only for the brand mark and CTA buttons — nothing else competes
- Alternation of airy white sections with large-radius near-black navy blocks (code, feature tiles, starry dark-mode card)
- Product dashboard screenshots and flow diagrams (stacked pill nodes joined by arrows) used as illustration instead of stock art

## Do / Don't
Do:
- Keep the canvas white and add atmosphere only via faint pink/peach radial gradients, never solid tinted sections
- Reserve #FB5A0C strictly for primary CTAs and the logo; use dark slate for headings and mid-gray for supporting text
- Use hairline-bordered white cards with little to no shadow; let borders, not elevation, define edges
- Drop in full-bleed rounded dark-navy blocks for technical content (code, API) to create contrast beats
- Show the product: dashboard mocks, workflow node diagrams, and email-type lists as the visuals

Don't:
- Don't introduce blue or purple as interactive colors — links and CTAs stay in the orange family
- Don't use heavy drop shadows or glassmorphism; the aesthetic is flat borders + soft background glow
- Don't center the hero copy; hero text is left-aligned with the visual on the right
- Don't fill sections edge-to-edge with color; dark blocks are rounded islands inside white padding
- Don't use decorative stock imagery — every illustration is product UI or a schematic diagram
