## Overview
A spreadsheet-nerd take on minimal-light: pure black ink and near-square geometry on white, punctuated by full-bleed blocks of saturated pastel (lavender hero, cyan, mint, yellow feature bands). Typography leans monospace — headings, button labels, and testimonial metadata all carry terminal/spreadsheet DNA. Decorative strips of multicolored "cells" (a pixel mosaic evoking a colored-in spreadsheet) frame the hero and footer.

## Layout
Thin white nav bar with "= Equals" wordmark left, plain text links center-right, and a solid black rectangular CTA at the far right. Hero sits on a flat lavender (#DDC2F9) field: centered headline with a hand-underlined "any", one-line subhead, then an email input + button pair, followed by a large framed product screenshot (spreadsheet UI with a yellow circular play button) and a monochrome customer-logo row. Section rhythm alternates: gray (#EFEFEF) "Meet Equals" band with dual CTAs, then three feature rows where the left half is white text-on-canvas and the right half is a full-bleed color block (cyan/mint/yellow) holding the visual. Testimonials form a dense multi-column masonry of flat colored cards. Footer is a full-bleed cyan block topped by the spreadsheet-mosaic strip.

## Components
Buttons: rectangular with barely-rounded corners, monospace labels; primary is solid black with white text, secondary is white with a thin black border and a subtle hard drop shadow. Email input: white rectangle, thin black border, gray placeholder. Testimonial cards: flat solid pastel fills (coral, marigold, lavender, mint), no border, square corners, pixel-art or photo avatars, monospace name/title/date, underlined black @mentions. Integration icons sit in white rounded tiles with soft shadows on the cyan block. Product screenshots framed as realistic app windows with toolbar chrome.

## Signature
- Spreadsheet-cell mosaic strips: rows of random saturated color rectangles bleeding off the page edges around hero and footer
- Monospace type woven through a grotesque system — buttons, captions, and metadata read like terminal output
- Full-bleed flat pastel blocks (lavender, cyan, mint, yellow) doing the work shadows and gradients would elsewhere
- Masonry wall of flat multicolor testimonial cards with pixel avatars, functioning as a rainbow texture section

## Do / Don't
Do:
- Keep ink pure black on white or on saturated pastels; contrast comes from color blocking, not gray scales
- Use rectangular, near-square buttons with monospace labels; black fill for the primary action, thin black border for secondary
- Alternate white sections with full-bleed flat color bands (one hue per feature) at a steady rhythm
- Use the multicolor cell-mosaic motif as a horizontal divider or edge decoration
- Show the real product as a chromed app window; anchor the hero on the flat lavender field

Don't:
- Don't add gradients, glassmorphism, or large soft shadows — surfaces are flat with at most a thin hard shadow on secondary buttons
- Don't round cards or buttons past ~6px (only small icon tiles get medium radius)
- Don't restrict the palette to one accent; the identity depends on many saturated pastels used as flat fills
- Don't use muted gray body text — copy is black, even on colored cards
- Don't set headings in a warm serif or humanist face; the mono/grotesque voice is the brand
