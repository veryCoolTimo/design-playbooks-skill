## Craft rules

1. **One idea per piece, pushed all the way.** The strongest works commit to a single conceit and let it carry everything: a delete interaction where cards literally fade to ghosts (`family-inspired-trash-interaction.jpg`), a support-ticket UI as a receipt printer (`ticket-reveal.jpg`), a job flyer die-cut as a giant numeral (`animated-number-concepts.jpg`), an invoice as a thermal till receipt (`receipt-style-studio-invoice.jpg`). No secondary gimmicks compete.
2. **Near-black over pure gray, one lit subject.** The corpus's dark 3D moods (`encrypter.jpg` #0a0a0a, `x-money-card.jpg` #050505, `flame-in-a-glass.jpg`, `3d-tornado-card-stream.jpg`) keep backgrounds at #050505–#141414 and spend all the light on one object. Darkness is a stage, not a fill color.
3. **Two neutrals plus one loud accent.** Recurring palette math: a warm off-white or near-black field, a mid neutral, and a single saturated hit — `factory-droid-identity.jpg` (#f2efe9 + #111 + red #e8380d), `organic-modulation.jpg` (#17171A + #F2F2F2 + #E8401C), `omou-stamp-characters.jpg` (#FCFBF9 + #33322E + #E4574E). When the accent floods the whole field instead (`the-cash-app-wand.jpg` electric green, `connection-signal.jpg` orange-red, `ultra-op-1-robotics-identity.jpg` solid orange), type drops to near-monochrome.
4. **Typography scale is binary: enormous or tiny, nothing in between.** Editorial pieces pair oversized display type with mono caption text and skip middle sizes — `trestle-inc.jpg` (giant serif wordmark, tiny footer links), `haoqi-wen.jpg` (huge grotesk + mono HUD labels), `anthrogen-labs-poster-series.jpg`, `juniq-brand-identity.jpg` (giant glyph + tiny manifesto). The jump in scale *is* the hierarchy.
5. **Texture earns trust — grain, halftone, and paper beat flat vectors.** Print-flavored works get their character from reproduction artifacts: risograph grain (`fifa-world-cup-posters.jpg`), newsprint dither (`tacit-labs-brand.jpg`, `about-page-design.jpg`), crumpled paper (`context-is-everything.jpg`), VHS scan grain (`doron-supply-rebrand.avif`), torn notebook paper (`industrial-modernization.jpg`). Texture is applied to the whole surface, not sprinkled as decoration.
6. **Make UI physical: components obey toy physics.** The most-shareable UI details borrow material behavior — jelly buttons with bubbles inside (`jello-button-microinteraction.jpg`), buttons tumbling in a physics pile (`button-pile.jpg`), a CTA that slots into a brick like a toy (`brick-button.jpg`), LEGO-brick notifications (`lego-dynamic-island.jpg`), page curls and sticker peels (`realistic-page-turn.jpg`, `sticker-peel-off-effect.jpg`). Softness, weight, and squish read as craft.
7. **Show the construction, not just the result.** Marks gain authority when the grid and bezier scaffolding stay visible: `quartr-brand-mark.jpg` (anchor points tracing "QU"), `wip-logo.jpg` (four-step circle-grid derivation), `lovable-testflight-icons.jpg` (icons with construction-grid overlays), `morphing-404-robot.jpg` (raw bezier outlines as the illustration).
8. **Technical spec-sheet dressing signals seriousness.** Mono type, numbered tables, registration marks, and classification labels turn brand collateral into engineering documents — `factory-droid-identity.jpg` (numbered 1-5 pipeline grid), `nomad-systems-label-design.jpg` (crate/ammo labels), `ultra-op-1-robotics-identity.jpg` (daily operation log), `system-studies.jpg` (INTERFACE/COMPONENT/DOMAIN/STATUS specs). Works best for industrial, AI, and dev-tool brands.
9. **Loading states are a stage, not dead time.** The corpus treats waiting as a branded moment: a particle burst beside "Calculating…" (`calculating-burst.jpg`), a serif percent counter over a wireframe monogram (`henry-labs.jpg`), striped-orb "Agent thinking.." variants (`thinking-states.jpg`), progressive "fetching metrics" skeletons (`live-data-loading-animation.jpg`). Give the wait one atmospheric element and quiet type.
10. **Iridescence and chrome are the current premium finish — use on one element only.** Holographic foil tickets (`holographic-ticket.jpg`), diffraction rings around a single button (`iridescent-home-button.jpg`), liquid-chrome lettering (`airbrush-typography.jpg`, `wonder-what-s-possible.jpg`), thermal-foil shards (`half-time.jpg`). It reads luxe when scarce and gaudy when tiled.
11. **Retro formats recontextualized beat retro styling.** The strongest nostalgia plays borrow a whole physical format: a music identity as a worn iPod (`sona-music-protocol-identity.jpg`), a brand as a perforated postage stamp (`stamps-brand-identity.jpg`), a film brand as a VHS box with price sticker (`blue-things-series.jpg`), gradients as a CD jewel case (`workos-cd-case-mockups.jpg`). The format supplies the story; don't just add scanlines.
12. **Photography needs a typographic counterweight.** Photo-led posters lock the image to hard-working type: motion-blurred sprinter with rotated repeated "Keep Running" (`noa-poster-system.jpg`), macro grapefruit with tiny mirrored captions (`natured-headlines.jpg`), Nike sole scan under a cobalt Swiss grid (`milano-design-week-2026.jpg`). The photo carries emotion; the grid carries credibility.

## How to navigate

- Every entry in `profiles/design.md` is one line: **filename** — one-sentence description · `style:` tags · `palette:` 2-3 hex values.
- The image/still itself lives at `media/design/<filename>` (e.g., `media/design/jello-button-microinteraction.jpg`). Read it with the Read tool when you need to see the actual work.
- Grep the profile by style tag to slice the corpus: `dark`, `minimal`, `typographic`, `3d`, `playful`, `vibrant`, `print`, `editorial`, `motion`, `glassmorphic`, `iridescent`, `brutalist`, `retro`, `pixel`, `grunge`, `mobile-ui`, `icon-design`, `fintech`. Note tags are delimited inconsistently (`/` in some entries, `, ` in others) — grep for the bare word.
- Grep by palette hex when matching an existing brand color (e.g., `#0a0a0a` finds the dark-luxe cluster; `#FF2D6C`/`#FF3E8A` the hot-pink accents).
- Filenames are descriptive slugs — searching `icon`, `poster`, `button`, `brand`, or `motion` in filenames alone is a fast first cut.
