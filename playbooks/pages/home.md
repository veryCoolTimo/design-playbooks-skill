# Home Page Playbook
> Coverage: 315 examples from corpus (analyzed 20)

## Anatomy

Ordered skeleton observed across the sample. Percentages = share of the 20 analyzed homes.

1. **Nav bar** (100%) — logo left, 4-6 text links, primary CTA button pinned right (aave "Launch App", glideapps "Sign up", heynds "Get Heynds"). Compact, single row, never a mega-hero nav.
2. **Hero** (100%) — H1 (4-9 words) + 1-2 sentence subline + 1-2 CTAs.
   - Centered hero: ~55% (cap, jitter, relate-so, seline, heynds, sprout, portalgaming, fold-money)
   - Left-aligned hero with visual on the right: ~45% (axelar-network, mux, coinflect, era-app, swagapp, oysterhr)
   - Hero visual is a real product screenshot/mockup: ~60% (cap, jitter, seline, relate-so, era-app, glideapps, mux, heynds)
   - Hero visual is a 3D/abstract render or pure atmosphere: ~30% (coinflect chrome chains, portalgaming glass cubes, aave aurora gradient, axelar isometric shapes)
   - Typography-only / type-as-hero: ~10% (tiblo giant black headline with floating cards, decideai marquee wordmark)
3. **Social-proof logo bar** (~55%) — directly under the hero, 5-10 grayscale logos with a one-line kicker (cap "Used by employees at leading tech companies", jitter, mux "We work with the world's top brands", mintlify, relate-so, oysterhr "Trusted by teams and partners"). Crypto variant: "Backers & Partners" twin panels (axelar-network).
4. **Primary feature block** (100%) — one of two shapes, often both:
   - Bento/card grid of 3-9 features: ~70% (liveblocks, cap, tiblo colored tiles, heynds 6-up, seline 3x3, oysterhr 4 pastel cards, coinflect)
   - Alternating text-left/visual-right rows: ~50% (axelar-network "How Axelar Works", era-app per-color sections, fold-money, glideapps, relate-so Prospect/Close/Retain/Recycle)
5. **Deep-dive / developer or product section** (~50%) — code snippets or real UI at large scale (mux "Video for developers" with code, liveblocks dev tooling, axelar API snippets, mintlify Web Editor, glideapps full-width app screenshots).
6. **Stats / numbers section** (~35%) — one huge number as anchor (aave "$20.8B locked" right under hero, swagapp "1M+ worldwide", glideapps $100k/$50k/800+, heynds 93%/86%/96%).
7. **Testimonials / quotes** (~60%) — either one oversized editorial pull-quote between sections (jitter, glideapps) or a card wall/tweet wall (liveblocks, heynds, tiblo, relate-so, oysterhr, mux stacked cards).
8. **Pricing on home** (~20%) — 2-tier simple table (era-app Free vs $8.99, tiblo $5/$8, heynds pay-once tiers).
9. **FAQ accordion** (~25%) — era-app, heynds, seline, tiblo; always near the bottom, right before the final CTA.
10. **Final CTA banner** (~85%) — full-width band or card repeating the hero promise + same CTAs (cap repeats hero verbatim in a card, mux giant orange "Start building for free", mintlify echoes hero art, heynds red banner, seline, jitter).
11. **Fat footer** (~95%) — 3-5 sitemap columns + legal + socials; several add a newsletter input (axelar-network, coinflect, oysterhr) or a giant wordmark (era-app, decideai marquee).

## Patterns that work

- **Show the actual product within the first viewport.** The strongest SaaS homes put a real, legible UI screenshot immediately under or beside the H1: cap embeds a live-looking recorder window on a photographic desktop, seline shows the full analytics dashboard with clickable tabs (Dashboard/Visitors/Journeys/Funnels), relate-so drops a full-width pipeline board right after the hero, era-app puts an annotated phone in the hero.
- **One giant number beats ten claims.** aave leads with "$20,888,734,186.29 of liquidity" as the first thing after the hero; swagapp renders "1M+" at display size; glideapps uses a 3-stat strip ($100,000 / $50,000 / 800+). Numbers are set 3-5x body-headline size.
- **Narrative section naming turns features into a story.** relate-so structures the page as Prospect. → Close. → Retain. → Recycle., one verb per section; era-app walks insight → suggestion → automation with a phone screen per step. Each step gets its own background color or screenshot.
- **Color-code sections to pace a long scroll.** era-app alternates black/sage/olive/lavender full-bleed bands, sprout rotates yellow/cream/orange/amber/plum, swagapp stays in one purple family but alternates tint intensity. The section boundary is the full viewport width, not a card.
- **Logo bar earns its place with a kicker line.** cap ("you're in great company"), mux ("We work with the world's top brands"), oysterhr ("Trusted by teams and partners") all caption the logos; axelar splits them into labeled "Backers" and "Partners" boxes — the label does the persuasion, logos are just evidence.
- **Repeat the hero as the closing CTA.** cap literally reuses the hero headline, subline, and both buttons in a rounded sky-textured card at the bottom; mintlify mirrors its hero art; mux escalates it into a full-orange band with an oversized button. The reader who scrolled everything gets the same ask with zero new cognitive load.
- **Interleave oversized pull-quotes as palate cleansers.** jitter drops two display-size customer quotes (with scribble highlights on key words) between feature blocks; glideapps opens with a quote + photo of a real customer machine. One quote, huge type, name + role — not a carousel.
- **Developer products show code, not diagrams.** axelar puts two runnable snippets (Universal Asset Transfer / General Message Passing) above the fold-adjacent area; mux and liveblocks render syntax-highlighted editors at full section width; heynds shows the actual shortcut UI. Code presence signals "real API" instantly.
- **Type-as-hero works when the product is simple.** tiblo sets "Turn your tasks into calendar blocks" at ~200px black weight and floats three small colored product cards between the words — no hero image needed; decideai does the same with a marquee wordmark band. Works only with a one-sentence value prop.
- **FAQ + simple 2-tier pricing close the loop for self-serve tools.** era-app, tiblo, and heynds all run pricing → FAQ → final CTA as the last three sections, answering objections exactly where the buy decision happens.

## Variants by style

- **Light minimal (jitter, relate-so, seline, cap, fold-money):** white/near-white ground, one brand hue, centered hero, product screenshot as the only large graphic. Sections separated by whitespace, not color. Quotes and logo bars carry the persuasion. Buttons are small and rounded; overall density is low and line lengths short.
- **Dark tech (aave, liveblocks, mintlify, coinflect, decideai, glideapps ~50/50):** near-black ground with one luminous accent (aave aurora gradient, liveblocks pink→purple gradient text, mintlify ambient green photography, coinflect fiery chrome 3D). Feature content lives in slightly-lighter cards (#111 on #000). Big stat sections and security/audit proof are common (aave's auditor hexagons, treasury chart). Glideapps alternates dark and light bands to reset attention.
- **Colorful / playful (oysterhr, sprout, swagapp, era-app):** full-bleed pastel or saturated section bands, illustration over photography (oysterhr hand-drawn globe and sunburst testimonial band, sprout collage frames, swagapp sticker-collage phones), rounded cards in candy palettes (oysterhr's mint/coral/yellow/lavender benefit cards). Serif or quirky display faces allowed (sprout's editorial serif, oysterhr's italic serif accents inside the headline).
- **Neo-brutalist / industrial (mux, tiblo, axelar-network):** visible grid borders and hairline dividers (mux draws every section boundary), mono/uppercase micro-labels ("WE SPEAK YOUR LANGUAGE:", "CROSS-CHAIN PLATFORM"), flat primary colors (orange/blue/black), condensed or ultra-heavy grotesque headlines, marquee tickers (axelar's grant announcement ribbon). CTA bands in solid brand color at full width.
- **Editorial mono/terminal (decideai):** black ground, monospace labels and index numbers (001/002/003), comparison table as a design object, wireframe 3D line renders instead of imagery, giant marquee wordmark used as a section divider.

## Anti-patterns

- **No stock photography anywhere in the sample.** Every visual is product UI, custom 3D, or custom illustration. Generic people-at-laptops photos would immediately break the pattern.
- **No carousels for core content.** Testimonials are stacked/gridded (heynds, tiblo, liveblocks); the only sliders are secondary (aave markets arrows, mux quote cards) and content remains readable without interacting.
- **No walls of small text.** Weakest moments in the sample are coinflect's dapployable section (four plain text columns, no visuals — visibly flatter than its 3D sections) and axelar's dense paragraph blocks; the strong pages cap body copy at 1-2 sentences per feature.
- **No unanchored abstract heroes.** Crypto sites that open with pure 3D (coinflect, portalgaming) still name the concrete offering in the H1; but coinflect's headline ("One Network Three Chains. Join the Addismya Testnet") buries the user benefit — compare aave, which pairs its gradient with the plain "Earn interest, borrow assets, and build applications."
- **Don't put more than 2 CTAs in the hero.** Every sampled hero has exactly 1-2 buttons; secondary is always visually quieter (cap's "Get Started" vs "Download App", mux outline vs filled).
- **Don't skip the final CTA.** The few pages that end weakly (coinflect ends on a text-only "Join us" with social icons) feel unfinished next to mux/cap/mintlify's full-width closing bands.
- **Don't mix accent hues without a system.** Sampled pages hold to one accent (heynds orange, relate-so blue, swagapp purple) or a fixed 3-color set applied to tiles (tiblo orange/blue/black); random per-section accent colors don't appear.
- **Navigation stays out of the way.** No sampled site uses a tall hero-integrated nav, hamburger-on-desktop (except swagapp's deliberate pill menu), or more than ~6 top-level items.

## Checklist

1. Nav: logo left, ≤6 links, one filled CTA button right; keep it one slim row.
2. Hero: benefit-first H1 (≤9 words), 1-2 sentence subline, primary + optional secondary CTA, and a reason-to-believe microcopy line if self-serve ("No credit card required" — cap).
3. Put a real product visual in or immediately after the hero: screenshot, phone mockup, or (crypto/infra) a branded 3D render — never stock photos.
4. Add a logo bar with a kicker caption within the first two scroll depths (5-10 grayscale logos).
5. Build the feature story as either a bento grid (3-9 cards, one line of copy each) or 3-5 alternating rows, each with its own screenshot; name sections with verbs if there's a workflow (relate-so pattern).
6. Include one oversized proof element: a giant stat (aave, swagapp) or a display-size pull-quote with attribution (jitter).
7. Dev-facing product? Show real syntax-highlighted code at section scale (mux, axelar, liveblocks).
8. Pace long pages with full-bleed background shifts (color bands or dark/light alternation) every 1-2 sections.
9. Self-serve tool? Add simple 2-tier pricing + FAQ accordion as the penultimate sections (era-app, tiblo, heynds).
10. Close with a full-width CTA band that repeats the hero promise and buttons; follow with a fat footer (sitemap columns, socials, legal, optional newsletter input or giant wordmark).

## Best references

- **cap** — textbook light SaaS home: hero → product window → logo bar → bento → hero-echo CTA card, all in one coherent sky-blue world.
- **relate-so** — verb-named narrative sections (Prospect/Close/Retain/Recycle) with real UI per step; cleanest feature storytelling in the sample.
- **mux** — industrial grid system, mono labels, code sections, verticals tabs, and the boldest closing CTA band; best dev-tool pacing.
- **aave** — dark-tech trust stack: gradient hero, $20B stat, market cards, auditors, treasury data — how to sell infrastructure with proof.
- **era-app** — color-banded scroll rhythm with one phone screen per band; best mobile-app home and a clean pricing+FAQ close.
- **liveblocks** — dark bento done right: gradient display type, dot-matrix motif, feature cards with mini-UIs, tweet-wall social proof.
- **tiblo** — type-as-hero neo-brutalism; proves a home page can work with zero hero imagery if the value prop is one sentence.
- **oysterhr** — colorful/playful benchmark: pastel benefit cards, custom illustration, sunburst testimonial band, warm without losing structure.
