# Misc Page Playbooks
> Coverage: mixed, 5-14 examples per type — see per-section badges

## careers  ·  coverage: 13 examples
**Anatomy:** Mission-first hero (statement headline or brand-culture visual), then either a benefits grid or a straight job-listing table grouped by department. Culture proof (team photos, values, perks) sits between hero and roles; page often ends at the roles list with no heavy CTA band.

**Patterns that work:**
- Grouped job table with inline compensation: `cluely` lists roles under ENGINEERING / GROWTH / OTHER category labels, each row showing location, on-site status, salary and equity range, with a right-aligned "Apply now" button — high-trust, scannable.
- Benefits as an icon grid: `astro-build` uses a 4x2 grid of small colorful icons with 2-3 word labels (remote-first, PTO, stipends), which fills the page even when there are zero open roles.
- Culture-led storytelling before roles: `clay-earth` opens with founders' hand-drawn signatures ("work we're proud to sign our name on") and a black-and-white team photo collage — careers as brand piece, jobs pushed to a link.

**Best references:** `cluely` (playful sticker-collage hero + rigorous transparent job table), `astro-build` (minimal mission + benefits grid, gracefully handles "no open roles").

## customers  ·  coverage: 12 examples
**Anatomy:** Hero states the aggregate claim ("Meet our customers", "Powering the leaders"), optionally with a logo strip or headline metrics band. Body is either a masonry of quote cards or alternating case-study rows (photo/product shot + metrics + quote). Ends in a conversion CTA.

**Patterns that work:**
- Metric-anchored case rows: `codex` alternates left/right story blocks, each with a product visual, an all-caps claim headline, and 3 stat callouts (15 sec, $50,000+, 500M) plus a "read customer story" button — every story is quantified.
- Quote-card masonry into a giant logo wall: `dub` stacks ~12 testimonial cards (bolded key phrase inside each quote, name/title/avatar) then a 3-column grid of 60+ logo tiles ending with an "Add your company" tile — social proof at two zoom levels.
- Themed story clusters: `clearbit` groups case studies under outcome headings ("Drive more traffic", "Increase form conversion") with one large featured card plus compact logo-led snippets per section.

**Best references:** `codex` (bold stat-driven storytelling, neon accent card for aggregate numbers), `dub` (quotes + exhaustive logo wall, low-maintenance and scalable).

## enterprise  ·  coverage: 11 examples
**Anatomy:** Hero pitches scale/reliability ("Built for the enterprise stack", "Reliability platform for the world's best") with a demo CTA and enterprise logo strip. Body is a trust-feature grid — SOC 2, SSO/SAML, encryption, SLA, support — plus one flagship customer quote with a big metric. Dark "book a demo" band closes.

**Patterns that work:**
- Compliance feature cells with custom illustrations: `antimetal` renders SOC 2, pentesting, backups, encryption, SSO, and EDP management as six illustrated cards inside a hairline-grid, keeping dry content visually rich.
- Concierge support as a differentiator section: `betterstack` dedicates a full section to "Expert engineering support" (24/7, migration help) plus a cost-comparison module against Datadog/CloudWatch — enterprise pages sell people and price, not just features.
- Badges as brand objects: `clay-com` redraws SOC 2 / GDPR / CCPA / ISO badges as playful flower shapes matching its illustrated world, then pairs them with a customer ROI card (40h/week, 4x, $34K+).

**Best references:** `antimetal` (clean light grid, security cells + testimonial photo card + dark CTA finale), `betterstack` (dark, benefit-sectioned, support and cost-savings angle).

## platform  ·  coverage: 9 examples
**Anatomy:** Umbrella-level hero ("the platform behind everything") with one hero product shot, then a features bento or long alternating feature sections, each pairing UI screenshots with capability copy. Integration/logo section and a consultative CTA near the end.

**Patterns that work:**
- Lifecycle framing: `ada` organizes the platform as a measure→improve loop (circular dial diagram) then walks through stages (preview, improve, automate) with cropped UI shots — a narrative spine instead of a flat feature list.
- Mixed-size bento with data-viz: `amra` fills a "Platform features" grid with real-feeling charts (dot cluster, bar trend, node flow) and platform logos, making an abstract data platform tangible.
- Segment-by-monitoring-type sections: `betterstack` splits the platform into labeled chapters (UPTIME MONITORING, TRANSACTION MONITORING, HEARTBEAT MONITORING) each with eyebrow label, headline, screenshot, and 2-3 supporting mini-features.

**Best references:** `betterstack` (dark chaptered structure with eyebrow labels — easy to clone), `ada` (color-blocked feature rows + circular platform diagram).

## templates  ·  coverage: 9 examples
**Anatomy:** Utility hero ("Browse templates", "Free design templates ready to edit") with minimal copy, straight into category-grouped thumbnail grids. Each card = live preview + name + one-line description. Product-pitch CTA band at the bottom, sometimes FAQ.

**Patterns that work:**
- Category rows with horizontal overflow: `artboard-studio` stacks sections (Mockups, Instagram Story, Animation…) each with a "See all" link and a carousel of real thumbnails — the artwork is the design.
- Faithful in-product previews: `paradigmai` renders each template as a mini spreadsheet with real rows, colored status chips, and gradient headers inside a hairline grid — previews double as a product demo.
- Sidebar-filtered gallery: `jitter` uses a left category rail + two-column thumbnail grid, followed by an FAQ accordion and a big "Try Jitter today" type-driven CTA.

**Best references:** `paradigmai` (editorial serif hero + hyper-real template cards on hairline grid), `artboard-studio` (dense multi-category showcase that scales to hundreds of templates).

## about-us  ·  coverage: 9 examples
**Anatomy:** Mission statement hero (often typographic, no product), then some mix of: team/leadership grid with photos and LinkedIn links, story/values prose, investors and advisors, closing hire-us or join-us CTA. The most text-forward page type in the corpus.

**Patterns that work:**
- Leadership → board → backers ladder: `clutch` sequences founder bios, board members with VC logos, industry-expert endorsements, then a careers CTA — credibility stacked in descending order of proximity.
- Full-team mosaic with personality: `fly` lists every employee as a card (photo, flag, role, GitHub/Twitter icons) under an illustrated painterly hero — humanizing at scale, zero prose.
- Type-as-hero manifesto: `fonts-ninja` fills the viewport with "Discover, Create, Inspire." display type plus cursor stickers, followed by bold paragraph blocks — no photos at all, voice carries the page.

**Best references:** `clutch` (structured trust ladder for B2B/security), `fonts-ninja` (pure typographic manifesto for brand-led products).

## products  ·  coverage: 8 examples
**Anatomy:** Single-product page inside a multi-product suite: hero names the product and its one-liner, trust logo strip, then feature sections (screenshot + copy), stats or "at a glance" band, FAQ accordion, and a themed get-started CTA.

**Patterns that work:**
- Stat dashboard band: `aave` shows "GHO at a glance" as four cards mixing numbers with matching mini-visualizations (gauge, line, dot field) in one brand hue — product health as design.
- Repeating screenshot-left rows with FAQ: `adaline` runs Editor/Playground/Datasets rows with consistent framed screenshots, then an 11-question FAQ accordion — thin content stretched credibly.
- Use-case grid + code: `cohere` alternates "What's possible" use-case cards, a code snippet block on dark, resource/article cards, and a playground screenshot — one product shown to both developers and buyers.

**Best references:** `aave` (monochrome-green system: dot motifs, gauges, FAQ — extremely cohesive), `cohere` (editorial grid with numbered "Why" list and gradient artwork).

## integrations  ·  coverage: 7 examples
**Anatomy:** Short hero ("Integrations", one-line value prop, CTA), then a logo-card grid — cards carry app icon, name, one-line description, sometimes install counts or category tags. Filters/search appear at ~50+ items; "build your own / submit" module is standard.

**Patterns that work:**
- Sidebar + card grid with counts: `astro-build` pairs a search + category filter rail and a "Submit an integration" panel with a 3-column grid where each card shows download counts and verified badges — marketplace credibility.
- Grouped by role: `cycle` splits integrations into "Sources" and "Collaboration" sections of large soft-gray icon tiles with tag pills and "Learn more" links — small catalogs look intentional, not sparse.
- Featured tier + long tail: `gitbook` leads with two large "Featured" screenshot cards (Linear, Slack), then compact icon rows, then a "Pre-installed and ready to use" set — three tiers of prominence in one page.

**Best references:** `astro-build` (full marketplace pattern: search, filter, counts, submit CTA), `gitbook` (featured/standard/pre-installed hierarchy for medium catalogs).

## community  ·  coverage: 7 examples
**Anatomy:** Invitation hero ("Join the community"), channel cards (Discord/X/GitHub) with per-channel CTAs, then people proof — member/evangelist avatar grids, events, community stats — and a contribute/host-your-own CTA.

**Patterns that work:**
- Channel cards as the primary nav: `holograph` gives Discord/X/Mirror equal boxed cards with icon, description, and monospace "Join" links, plus a full-width ticker band repeating the three channels.
- Faces + numbers: `lottiefiles` shows a huge evangelist avatar grid (name, country flag, social icons), then a "Global impact" stat band (30+ evangelists, 80+ events), then event video cards and partner logos — people first, program second.
- Community as content library: `betterstack` structures its community page as a guides library with a left topic rail and dense article list, closing with a "Join the writer's program" module — contribution framed as the community.

**Best references:** `lottiefiles` (people-dense program page: avatars, events, partners, stats), `holograph` (minimal brutalist channel-hub for crypto/dev audiences).

## contact  ·  coverage: 5 examples
**Anatomy:** One-viewport page: friendly headline ("Get in Touch", "Hey! Let's deploy."), then either a short form (name/email/message) or routing cards splitting sales vs. support vs. docs. Social links and office address in or near the footer; almost no scrolling.

**Patterns that work:**
- Route-splitting panels instead of a form: `buddy-works` divides the page into "Talk to our expert" (chat CTA) and "Help & support" (email button) plus Guides/Docs/API tiles and a live status pill — every visitor self-sorts in one glance.
- Single centered form on brand atmosphere: `openserv` floats a minimal 3-field form over a dark gradient with "We're active here too" social row — fastest to build, feels finished.
- Contact as partner-recruitment: `amplemarket` turns a contact-ish page into an Apply/Plan/Earn timeline, "Who can apply" checklist chips, and a testimonial slider — use when the page's real job is lead qualification.

**Best references:** `buddy-works` (playful routing hub with status badge and giant outline wordmark footer), `openserv` (minimal dark form done cleanly).

## for-teams  ·  coverage: 5 examples
**Anatomy:** Segment-pitch landing: hero reframes the core product for teams ("Automate your design process at any scale"), trust logo strip, then alternating benefit sections with product screenshots, a differentiator grid, testimonials from team leads, and a demo CTA.

**Patterns that work:**
- Quantified team benefits: `artboard-studio` headlines each section with an operational win ("Increase your efficiency by up to 80%", "No more email ping pong") — collaboration pain, not feature names.
- Highlighted-phrase headlines: `canopy-is` marks the key phrase of every section headline with a highlighter background ("the fastest onramp", "most critical skills"), tying the page together and keeping long copy scannable; role-specific testimonials (VP, manager) with 95%/93% stat pair.
- Workflow-adaptation demo: `tana` shows the actual team workspace (calendar, tasks, brand-asset hub) in oversized blue-framed screenshots with interleaved practitioner quotes — proof by interface.

**Best references:** `canopy-is` (dark, highlight-marker system + role-matched social proof), `artboard-studio` (warm light version with metric-led sections and integration close).

## security  ·  coverage: 5 examples
**Anatomy:** Declarative hero ("Security", "A public cloud for security nerds") with the audit/compliance claim up top. Body enumerates measures — hardware isolation, encryption, SOC 2, pentests, passkeys — as numbered rows or illustrated sections, citing named auditors/partners. Ends with newsletter/demo CTA.

**Patterns that work:**
- Numbered measure list: `ctrl` lays out 01-04 pill-shaped rows (Ledger support, dApp scanning, transaction simulation/analysis), each with an icon, plain-language explanation, and a named provider link (Blowfish, Tenderly) — attribution builds trust.
- Illustrated narrative tour: `fly` walks isolation → security team → encryption → SOC 2 → pentesting as a story with hand-drawn artwork and named pentest firms, then a features grid and "we're happy to jump on a call" card — technical depth without sterility.
- Verify-don't-trust receipts: `pass-app` ends with a "Security you don't just trust, you verify" block linking the actual dated audit report, after explaining passkey infrastructure and its Turnkey partner in depth.

**Best references:** `fly` (personality-rich yet substantive; the benchmark for making compliance readable), `ctrl` (crisp numbered checklist format that works at small scale).
