# Solutions Page Playbook
> Coverage: 21 examples from corpus (analyzed 20)

Two archetypes exist in the corpus. **A) Targeted solution page** (~80%): the whole page sells the product to one audience or use case ("Front for Technology", "Kindsight for Arts & Culture", "Gamma for Consultants", "Lithic: Bill Pay & AP", "Tines: Security", "turn: chat for healthcare"). **B) Solutions hub/index** (~20%): a router page listing solutions by role/industry (jasper's accordions, uizard's persona rows, trustditto's tabbed card directory). Decide which you are building first — mixing them produces a mushy page.

## Anatomy

Ordered skeleton for archetype A (targeted page); frequencies out of 20 analyzed:

1. **Nav + eyebrow label naming the segment** — 75%. Small pill/kicker above the H1: "Front for Technology", "Gamma for Consultants", "Bill Pay & Accounts Payable" (lithic), "SECURITY" (tines), "Enterprise Testing Solutions" (saucelabs), "Internal Knowledge Base" (gitbook), "Agencies" (lattice), "For Your Users" (brainfishai).
2. **Hero: outcome headline + 1–2 sentence subhead + primary CTA (often paired with secondary)** — 100%. Split layout with product screenshot/photo right ~50% (front, clay-com, paradigmai, brainfishai, gamma); centered ~50% (lithic, gitbook, notion, tines, specifyapp, uizard, trustditto).
3. **Social proof strip immediately after hero** — 55%: logo bar (gotomorro, lattice, specifyapp, turn, brainfishai), logos + count ("9,000+ businesses" front, "50% of Fortune 500" notion), or hero-adjacent stat pair (brainfishai's "50% decrease in CTR to human assistance").
4. **Benefit trio / quick-scan value props** — 60%. Three icon+blurb columns (front, saucelabs, gamma's 4 use-case cards, clay-com's 4-col blurbs, artboard-studio's 3-col illustrated cards).
5. **Alternating feature rows: heading + copy + bullets one side, product screenshot the other** — 70%. Core body of the page (artboard-studio, front, kindsight, gotomorro, specifyapp, lattice, clay-com, gitbook, paradigmai).
6. **Proof interlude mid-page: big pull quote, stat panel, or case study** — 70%. Quote with attribution (tines, kindsight, front, gamma, notion), stat trio (gamma's 95% / $47M / 500+, front's 0% churn stats, notion's 98%/70%/32%/86%), challenge→solution case block (gotomorro's Evaneos, saucelabs' Verizon Media).
7. **Comparison / "with vs. without" section** — 25% but high-impact: front's Front-vs-legacy-helpdesk checklist table, brainfishai's "What Users Get / How Brainfish Delivers It" table and "without Brainfish" pain section, clay-com's with/without data-acquisition split.
8. **Adjacent use cases or teams** — 40%: gotomorro's "Designed for the entire company" (Legal/Sales/Finance team cards), paradigmai's "Other use cases to explore", front's "Unify across teams" quadrant, turn's "Life-saving solutions" 5-up.
9. **Resources/related content cards** — 50%: clay-com, gotomorro, kindsight, lattice, lithic ("Latest Updates"), saucelabs, shopify, front.
10. **Closing CTA banner, visually loud** — 95%. Full-width colored/gradient band or boxed card with restated promise + CTA (lithic's orange band, saucelabs' yellow band, brainfishai's gradient demo mock, gamma's immersive artwork, clay-com's illustrated landscape, tines' boxed card with illustration).
11. **Deep footer** — 100%; several use an oversized wordmark as a sign-off (brainfishai, turn, gamma).

Archetype B skeleton: centered hero → grouping device (tabs: trustditto; accordions by Role then Industry: jasper; alternating persona rows with "Read more" links: uizard) → social proof logo bar → one or two shared product sections → closing CTA.

## Patterns that work

- **Name the audience in the first 100px.** The eyebrow "X for Y" pattern (front, gamma, kindsight, lattice, specifyapp) instantly confirms the visitor is in the right place; the H1 then sells the outcome, not the feature ("Ditch your legacy help desk" — front; "Less tedious tasks. More room for creativity" — artboard-studio).
- **Quantify early and often.** brainfishai puts two stats under the hero subhead; gamma embeds a dark stats panel (95% retention, $47M, 500+) with client logos before the first scroll ends; turn leads a feature with "16x more engagement on WhatsApp than SMS". Numbers beat adjectives on these pages.
- **Show the actual product in every feature row.** Real UI screenshots in rounded cards, often on tinted or color-blocked backgrounds: clay-com's blue/green/yellow/orange feature cards, lattice's screenshots on gradient bands, paradigmai's spreadsheet captures. Weakest sections in the corpus are the ones with icon-only grids and no product evidence.
- **Sequence the workflow as numbered steps.** paradigmai walks Step 1→4 (Ask, Enrich, Surface, Draft & Send) with a screenshot per step; lattice uses an 01/02/03 stepper; gotomorro's "Start today, deploy next week" shows Set-up → Training → Deployment. This converts a feature list into a story of adoption.
- **Use a comparison table to frame the switch.** front's side-by-side "Front vs. Help desk" checklist and brainfishai's "What Users Get / How Brainfish Delivers It" table give switchers a rational artifact to forward internally. Pair it with a problem section ("How Traditional Support Fails Users" — brainfishai; "Don't let a legacy help desk slow you down" — front).
- **Make the mid-page quote a design moment, not a gray box.** kindsight sets its quote on a lime block with the customer's logo at poster scale ("FIELD."); tines pairs an oversized serif quote with a "93 days" stat card; gamma runs the quote as a full-width dark band with a portrait. Testimonials treated as section-level art get read.
- **Segment-appropriate imagery.** Solutions pages targeting a vertical show that vertical's people: gotomorro photographs legal/sales/finance teams, turn shows a mother and child for healthcare, kindsight shows museum staff. Product-led pages (lithic, specifyapp) skip people entirely and diagram the mechanics — both work; mismatch doesn't.
- **For hub pages, make the grouping device do the work.** jasper's paired accordions (Solutions by Role, then inverted dark Solutions by Industry) keep 10 destinations scannable in two viewports; trustditto's status badges (ACTIVE / COMING SOON) plus per-card CTAs turn a directory into a roadmap; uizard gives each persona row its own screenshot and "Read more" link.
- **End with a loud, on-brand CTA band that restates the promise.** saucelabs (yellow), lithic (orange with doodle pattern), brainfishai (gradient with product mock), gamma (full-bleed illustrated universe). Nearly every strong page repeats the primary CTA 3+ times: hero, mid-page, closing. artboard-studio adds "No credit card is required" microcopy under the final button.

## Variants by style

- **Light minimal / SaaS-clean** (clay-com, front, lattice, notion, lithic, uizard, jasper): white or near-white base, one accent color, product screenshots carry the visual load. Feature rows alternate left/right; color arrives via tinted screenshot backdrops (clay-com), soft gradients (front, lattice), or a single saturated CTA band (lithic). Typography is a neutral sans; density is high but whitespace generous.
- **Dark tech** (gitbook, specifyapp, and jasper/paradigmai/turn's dark sections): near-black base, glowing gradients or 3D floating objects in the hero (specifyapp's tokens, gitbook's aurora), monospace/technical eyebrow labels, screenshots in dark chrome. These pages lean on atmosphere for credibility and keep body sections sparse; the closing CTA is often a light card inverted against the dark (gitbook).
- **Colorful / playful** (artboard-studio, brainfishai, saucelabs, tines, turn, shopify, gamma): a signature saturated hue owns the hero band (artboard-studio lime, saucelabs yellow, turn deep green→purple), sections alternate background colors instead of white-on-white, custom illustration and pastel cards fill non-product sections (tines' template library, saucelabs' iso-illustration). brainfishai adds neo-brutalist borders/shadows and a wavy logo-marquee divider. These pages change background color at nearly every section boundary to pace the scroll.
- **Editorial serif** (kindsight, paradigmai, trustditto, tines' headlines): serif display type, muted paper/cream or olive palettes, oversized numerals (kindsight's 1–6 feature grid), hairline grid rules (paradigmai's visible column borders), quotes treated typographically. Product screenshots still appear but framed like plates in a magazine. Best fit for premium, trust-heavy, or culture-adjacent audiences.

## Anti-patterns

- **Generic hero with no audience marker.** uizard's hub hero is literally the word "Solutions" plus boilerplate — the weakest opening in the corpus; every strong page names who it's for or what changes for them.
- **Icon grids with no product evidence.** specifyapp's "Why use Specify?" is six near-identical blue icons with one-line labels — it reads as filler between the hero and the real content. When a benefits grid appears in stronger pages it's illustrated per-cell (artboard-studio) or backed by adjacent screenshots (saucelabs).
- **No social proof anywhere.** lithic ships zero logos, quotes, or stats — defensible for a developer audience but conspicuously bare next to the rest of the corpus; everyone else finds at least one proof device. Don't skip all three.
- **Stats without context.** front's "0%" stat row is visually striking but the meaning ("0% of what?") only emerges from tiny caption text — make the metric's object legible at headline size (compare gamma's labeled "95% Client Retention").
- **One CTA verb for two intents.** Pages selling to both self-serve and enterprise pair CTAs ("Try for Free" + "Request a demo" — front, gamma, paradigmai); pages offering only "Book a demo" for a low-commitment product (specifyapp) create friction.
- **Endless same-rhythm alternating rows.** notion and uizard run long stretches of identically shaped sections; the corpus's best pages break rhythm every 2–3 sections with a color band, quote poster, comparison table, or stats panel (brainfishai, clay-com, kindsight).
- **Hub pages that dead-end.** Avoid listing personas without a per-item link or status; trustditto (per-card CTA + ACTIVE badge) and jasper (per-accordion "Solutions for X" button) show the fix.
- **Burying the segment proof.** Putting the only customer logos in the footer or last scroll; the corpus norm is proof within the first 1.5 viewports (gotomorro, lattice, turn, front).

## Checklist

1. Pick the archetype: targeted page (one audience, narrative scroll) or hub (router with tabs/accordions/rows). Don't blend.
2. Eyebrow label "ProductName for [Audience]" + outcome-first H1 + subhead + primary CTA (add secondary "demo/free" pair if both motions exist).
3. Proof within 1.5 viewports: logo bar, customer count, or 1–2 hard stats tied to the hero claim.
4. 3–5 alternating feature rows, each with a real product screenshot (tinted/rounded container), a benefit heading, and 2–3 scannable bullets.
5. Insert one rhythm-breaker per 2–3 sections: full-bleed quote poster with logo/portrait, labeled stat trio, or a with/without comparison table.
6. If displacing an incumbent, add a problem section + side-by-side comparison checklist (front, brainfishai pattern).
7. Show workflow as numbered steps (Step 1→N with screenshots) when the product is a process (paradigmai, lattice, gotomorro).
8. Add an adjacent-use-cases or teams section linking sideways to sibling solutions pages (gotomorro, paradigmai, jasper).
9. Optional resources row (3–4 cards: guide, case study, blog) before the close (clay-com, saucelabs, kindsight).
10. Close with a full-width, high-saturation CTA band restating the promise, repeating the hero CTA, plus trust microcopy ("No credit card required" — artboard-studio).
