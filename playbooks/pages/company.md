# Company Page Playbook
> Coverage: 20 examples from corpus (analyzed 20)

## Anatomy

Ordered skeleton observed across the corpus. A company page is a mission-first narrative page, not a product page — the H1 states what the company believes or is building, almost never the word "About."

1. **Mission-statement hero** (100%) — nav + large headline phrased as a mission or belief ("Making cloud development work like magic" — modal; "We are engineers, making the tools we always wanted" — betterstack; "Real business insight happens in spreadsheets" — equals). ~85% skip "About Us" as the H1 entirely; only gitbook uses "About us," and only as a secondary heading under a mission headline. ~40% add an eyebrow label ("Our mission", "Our story", "About us") above the H1 (jasper, liveblocks, scenery, spellbook, synthesia).
2. **Founding story / manifesto in long-form prose** (60%) — 2-5 paragraphs of first-person narrative: why the company exists, often the founders' prior struggle (lithic's Privacy.com origin, liveblocks' InVision story, usetola's SMB letter). Equals and betterstack sign it like a letter ("— Team Equals", handwritten "Better Stack Team").
3. **Real team photography** (75%) — one of two modes: candid photo collage/strip (betterstack polaroids, gitbook B&W mosaic, lithic bar-night strip, modal carousel, clay-com marquee, jasper all-hands panorama, amra scattered snapshots) or a single big group photo (liveblocks, gitbook). Zero stock photography in the corpus.
4. **Values / operating principles** (55%) — 4-8 items, each with a short name plus 1-3 explanatory sentences: numbered 2x2 grid (amra, liveblocks), accordion (betterstack), bold color cards (clay-com), acronym letters K-I-N-D (kindsight), radical-transparency card grid (outseta).
5. **Stats band** (35%) — 3-4 large numbers: founded year / funding / users (betterstack, liveblocks), customers / Fortune 500 share (jasper), community metrics (lattice), "big numbers" cards (kindsight).
6. **Team roster or leadership grid** (60%) — either leadership-only with bios (amra, kindsight, lattice, jasper) or the whole team with names, roles, and social links (height-app, equals, synthesia, liveblocks). Equals adds name-tag stickers on headshots; synthesia captions each "Hi, I'm X."
7. **Investors / backers** (55%) — logo row, frequently upgraded with named angels and their titles (betterstack, modal, lattice board prose). usetola replaces investor logos with an app-icon marquee of products the team previously built (Stripe, PayPal, Klarna) — credibility via alumni instead of backers. scenery does both: "Backed by" logos + "Teammates from" logos.
8. **Press / news / awards** (30%) — news card row (amra, jasper, spellbook), Featured/Published link lists (lithic), recent interviews + downloadable media kit (height-app), award badges (kindsight, lattice).
9. **Careers block** (75%) — inline list of actual open roles (betterstack's ~20-role list with stats sidebar, amra, gitbook role cards, getrepeat) or a colored CTA band to a careers page (scenery yellow band, synthesia blue band, kindsight dark green band, lattice office-locations grid).
10. **Closing CTA** (95%) — either a careers CTA ("Join our mission") or a hand-off back to product ("Ship your first app in minutes" — modal; free-trial form — spellbook; "Get started with Outseta today").

## Patterns that work

- **Lead with a belief, not a label.** The strongest heroes state a thesis the reader can agree with: "Real business insight happens in spreadsheets" (equals), "Making video a team sport" (scenery), "We help lawyers do more of the work they love" (spellbook). The company name barely appears in the H1.
- **Write the origin story as a signed letter.** First-person prose ending in a signature reads as sincere and is cheap to produce: equals ("— Team Equals"), betterstack (handwritten-font sign-off), usetola and lithic (founder-struggle narrative). It replaces an entire "timeline" section.
- **Candid photos beat headshots for culture, headshots beat candids for trust.** Culture-selling pages scatter off-sites and polaroids (betterstack, gitbook, clay-com, lithic); trust-selling pages use formal leadership grids with LinkedIn links (kindsight, lattice, amra). Several do both, candids first (amra, lattice).
- **Upgrade the investor row with named angels.** Logos alone are wallpaper; betterstack, modal, and lattice list individual angels with their titles ("Lachy Groom — ex-Stripe", "Barr Moses — CEO, Monte Carlo"), which reads as social proof from operators, not just funds.
- **Put real open roles on the page, not just a "Careers" button.** betterstack lists ~20 roles next to founded/users/funding stats; amra, gitbook, and getrepeat inline role cards with location/remote tags. Pages that only link out (lithic) feel thinner.
- **A 3-4 number stats band compresses credibility.** "2021 Founded / 200,000+ Developers / $28.6M Funding" (betterstack), liveblocks' sidebar stats inside the story, jasper's "How we show up" row. Keep labels one word-ish and numbers huge.
- **Values need explanations and structure, not adjectives.** Every values section in the corpus pairs each value with sentences and a visual system: numbered grid (amra), expandable accordion (betterstack), full-color statement cards (clay-com), acronym (kindsight). outseta goes furthest — publishing salary policy ($210k standardized pay) and org principles as values.
- **Alumni credibility works when you're early.** scenery ("Teammates from" Yahoo/Apple/Adobe/Twitter + founder acquisition history) and usetola (icon marquee of fintech products the team built) substitute team pedigree for missing traction metrics.
- **Close with a fork: join us or try the product.** modal, spellbook, gitbook, jasper, and outseta end with a product CTA after the careers content; scenery, kindsight, and synthesia end on a high-contrast "join our team" color band. Never end on the press section.
- **Departmental team grouping scales better than one giant grid.** clay-com's "Meet our teams" (Engineering / Marketing / GTM / Brand...) with per-team blurbs stays readable; synthesia's 30+ person flat grid gets monotonous by row four.

## Variants by style

- **Light minimal** (gitbook, lattice, synthesia, jasper, amra, usetola, outseta): centered mission H1 on white/near-white, generous whitespace, B&W or desaturated team photography, one accent color reserved for CTAs and stat numbers. Sections separated by whitespace or soft full-width tint bands (synthesia's blue careers band, jasper's dark leadership card). Gradient auras used sparingly as hero backdrops (gitbook, jasper highlight marks).
- **Dark tech** (betterstack, height-app, liveblocks, modal, scenery, spellbook, lithic, oxide-computer): near-black canvas, single saturated accent (modal green, scenery yellow, liveblocks purple), photography as bright rectangles floating on dark. Culture is voice-driven: blunt manifesto bullets ("No Zoom calls", "Async-first" — betterstack), engineer-to-engineer tone. Investors and stats render as bordered cards/tables. Playful accents allowed: polaroid scatter (betterstack), rainbow squiggle (height-app), brand squiggles on quote cards (spellbook).
- **Colorful / playful** (clay-com, equals, getrepeat): loud full-bleed color fields (equals' yellow hero, getrepeat's chartreuse band, clay-com's color value-cards), oversized display type — getrepeat sets the H1 in huge uppercase with inline avatar chips; equals uses monospace editorial type with pixel-art motifs and sticker name tags. Culture proof is raw and specific: Slack screenshots (getrepeat), retreat photo walls (clay-com), interview-process steps spelled out 1-5 (clay-com).
- **Editorial serif** (kindsight, steep, and spellbook's headlines): serif display with italic emphasis ("Our *Purpose*.", "A recognized *leader*."), deep brand color fields (kindsight maroon → cream → dark green), pull-quote cards in contrasting tints (steep's dark card with yellow serif quote), line illustrations instead of photos (kindsight). Story told through a bento of narrative cards + founder quotes (steep) rather than one long column.

## Anti-patterns

- **Shipping a non-page under /company.** oxide-computer's company URL resolves to a bare events table — no mission, no team, no hiring hook. Functional, but it does none of a company page's persuasion work; every other site treats the page as a recruiting/trust asset.
- **"About Us" as the headline.** 19 of 20 avoid it in the H1. The corpus consistently spends the H1 on the mission.
- **Stock photography.** Zero instances. Weaker-budget sites (kindsight) use line illustrations rather than fake photos; equals uses deliberately lo-fi webcam-style headshots with stickers.
- **A flat, unbroken mega-grid of headshots.** synthesia's 30+ identical B&W tiles is the corpus's most fatiguing section; gitbook and lithic solve the same "we're real people" goal by interleaving text/stat cards into the photo mosaic, clay-com by grouping per team.
- **Company timelines.** No site uses a founding-date timeline/milestone strip; the story is always prose or stats instead.
- **One-word values.** No corpus site lists bare adjectives ("Integrity. Innovation."); every values section explains each item, and the weakest-feeling version is still a sentence per value.
- **Careers section with zero evidence of hiring.** lithic's "Come change the game" band with no roles is the thinnest careers treatment in the corpus; contrast betterstack's full role list with team stats.
- **Ending on press clippings.** News/press sections always sit mid-page (amra, jasper, lithic, height-app); the final section is always a CTA.

## Checklist

1. Write the H1 as a mission/belief statement containing zero instances of "About Us"; optional small eyebrow label ("Our mission" / "Our story") above it.
2. Add a 2-5 paragraph first-person founding story or manifesto; sign it ("— Team X") if the tone allows.
3. Include real photography: one group shot or a candid collage/strip for culture; B&W or desaturated treatment if photos vary in quality. Never stock; use line illustration if no photos exist.
4. Add a 3-4 stat band (founded, funding, customers, team size) with oversized numerals and one-word labels.
5. Build a values section: 4-8 named values, each with 1-3 sentences, in a structured layout (numbered grid, accordion, or color cards) — never bare adjectives.
6. Show people: leadership grid with roles + LinkedIn for trust-oriented audiences, or full-team grid with socials for culture-oriented ones; group by department once past ~15 people.
7. Add social proof: investor logo row (upgrade with named angels + titles), or alumni logos ("Teammates from...") if pre-traction; press/news cards mid-page if available.
8. Put real open roles inline (title + location/remote tag + link), not just a "See careers" button.
9. Close with a CTA: a high-contrast "Join us" band and/or a product hand-off ("Get started with X") — never end on press or footer filler.
10. Match the section rhythm to the site style: whitespace separation for light minimal, bordered cards on near-black for dark tech, full-bleed color fields for playful, tinted narrative bento for editorial serif.

## Best references

- **betterstack** — the complete dark-tech culture page: blunt manifesto, polaroid scatter, values accordion, inline role list with stats, angels named with titles.
- **clay-com** — richest playful careers-forward page: department grouping, benefits grid, retreat galleries, explicit 5-step interview process.
- **liveblocks** — cleanest story-first structure: hero photo, founding narrative with sidebar stats, colored-dot values, team grid, nothing extra.
- **outseta** — radical-transparency benchmark: operating principles and salary policy as the values section; deeply personal team bios.
- **kindsight** — editorial serif treatment: acronym values, color-field pacing (maroon/cream/green), leadership grid + awards for a trust-heavy audience.
- **modal** — best trust utilities on dark: office map cards, contact/media/brand-kit row, investor grid with angel names.
- **usetola** — minimal page that earns credibility without traction: mission letter plus an alumni product-icon marquee instead of investor wallpaper.
- **equals** — boldest editorial voice: yellow manifesto letter signed by the team, sticker-tag headshot grid; proof a company page can be 4 sections.
