# Big Type / Brutalist Editorial Style Playbook
> Coverage: 30 sites from corpus (analyzed 30)

## Essence
Typography is the interface: headlines are set at poster-to-viewport scale — 60px to 126px and beyond — and everything else (nav, body, buttons) is deliberately small so scale contrast does the persuading (tiblo, wise, fonts-ninja, wowpage). Pages are built as stacked full-bleed color bands rather than cards on a shared background; a new section is announced by a background-color change, not by a container (eclipse, cntrl, save-day, getrepeat). Surfaces are aggressively flat — no drop shadows, no gradients, no glassmorphism — with depth expressed through polarity flips (light band → dark band), hairline 1px rules, and surface-contrast steps (uber, mux, wired, mezmo). Color is rationed: one or two saturated accents (often an acid chartreuse, electric blue, or searing orange) do all conversion work against a black/white/cream monochrome base (wise, thepatchsystem, film-ai, useparallel). The mood ranges from Swiss-print severity (wired, bmw-m, maneken) to zine-like playfulness with stickers and doodles (figura-digital, save-day, fonts-ninja), but the skeleton — huge type, flat bands, rationed accent — is constant.

## Palette formula
- **Canvas** is rarely pure white: warm cream/greige (#FAF2E7 arcadia, #E3E2DA mux, #FAF6EF useparallel, #F4F3F0 fonts-ninja, #D9D6D0 getrepeat), cool gray (#dae0e4 wowpage, #d3d3d3 thepatchsystem, #F0F0F0 film-ai), sage tint (#e8ebe6 wise), or near-black (#0D0D0D supertape, #131313 theverge, #151515 figura-digital, #162415 mezmo, #000000 bmw-m). True #FFFFFF appears mostly on the strictest systems (uber, wired, jitter, axelar-network).
- **Ink** is near-black at extreme contrast (#111–#232323) or its inverse; some brands substitute a colored ink for black entirely — forest green #273F2B (gotomorro), blue #1f00fe as body-text color (thepatchsystem), forest green #2b4a3b (wowpage).
- **Primary/accent** is one loud conversion color used exclusively for CTAs and highlights, never diluted into chrome: acid chartreuse/lime is the style's signature hue (#E0F64C getrepeat, #E3FF3C useparallel, #9fe870 wise, #DBFE08 figura-digital, #E4F97B mezmo, #79EA54 gotomorro, #0EFE89 arcadia). Alternatives: electric blue (#213CFF headroom, #0251B8 maneken), orange-red (#FF3700 film-ai, #ff622d thepatchsystem, #F4501A tiblo), or plain black as the only action color (uber, wired, fonts-ninja).
- **Secondary accents** live only inside illustrations, chips, or one-off tiles — never in UI chrome (axelar-network, mux, wise, wowpage).
- Example palettes:
  - Cream #FAF2E7 / forest #014036 / neon green #0EFE89 / aqua #B7EEED (arcadia)
  - Greige #E3E2DA / charcoal #232323 / saturated print-ink accents #FB4E0D, #F553B8, #2160FD (mux)
  - Near-black forest #162415 / chartreuse #E4F97B, two-color terminal system (mezmo)
  - Sage #e8ebe6 / olive-black #0e0f0c / lime #9fe870 (wise)
  - Gray #d3d3d3 / two blues #072af8 + #1f00fe / orange CTA #ff622d (thepatchsystem)
  - Pure white / pure black / default-blue links only — zero chromatic accent (uber, wired)

## Typography formula
- **Display**: heavy condensed grotesques dominate — Druk/Founders Grotesk Condensed feel at black weight, all-caps, tight-to-negative tracking (arcadia, axelar-network, thepatchsystem, save-day, mezmo, gotomorro); or neo-grotesques (Neue Haas / Helvetica Now / Aeonik) at bold-to-black (maneken, jitter, tiblo, figura-digital, fonts-ninja, wowpage, eclipse). Weight extremes are the voice: 900-weight heroes (wise), 700-vs-300 pairing (bmw-m).
- **Counter-current 1 — scale over weight**: some set book/regular weight at enormous size and let scale alone carry hierarchy (cord, supertape, wired at 400).
- **Counter-current 2 — editorial serif display**: high-contrast serif headlines on otherwise brutalist bones (fiberplane Tiempos-feel, wired's proprietary condensed didone).
- **Body**: same grotesque family at small sizes, regular weight, often muted gray — body copy stays tiny and quiet to amplify contrast (cntrl, tiblo, fiberplane, save-day).
- **Monospace as third voice**: uppercase mono for nav, eyebrows, labels, buttons, timestamps — the engineering-spec register (mux, mezmo, supertape, axelar-network, theverge).
- **Type tricks that recur**: one word italicized or color-flipped inside a headline (arcadia, axelar-network); a word in an inverted black highlight box (fiberplane, cord); glyph swaps and pixel-glitch logotypes (film-ai, cntrl); inline avatars/icons interrupting headline text (getrepeat, fonts-ninja, fiberplane); staggered stair-stepped line breaks (film-ai, useparallel, fiberplane); ghost/watermark mega-type behind content (figura-digital, studio-design, headroom, wowpage); wordmark set so large it clips the viewport (eclipse, supertape, getrepeat, useparallel).

## Layout & shape
- **Full-bleed color-band stacking** is the master pattern: sections are edge-to-edge slabs whose background change IS the section break (eclipse, cntrl, save-day, buzzsprout, pass-app, wowpage, headroom). Some round the band edges 16–40px+ where slabs meet, like stacked paper (getrepeat, pass-app, jitter, maneken).
- **Heroes are type-first**: viewport-filling headline, tiny subline, one CTA; imagery is a product screenshot below or bleeding off one edge (tiblo, cntrl, fonts-ninja, supertape). Left-anchored heroes are common (arcadia, figura-digital, eclipse, fonts-ninja); centered stacks appear on the friendlier end (jitter, gotomorro, save-day).
- **Visible structure**: 1px hairline rules, ruled logo grids, table-cell footers, dividers instead of whitespace (mux's exposed grid, wired's hairline story rows, supertape's rule-banded pages, arcadia's logo tables, thepatchsystem's numbered spec cards).
- **Radius is bimodal**: either near-zero/sharp (bmw-m 0px, wired 0px, tiblo ~4px, thepatchsystem 2–4px, mezmo 2–6px) or full pill/large-radius (jitter, cord, mux, wise 24px, gotomorro) — rarely the timid middle. Several systems deliberately mix both dialects (cntrl's sharp hero CTA vs closing pill; wowpage's sharp data tiles vs rounded chips; fonts-ninja's pills vs small-radius cards).
- **Shadows are essentially banned**: flat fills plus hairline borders; the few exceptions are deliberate — hard offset black shadows as a neo-brutalist wink (useparallel), soft shadows only under floating product screenshots (fiberplane, buzzsprout), a single shadowed hero form card (uber).
- **Whitespace**: generous vertical padding inside bands, one idea per band (save-day, studio-design, buzzsprout); or the opposite — zero-gap slabs butting together with rhythm from color alone (eclipse).

## Do
- Set headlines far larger than feels safe — 50–90% of viewport width — in a heavy condensed or black-weight grotesque with tight tracking (jitter, film-ai, tiblo, wise, fonts-ninja).
- Build the page as stacked full-bleed color bands and change the band color to mark a new section instead of adding containers (eclipse, cntrl, save-day, getrepeat, buzzsprout).
- Ration one accent color for CTAs and highlights only; keep all other chrome black/white/neutral (wise lime, thepatchsystem orange, useparallel chartreuse, studio-design gradient pill, axelar-network azure).
- Keep body copy tiny, quiet, and often gray — the display/body scale gap is the identity (cntrl, tiblo, fiberplane, bmw-m's 700-vs-300 weight gap).
- Express structure with 1px hairline rules, ruled grids, and table-cell layouts rather than boxed cards (mux, wired, supertape, arcadia, getrepeat).
- Flip polarity for depth: full black or dark-ink bands with inverted text replace shadows as the elevation system (uber, jitter's #18082c customers page, wise's dark promo cards, studio-design).
- Use monospace uppercase for eyebrows, nav, labels, timestamps, and button microcopy when the product is technical (mux, mezmo, supertape, axelar-network, theverge).
- Deploy one signature type trick per site — highlight-box word, glyph swap, inline avatar, clipped viewport wordmark, ghost watermark — and repeat it consistently (fiberplane, film-ai, getrepeat, eclipse, studio-design).
- Let product screenshots, flat diagrams, doodles, or duotone photos be the only imagery; each site picks exactly one imagery language (cntrl screenshots-only, axelar-network diagrams, save-day doodles, thepatchsystem blueprint sketches, bmw-m photography).
- Commit to the corner-radius dialect you choose — all-sharp or all-pill — and if mixing, make the contrast obviously deliberate (bmw-m rectangles + perfect circles; wowpage sharp tiles + rounded chips).

## Don't
- Don't add drop shadows, gradients, glows, or glassmorphism — nearly every profile forbids them explicitly; flatness is the style's load-bearing wall (eclipse, mux, jitter, wise, figura-digital, tiblo).
- Don't set display type light or at "safe" h1 sizes; airy or timid headlines collapse the whole system (jitter, axelar-network, wise, save-day) — unless the site's stated dialect is scale-over-weight (cord, supertape).
- Don't introduce a second saturated accent into UI chrome; extra hues live only inside illustrations or one-off tiles (axelar-network, wise, mux, wowpage, studio-design).
- Don't let the accent color wash across backgrounds or body text — chartreuse/mint/orange are for CTAs, bands, and small doses (figura-digital, theverge, thepatchsystem, useparallel).
- Don't box content in shadowed floating cards on a shared background; separate with band color changes and hairline rules instead (eclipse, save-day, mux, wired).
- Don't use stock photography or generic 3D as decoration; imagery must be product truth, diagrams, or intentional illustration (axelar-network, headroom, cntrl, figura-digital).
- Don't set body copy in the display face or at display sizes — paragraphs stay small in the workhorse cut (arcadia, theverge, wired).
- Don't soften with mid-range radii, pastel-muted accents, or polite centered heroes when the system calls for hard edges and left-anchored asymmetry (mux, eclipse, film-ai, tiblo).
- Don't loosen headline tracking or letter-space the display face; tight-to-negative tracking is a near-universal constant (jitter, uber, fonts-ninja, wired).
- Don't crowd a band with multiple ideas — one headline, one short paragraph, one visual per band (save-day, studio-design, buzzsprout).

## Canonical examples
- **tiblo** — purest scale-tension demo: viewport headline with flat color rectangles collaged between the words, everything else tiny.
- **eclipse** — the color-band grammar at its strictest: zero-gap slabs, per-page palette swaps, viewport-clipped wordmark hero and footer.
- **mux** — brutalist spec-sheet: exposed 1px grid, machined uppercase display + mono labels, saturated accent bands on greige.
- **jitter** — friendly pole of the style: absurdly large tight black headlines, pill-everything, three rationed brand notes.
- **wise** — big-type at fintech polish: 900-weight 126px heroes, single lime action color, surface contrast instead of shadows.
- **wired** — the editorial-print ancestor: giant weight-400 display serif, absolute black/white, zero radius, hairline-only elevation.
- **mezmo** — dark terminal dialect: two-color chartreuse-on-forest system, condensed caps display, 1px blueprint linework.
- **thepatchsystem** — blueprint brutalism: blue body text, numbered spec cards, dashed grid canvas, orange reserved for CTAs.
- **film-ai** — two-color poster system with glyph-swapped display type and stair-stepped headline layout.
- **cord** — scale-over-weight variant: book-weight poster type dressed in outline-pill and black-bar headline devices.
- **fonts-ninja** — type as content and interface: monochrome mega-headlines with sticker interruptions, specimen-card grid.
- **useparallel** — neo-brutalist SaaS blend: cream/black band rhythm, chartreuse CTA slabs, hard offset shadows, scribble stickers.
